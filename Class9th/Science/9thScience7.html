1 An athlete completes one round of a circular track of diameter 200 m in 40 s. What will be the distance covered and the displacement at the end of 2 minutes 20 s?

Given:

Diameter of circular track = 200 m

Radius, r = 100 m

Time for one round = 40 s

Total time = 2 min 20 s = 140 s


Solution:

Number of rounds completed in 140 s = 140 / 40 = 3.5 rounds

Distance covered = 3.5 × circumference = 3.5 × 2πr = 3.5 × 2 × 3.14 × 100 = 2198 m

Displacement after 3.5 rounds = Diameter of the track = 200 m (since the athlete is at the opposite end of the starting point)


Answer: Distance covered = 2198 m, Displacement = 200 m

2 Joseph jogs from one end A to the other end B of a straight 300 m road in 2 minutes 30 seconds and then turns around and jogs 100 m back to point C in another 1 minute. What are Joseph's average speeds and velocities in jogging (a) from A to B and (b) from A to C?

(a) From A to B:

Distance = 300 m

Time = 2 min 30 s = 150 s

Average speed = Distance/Time = 300/150 = 2 m/s

Displacement = 300 m (from A to B)

Average velocity = Displacement/Time = 300/150 = 2 m/s


(b) From A to C:

Total distance = 300 m + 100 m = 400 m

Total time = 150 s + 60 s = 210 s

Average speed = Total distance/Total time = 400/210 ≈ 1.90 m/s

Displacement from A to C = 300 m - 100 m = 200 m

Average velocity = Displacement/Time = 200/210 ≈ 0.95 m/s


Answer: (a) Average speed = 2 m/s, Average velocity = 2 m/s; (b) Average speed ≈ 1.90 m/s, Average velocity ≈ 0.95 m/s

3 Abdul, while driving to school, computes the average speed for his trip to be 20 km/h. On his return trip along the same route, there is less traffic and the average speed is 30 km/h. What is the average speed for Abdul's trip?

Let the distance to school be D km.

Time taken for forward journey = D/20 hours

Time taken for return journey = D/30 hours

Total distance = 2D km

Total time = D/20 + D/30 = (3D + 2D)/60 = 5D/60 = D/12 hours

Average speed = Total distance/Total time = 2D/(D/12) = 24 km/h


Answer: Average speed for the entire trip = 24 km/h

4 A motorboat starting from rest on a lake accelerates in a straight line at a constant rate of 3.0 m/s² for 8.0 s. How far does the boat travel during this time?

Given:

Initial velocity, u = 0 m/s

Acceleration, a = 3.0 m/s²

Time, t = 8.0 s


Solution:

Using the equation of motion: s = ut + ½at²

s = 0 × 8 + ½ × 3 × (8)²

s = 0 + ½ × 3 × 64 = 96 m


Answer: The boat travels 96 m during this time.

5 A driver of a car travelling at 52 km/h applies the brakes and accelerates uniformly in the opposite direction. The car stops in 5 s. Another driver going at 3 km/h in another car applies his brakes slowly and stops in 10 s. On the same graph paper, plot the speed versus time graphs for the two cars. Which of the two cars travelled farther after the brakes were applied?

Given:

First car: Initial velocity = 52 km/h = 14.44 m/s, Time to stop = 5 s

Second car: Initial velocity = 3 km/h = 0.83 m/s, Time to stop = 10 s


Solution:

Distance travelled = Area under speed-time graph

For first car: Distance = ½ × base × height = ½ × 5 × 14.44 = 36.1 m

For second car: Distance = ½ × base × height = ½ × 10 × 0.83 = 4.15 m


Answer: The first car travelled farther (36.1 m) after the brakes were applied.

7 A ball is gently dropped from a height of 20 m. If its velocity increases uniformly at the rate of 10 m/s², with what velocity will it strike the ground? After what time will it strike the ground?

Given:

Initial velocity, u = 0 m/s

Height, s = 20 m

Acceleration, a = 10 m/s²


Solution:

Using v² = u² + 2as

v² = 0 + 2 × 10 × 20 = 400

v = √400 = 20 m/s


Using v = u + at

20 = 0 + 10 × t

t = 20/10 = 2 s


Answer: The ball will strike the ground with a velocity of 20 m/s after 2 seconds.

9 State which of the following situations are possible and give an example for each of these:
(a) an object with a constant acceleration but with zero velocity
(b) an object moving with an acceleration but with uniform speed
(c) an object moving in a certain direction with an acceleration in the perpendicular direction

(a) Possible. Example: When a ball is thrown vertically upward, at the highest point its velocity is zero but acceleration due to gravity is constant (9.8 m/s² downward).

(b) Possible. Example: Uniform circular motion - an object moving in a circle with constant speed has acceleration (centripetal acceleration) directed toward the center.

(c) Possible. Example: In projectile motion, the horizontal velocity is constant while acceleration due to gravity acts vertically downward (perpendicular to horizontal motion).


Answer: All three situations are possible with the examples given above.

10 An artificial satellite is moving in a circular orbit of radius 42250 km. Calculate its speed if it takes 24 hours to revolve around the earth.

Given:

Radius of orbit, r = 42250 km

Time for one revolution, t = 24 hours


Solution:

Distance covered in one revolution = Circumference of orbit = 2πr

Distance = 2 × 3.14 × 42250 ≈ 265,330 km

Speed = Distance/Time = 265,330 km / 24 h ≈ 11,055 km/h


In m/s: 11,055 km/h = 11,055 × 1000 / 3600 ≈ 3070.8 m/s


Answer: The speed of the satellite is approximately 11,055 km/h or 3070.8 m/s.
