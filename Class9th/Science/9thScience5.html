1 Make a comparison and write down ways in which plant cells are different from animal cells.

Plant Cells:

• Have a rigid cell wall made of cellulose outside the plasma membrane

• Contain plastids (chloroplasts, chromoplasts, leucoplasts)

• Have a large central vacuole that occupies most of the cell volume

• Generally have a fixed, rectangular shape

• Store food as starch


Animal Cells:

• Do not have a cell wall

• Do not contain plastids

• Have small, temporary vacuoles

• Usually have irregular shapes

• Store food as glycogen

2 How is a prokaryotic cell different from a eukaryotic cell?

Prokaryotic Cells:

• Generally small in size (1-10 μm)

• Nuclear region is poorly defined (nucleoid) without nuclear membrane

• Have a single chromosome

• Membrane-bound cell organelles are absent

• Examples: Bacteria, blue-green algae


Eukaryotic Cells:

• Generally large in size (5-100 μm)

• Nuclear region is well-defined with a nuclear membrane

• Have more than one chromosome

• Membrane-bound cell organelles are present

• Examples: Plant cells, animal cells, fungi

3 What would happen if the plasma membrane ruptures or breaks down?

If the plasma membrane ruptures or breaks down:

• The cell would lose its ability to control the movement of substances in and out

• Important cellular components could leak out

• Harmful substances could enter freely

• The cell would not be able to maintain its internal environment

• The cell would ultimately die as it loses its integrity and cannot perform essential functions

4 What would happen to the life of a cell if there was no Golgi apparatus?

Without the Golgi apparatus:

• The cell would not be able to store, modify, and package products

• Transportation of materials within and outside the cell would be disrupted

• Formation of lysosomes would not occur

• Complex sugars would not be synthesized from simple sugars

• The cell's waste disposal system would be impaired

• Ultimately, the cell would not survive as many essential functions would cease

5 Which organelle is known as the powerhouse of the cell? Why?

Mitochondria are known as the powerhouse of the cell.


Reason:

Mitochondria are responsible for producing energy in the form of ATP (Adenosine Triphosphate) molecules through cellular respiration. This energy is required for various chemical activities needed for life. The body uses energy stored in ATP for making new chemical compounds and for mechanical work.

6 Where do the lipids and proteins constituting the cell membrane get synthesised?

Lipids: Synthesized in the Smooth Endoplasmic Reticulum (SER)

Proteins: Synthesized in the Rough Endoplasmic Reticulum (RER) by ribosomes attached to its surface


Both lipids and proteins are then transported to the Golgi apparatus for modification and packaging before being incorporated into the cell membrane through a process called membrane biogenesis.

7 How does an Amoeba obtain its food?

Amoeba obtains its food through a process called endocytosis:

1. Amoeba extends its pseudopodia (false feet) to surround the food particle

2. The food particle gets enclosed in a food vacuole

3. Digestive enzymes are secreted into the food vacuole

4. The food is digested and absorbed

5. The undigested material is expelled from the cell


This process is possible due to the flexibility of the cell membrane in Amoeba.

8 What is osmosis?

Osmosis is the movement of water molecules from a region of higher water concentration to a region of lower water concentration through a selectively permeable membrane.


It is a special case of diffusion where only water molecules move across the membrane. Osmosis plays a crucial role in:

• Absorption of water by plant roots

• Movement of water in and out of cells

• Maintaining turgidity in plant cells

9 Carry out the following osmosis experiment:

Experiment Setup:

Take four peeled potato halves and scoop each one out to make potato cups. One of these potato cups should be made from a boiled potato. Put each potato cup in a trough containing water. Now:

(a) Keep cup A empty

(b) Put one teaspoon sugar in cup B

(c) Put one teaspoon salt in cup C

(d) Put one teaspoon sugar in the boiled potato cup D

Keep these for two hours. Then observe the four potato cups.


Observations and Explanations:

(i) Water gathers in the hollowed portion of B and C because:

    The sugar/salt solution inside the cups is hypertonic compared to the water outside. Water moves from the outside (higher water concentration) to the inside (lower water concentration) through osmosis.


(ii) Potato A is necessary as a control experiment to show that without any solute, water does not gather in the hollowed portion.


(iii) Water does not gather in the hollowed portions of A and D because:

    • In A, there is no concentration gradient as both sides have water

    • In D, the boiled potato cells are dead, so the selectively permeable membrane is destroyed and osmosis cannot occur

10 Which type of cell division is required for growth and repair of body and which type is involved in formation of gametes?

For growth and repair of body: Mitosis

• Produces two identical daughter cells with the same number of chromosomes as the mother cell

• Helps in growth and repair of tissues


For formation of gametes: Meiosis

• Produces four new cells with half the number of chromosomes than the mother cell

• Ensures genetic variation in offspring

• Occurs in reproductive organs to form gametes (sperm and egg cells)
