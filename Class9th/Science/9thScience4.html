2 What are the limitations of J.J. Thomson's model of the atom?
Limitations of Thomson's model:
1. It could not explain the results of Rutherford's alpha-particle scattering experiment.
2. It did not explain how the positive charge holds the negatively charged electrons in the atom.
3. It failed to explain the stability of atoms.
4. It could not explain the arrangement of electrons in different energy levels.
3 What are the limitations of Rutherford's model of the atom?
Limitations of Rutherford's model:
1. According to classical electromagnetic theory, an accelerating charged particle (like an electron revolving around the nucleus) should continuously radiate energy and spiral into the nucleus, making the atom unstable. But atoms are stable.
2. It could not explain the discrete line spectrum of atoms.
3. It did not explain how electrons are arranged in different energy levels.
4 Describe Bohr's model of the atom.
Bohr's model of the atom:
1. Electrons revolve around the nucleus in specific, fixed circular paths called orbits or shells.
2. These orbits are associated with definite energy levels and are called energy shells (K, L, M, N, etc.).
3. While revolving in these discrete orbits, electrons do not radiate energy.
4. Energy is emitted or absorbed only when an electron jumps from one energy level to another.

6 Summarise the rules for writing of distribution of electrons in various shells for the first eighteen elements.
Rules for electron distribution (Bohr-Bury scheme):
1. The maximum number of electrons in a shell is given by 2n², where n is the orbit number.
2. The maximum number of electrons in the outermost shell is 8.
3. Electrons are not accommodated in a given shell unless the inner shells are filled.
4. Shells are filled in a step-wise manner.
7 Define valency by taking examples of silicon and oxygen.
Valency is the combining capacity of an atom, determined by the number of electrons it can lose, gain, or share to achieve a stable electron configuration (octet).

Silicon (atomic number 14): Electron configuration - K=2, L=8, M=4
It can either lose 4 electrons or gain 4 electrons to complete its octet. Thus, its valency is 4.

Oxygen (atomic number 8): Electron configuration - K=2, L=6
It can gain 2 electrons to complete its octet. Thus, its valency is 2.
8 Explain with examples (i) Atomic number, (ii) Mass number, (iii) Isotopes and (iv) Isobars. Give any two uses of isotopes.
(i) Atomic Number (Z): The number of protons in the nucleus of an atom. Example: Carbon has atomic number 6 because it has 6 protons.

(ii) Mass Number (A): The sum of the number of protons and neutrons in the nucleus of an atom. Example: Carbon-12 has mass number 12 (6 protons + 6 neutrons).

(iii) Isotopes: Atoms of the same element having the same atomic number but different mass numbers. Example: Carbon-12 and Carbon-14 are isotopes of carbon.

(iv) Isobars: Atoms of different elements having the same mass number but different atomic numbers. Example: Calcium-40 and Argon-40 are isobars.

Two uses of isotopes:
1. An isotope of uranium is used as a fuel in nuclear reactors.
2. An isotope of cobalt is used in the treatment of cancer.
9 Na⁺ has completely filled K and L shells. Explain.
Sodium atom (Na) has atomic number 11 with electron distribution: K=2, L=8, M=1.
When it loses one electron from its outermost M shell to form Na⁺, the electron distribution becomes: K=2, L=8.
This gives Na⁺ the stable electron configuration of neon (completely filled K and L shells).

11 The average atomic mass of a sample of an element X is 16.2 u. What are the percentages of isotopes 168X and 188X in the sample?
Let the percentage of 168X be x%, then percentage of 188X is (100-x)%.
Average atomic mass = (16 × x + 18 × (100-x)) / 100 = 16.2
16x + 1800 - 18x = 1620
-2x = 1620 - 1800 = -180
x = 90
So, percentage of 168X = 90%
Percentage of 188X = 10%
12 If Z = 3, what would be the valency of the element? Also, name the element.
Atomic number Z = 3 corresponds to Lithium (Li).
Electron configuration: K=2, L=1
It has 1 electron in its outermost shell, so it can lose 1 electron to achieve stable configuration.
Therefore, valency = 1
13 Composition of the nuclei of two atomic species X and Y are given as under:
X: Protons = 6, Neutrons = 6
Y: Protons = 6, Neutrons = 8
Give the mass numbers of X and Y. What is the relation between the two species?
Mass number of X = Protons + Neutrons = 6 + 6 = 12
Mass number of Y = Protons + Neutrons = 6 + 8 = 14
Both have same atomic number (6) but different mass numbers.
Therefore, X and Y are isotopes of carbon (Carbon-12 and Carbon-14).
