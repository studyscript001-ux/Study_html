Improvement in Food Resources

1 Explain any one method of crop production which ensures high yield.

One method of crop production that ensures high yield is inter-cropping. This involves growing two or more crops simultaneously on the same field in a definite pattern. For example, growing soyabean and maize in alternating rows. The crops are selected such that their nutrient requirements are different, which ensures maximum utilization of the nutrients supplied. This method also prevents pests and diseases from spreading to all plants belonging to one crop in a field, resulting in better returns for both crops.

2 Why are manure and fertilizers used in fields?

Manure and fertilizers are used in fields to:

  • Supply essential nutrients to plants for their growth and development
  • Enrich the soil with nutrients and organic matter
  • Increase soil fertility
  • Improve soil structure and water-holding capacity
  • Ensure good vegetative growth and healthy plants
  • Achieve higher crop yields

While fertilizers provide concentrated nutrients, manure adds organic matter to the soil and improves its physical properties.

3 What are the advantages of inter-cropping and crop rotation?

Advantages of Inter-cropping:

  • Ensures maximum utilization of nutrients supplied
  • Prevents pests and diseases from spreading to all plants of one crop
  • Provides better returns as multiple crops are grown together
  • Reduces risk of crop failure

Advantages of Crop Rotation:

  • Allows growing of multiple crops in a year with good harvests
  • Helps in maintaining and improving soil fertility
  • Reduces soil erosion
  • Helps in controlling weeds, pests, and diseases
  • Reduces dependence on chemical fertilizers
4 What is genetic manipulation? How is it useful in agricultural practices?

Genetic manipulation is the process of introducing specific genes into crop plants to give them desired characteristics. This can be done through:

  • Hybridization (crossing between genetically dissimilar plants)
  • Direct introduction of genes to create genetically modified crops

Usefulness in agricultural practices:

  • Developing crops with higher yields
  • Creating varieties resistant to diseases, pests, and environmental stresses
  • Improving quality of crop products (better baking quality in wheat, protein quality in pulses)
  • Developing crops with shorter maturity duration
  • Creating varieties with wider adaptability to different climatic conditions
  • Developing crops with desirable agronomic characteristics
5 How do storage grain losses occur?

Storage grain losses occur due to various factors:

Biotic factors:

  • Insects that infest stored grains
  • Rodents that consume and contaminate grains
  • Fungi, mites, and bacteria that cause degradation

Abiotic factors:

  • Inappropriate moisture content in stored grains
  • Unsuitable temperatures in storage areas
  • Humidity fluctuations

These factors lead to:

  • Degradation in quality
  • Loss in weight
  • Poor germinability
  • Discoloration of produce
  • Reduced marketability
6 How do good animal husbandry practices benefit farmers?

Good animal husbandry practices benefit farmers in several ways:

  • Increase production of milk, eggs, and meat, leading to higher income
  • Improve the health and productivity of livestock
  • Provide better disease control, reducing mortality and treatment costs
  • Enhance the quality of animal products, increasing their market value
  • Provide sustainable livelihood through mixed farming systems
  • Generate additional income through by-products like animal manure for crops
  • Create employment opportunities in rural areas
  • Help in efficient utilization of agricultural by-products as animal feed
7 What are the benefits of cattle farming?

Cattle farming provides multiple benefits:

For Milk Production:

  • Provides milk and milk products which are rich sources of nutrition
  • Can be a steady source of income through dairy farming

For Draught Labor:

  • Provides animal power for agricultural work like tilling, irrigation, and carting
  • Reduces dependence on mechanical equipment and fuel

Other Benefits:

  • Provides manure which enriches soil fertility
  • Creates employment opportunities in rural areas
  • Utilizes agricultural by-products as cattle feed
  • Can be integrated with crop farming for sustainable agriculture
8 For increasing production, what is common in poultry, fisheries and bee-keeping?

Common practices for increasing production in poultry, fisheries, and bee-keeping include:

  • Use of improved varieties/breeds: Selecting and breeding high-yielding varieties (e.g., cross-breeding in poultry, composite fish culture, Italian bees in apiculture)
  • Proper feeding/nutrition: Providing balanced and nutrient-rich feed (e.g., protein-rich feed for broilers, appropriate food combinations in fish culture, adequate pasturage for bees)
  • Disease control and prevention: Implementing vaccination programs, regular cleaning, and sanitation measures
  • Scientific management practices: Proper housing, temperature control, and hygienic conditions
  • Efficient utilization of resources: Using agricultural by-products as feed, integrated farming approaches
  • Regular monitoring and care: Close observation of health, growth, and environmental conditions
9 How do you differentiate between capture fishing, mariculture and aquaculture?
Type Definition Examples
Capture Fishing Obtaining fish from natural resources like oceans, rivers, and lakes without raising them Catching pomphret, mackerel, tuna from the sea using fishing nets
Mariculture Culture of marine fish and other organisms in seawater, either in the open sea or in enclosures Farming of mullets, bhetki, prawns, mussels, oysters, and seaweed in seawater
Aquaculture Farming of aquatic organisms in freshwater or brackish water environments Composite fish culture in ponds, rice-fish culture, freshwater prawn farming

Key Differences:

  • Capture fishing involves harvesting naturally available fish, while mariculture and aquaculture involve rearing and farming fish.
  • Mariculture is specifically done in marine (saltwater) environments, while aquaculture can be in both freshwater and brackish water.
  • Aquaculture is a broader term that includes mariculture as a subset when done in marine waters.
