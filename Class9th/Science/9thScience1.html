Chapter : 1
Matter in Our Surroundings

1 Convert the following temperatures to the celsius scale.
(a) 293 K
(b) 470 K
Answer:
To convert Kelvin to Celsius, we use the formula: °C = K - 273

(a) 293 K = 293 - 273 = 20°C
(b) 470 K = 470 - 273 = 197°C
2 Convert the following temperatures to the kelvin scale.
(a) 25° C
(b) 373° C
Answer:
To convert Celsius to Kelvin, we use the formula: K = °C + 273

(a) 25°C = 25 + 273 = 298 K
(b) 373°C = 373 + 273 = 646 K
3 Give reason for the following observations.
(a) Naphthalene balls disappear with time without leaving any solid.
(b) We can get the smell of perfume sitting several metres away.
Answer:
(a) Naphthalene balls undergo sublimation, i.e., they change directly from solid to gas without passing through the liquid state. Hence, they disappear without leaving any solid.

(b) The particles of perfume are in a gaseous state and they diffuse into the air. Due to the high speed of particles and large space between them, gases diffuse very fast and can travel several metres.
4 Arrange the following substances in increasing order of forces of attraction between the particles— water, sugar, oxygen.
Answer:
The forces of attraction are maximum in solids, intermediate in liquids, and minimum in gases.

Sugar is a solid, water is a liquid, and oxygen is a gas.

So, increasing order: oxygen (gas) < water (liquid) < sugar (solid)
5 What is the physical state of water at—
(a) 25° C
(b) 0° C
(c) 100° C ?
Answer:
(a) At 25°C, water is in the liquid state.

(b) At 0°C, water is at its melting point. Both solid (ice) and liquid water can coexist at this temperature.

(c) At 100°C, water is at its boiling point. Both liquid water and gaseous water (steam) can coexist at this temperature.
6 Give two reasons to justify—
(a) water at room temperature is a liquid.
(b) an iron almirah is a solid at room temperature.
Answer:
(a) Water at room temperature is a liquid because:
1. It has a fixed volume but no fixed shape. It takes the shape of the container.
2. The particles of water are not rigid and can move around, which is a characteristic of liquids.

(b) An iron almirah is a solid because:
1. It has a definite shape and distinct boundaries.
2. It is rigid and cannot be compressed, which are properties of solids.
7 Why is ice at 273 K more effective in cooling than water at the same temperature?
Answer:
Ice at 273 K (0°C) is more effective in cooling than water at the same temperature because ice absorbs the latent heat of fusion from the surroundings to convert itself into water at 0°C. This absorption of heat energy from the surroundings makes the surroundings colder. Water at 0°C does not absorb such extra heat energy (latent heat) from the surroundings.
8 What produces more severe burns, boiling water or steam?
Answer:
Steam produces more severe burns than boiling water because steam has more energy than boiling water at the same temperature (100°C). Steam at 100°C contains extra heat energy in the form of latent heat of vaporization (which is 2260 J/g). When steam comes in contact with the skin, it condenses and releases this extra heat, causing more severe burns.
