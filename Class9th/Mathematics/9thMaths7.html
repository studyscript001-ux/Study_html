Question 1: In quadrilateral ACBD, AC = AD and AB bisects ∠A (see Fig. 7.16). Show that ΔABC ≅ ΔABD. What can you say about BC and BD?

Solution:

Given: AC = AD and AB bisects ∠A

In ΔABC and ΔABD:

AC = AD (Given)

∠CAB = ∠DAB (AB bisects ∠A)

AB = AB (Common side)

Therefore, ΔABC ≅ ΔABD (by SAS congruence rule)

Since the triangles are congruent, BC = BD (CPCT)

Answer: BC = BD

Hinglish Explanation: Diya gaya hai ki AC = AD aur AB, ∠A ko bisect karta hai. Dono triangles ABC aur ABD mein, AC = AD, ∠CAB = ∠DAB, aur AB common side hai. Isliye SAS congruence rule se ΔABC ≅ ΔABD. Toh corresponding sides BC aur BD equal hain.

Question 2: ABCD is a quadrilateral in which AD = BC and ∠DAB = ∠CBA (see Fig. 7.17). Prove that (i) ΔABD ≅ ΔBAC (ii) BD = AC (iii) ∠ABD = ∠BAC.

Solution:

Given: AD = BC and ∠DAB = ∠CBA

(i) In ΔABD and ΔBAC:

AD = BC (Given)

∠DAB = ∠CBA (Given)

AB = BA (Common side)

Therefore, ΔABD ≅ ΔBAC (by SAS congruence rule)

(ii) Since ΔABD ≅ ΔBAC, BD = AC (CPCT)

(iii) Since ΔABD ≅ ΔBAC, ∠ABD = ∠BAC (CPCT)

Answer: All three parts proved

Hinglish Explanation: Diya gaya hai ki AD = BC aur ∠DAB = ∠CBA. Dono triangles ABD aur BAC mein, AD = BC, ∠DAB = ∠CBA, aur AB common side hai. Isliye SAS rule se ΔABD ≅ ΔBAC. CPCT se BD = AC aur ∠ABD = ∠BAC.

Question 3: AD and BC are equal perpendiculars to a line segment AB (see Fig. 7.18). Show that CD bisects AB.

Solution:

Given: AD ⊥ AB, BC ⊥ AB, and AD = BC

In ΔAOD and ΔBOC:

∠DAO = ∠CBO = 90° (Both perpendicular to AB)

AD = BC (Given)

∠AOD = ∠BOC (Vertically opposite angles)

Therefore, ΔAOD ≅ ΔBOC (by AAS congruence rule)

Since the triangles are congruent, AO = OB (CPCT)

Hence, CD bisects AB at O

Answer: CD bisects AB

Hinglish Explanation: Diya gaya hai ki AD aur BC dono AB par perpendicular hain aur AD = BC. Triangles AOD aur BOC mein, ∠DAO = ∠CBO = 90°, AD = BC, aur ∠AOD = ∠BOC (vertically opposite). Isliye AAS rule se ΔAOD ≅ ΔBOC. CPCT se AO = OB, matlab CD, AB ko bisect karta hai.

Question 4: l and m are two parallel lines intersected by another pair of parallel lines p and q (see Fig. 7.19). Show that ΔABC ≅ ΔCDA.

Solution:

Given: l || m and p || q

In ΔABC and ΔCDA:

∠BAC = ∠DCA (Alternate interior angles, p || q)

∠BCA = ∠DAC (Alternate interior angles, l || m)

AC = CA (Common side)

Therefore, ΔABC ≅ ΔCDA (by ASA congruence rule)

Answer: ΔABC ≅ ΔCDA

Hinglish Explanation: Diya gaya hai ki l || m aur p || q. Triangles ABC aur CDA mein, ∠BAC = ∠DCA (alternate interior angles, p || q), ∠BCA = ∠DAC (alternate interior angles, l || m), aur AC common side hai. Isliye ASA rule se ΔABC ≅ ΔCDA.

Question 5: Line l is the bisector of an angle ∠A and B is any point on l. BP and BQ are perpendiculars from B to the arms of ∠A (see Fig. 7.20). Show that: (i) ΔAPB ≅ ΔAQB (ii) BP = BQ or B is equidistant from the arms of ∠A.

Solution:

Given: l is the bisector of ∠A, BP ⊥ AP, BQ ⊥ AQ

(i) In ΔAPB and ΔAQB:

∠PAB = ∠QAB (l bisects ∠A)

∠APB = ∠AQB = 90° (Both are perpendiculars)

AB = AB (Common side)

Therefore, ΔAPB ≅ ΔAQB (by AAS congruence rule)

(ii) Since ΔAPB ≅ ΔAQB, BP = BQ (CPCT)

Hence, B is equidistant from the arms of ∠A

Answer: Both parts proved

Hinglish Explanation: Diya gaya hai ki l, ∠A ka bisector hai, aur BP aur BQ perpendicular hain. Triangles APB aur AQB mein, ∠PAB = ∠QAB (l bisects ∠A), ∠APB = ∠AQB = 90°, aur AB common side hai. Isliye AAS rule se ΔAPB ≅ ΔAQB. CPCT se BP = BQ, matlab B, ∠A ki arms se equidistant hai.

Question 6: In Fig. 7.21, AC = AE, AB = AD and ∠BAD = ∠EAC. Show that BC = DE.

Solution:

Given: AC = AE, AB = AD, ∠BAD = ∠EAC

From ∠BAD = ∠EAC, we can write:

∠BAD + ∠DAC = ∠EAC + ∠DAC

So, ∠BAC = ∠DAE

In ΔABC and ΔADE:

AB = AD (Given)

AC = AE (Given)

∠BAC = ∠DAE (Proved above)

Therefore, ΔABC ≅ ΔADE (by SAS congruence rule)

Since the triangles are congruent, BC = DE (CPCT)

Answer: BC = DE

Hinglish Explanation: Diya gaya hai ki AC = AE, AB = AD, aur ∠BAD = ∠EAC. ∠BAD = ∠EAC se hum likh sakte hain ∠BAD + ∠DAC = ∠EAC + ∠DAC, yaani ∠BAC = ∠DAE. Triangles ABC aur ADE mein, AB = AD, AC = AE, aur ∠BAC = ∠DAE. Isliye SAS rule se ΔABC ≅ ΔADE. CPCT se BC = DE.

Question 7: AB is a line segment and P is its mid-point. D and E are points on the same side of AB such that ∠BAD = ∠ABE and ∠EPA = ∠DPB (see Fig. 7.22). Show that (i) ΔDAP ≅ ΔEBP (ii) AD = BE

Solution:

Given: P is mid-point of AB, ∠BAD = ∠ABE, ∠EPA = ∠DPB

From ∠EPA = ∠DPB, we can write:

∠EPA + ∠DPE = ∠DPB + ∠DPE

So, ∠APD = ∠BPE

(i) In ΔDAP and ΔEBP:

AP = BP (P is mid-point of AB)

∠DAP = ∠EBP (Given: ∠BAD = ∠ABE)

∠APD = ∠BPE (Proved above)

Therefore, ΔDAP ≅ ΔEBP (by ASA congruence rule)

(ii) Since ΔDAP ≅ ΔEBP, AD = BE (CPCT)

Answer: Both parts proved

Hinglish Explanation: Diya gaya hai ki P, AB ka mid-point hai, ∠BAD = ∠ABE, aur ∠EPA = ∠DPB. ∠EPA = ∠DPB se hum likh sakte hain ∠EPA + ∠DPE = ∠DPB + ∠DPE, yaani ∠APD = ∠BPE. Triangles DAP aur EBP mein, AP = BP, ∠DAP = ∠EBP, aur ∠APD = ∠BPE. Isliye ASA rule se ΔDAP ≅ ΔEBP. CPCT se AD = BE.

Question 8: In right triangle ABC, right angled at C, M is the mid-point of hypotenuse AB. C is joined to M and produced to a point D such that DM = CM. Point D is joined to point B (see Fig. 7.23). Show that: (i) ΔAMC ≅ ΔBMD (ii) ∠DBC is a right angle. (iii) ΔDBC ≅ ΔACB (iv) CM = 1/2 AB

Solution:

Given: ∠C = 90°, M is mid-point of AB, DM = CM

(i) In ΔAMC and ΔBMD:

AM = BM (M is mid-point of AB)

∠AMC = ∠BMD (Vertically opposite angles)

CM = DM (Given)

Therefore, ΔAMC ≅ ΔBMD (by SAS congruence rule)

(ii) Since ΔAMC ≅ ΔBMD, ∠ACM = ∠BDM (CPCT)

But these are alternate interior angles for lines AC and BD with transversal CD

So, AC || BD

Now, AC ⊥ BC (Given: ∠C = 90°)

Since AC || BD and AC ⊥ BC, therefore BD ⊥ BC

So, ∠DBC = 90°

(iii) In ΔDBC and ΔACB:

BC = CB (Common side)

∠DBC = ∠ACB = 90°

DB = AC (Since ΔAMC ≅ ΔBMD, so AC = BD by CPCT)

Therefore, ΔDBC ≅ ΔACB (by SAS congruence rule)

(iv) Since ΔDBC ≅ ΔACB, DC = AB (CPCT)

But DC = 2CM (Since DM = CM)

So, 2CM = AB, or CM = 1/2 AB

Answer: All four parts proved

Hinglish Explanation: Diya gaya hai ki ∠C = 90°, M, AB ka mid-point hai, aur DM = CM. (i) Triangles AMC aur BMD mein, AM = BM, ∠AMC = ∠BMD (vertically opposite), aur CM = DM. Isliye SAS rule se ΔAMC ≅ ΔBMD. (ii) CPCT se ∠ACM = ∠BDM, jo alternate interior angles hain, isliye AC || BD. AC ⊥ BC, toh BD ⊥ BC, yaani ∠DBC = 90°. (iii) Triangles DBC aur ACB mein, BC common, ∠DBC = ∠ACB = 90°, aur DB = AC (CPCT). Isliye SAS rule se ΔDBC ≅ ΔACB. (iv) CPCT se DC = AB, par DC = 2CM, isliye CM = 1/2 AB.

Exercise 7.2

Question 1: In an isosceles triangle ABC, with AB = AC, the bisectors of ∠B and ∠C intersect each other at O. Join A to O. Show that: (i) OB = OC (ii) AO bisects ∠A

Solution:

Given: AB = AC, OB and OC are bisectors of ∠B and ∠C respectively

(i) Since AB = AC, ∠B = ∠C (Angles opposite to equal sides)

So, 1/2 ∠B = 1/2 ∠C, i.e., ∠OBC = ∠OCB

Therefore, OB = OC (Sides opposite to equal angles)

(ii) In ΔAOB and ΔAOC:

AB = AC (Given)

OB = OC (Proved above)

AO = AO (Common side)

Therefore, ΔAOB ≅ ΔAOC (by SSS congruence rule)

So, ∠BAO = ∠CAO (CPCT)

Hence, AO bisects ∠A

Answer: Both parts proved

Hinglish Explanation: Diya gaya hai ki AB = AC, aur OB aur OC, ∠B aur ∠C ke bisectors hain. (i) AB = AC se ∠B = ∠C, isliye 1/2 ∠B = 1/2 ∠C, yaani ∠OBC = ∠OCB. Toh OB = OC. (ii) Triangles AOB aur AOC mein, AB = AC, OB = OC, aur AO common. Isliye SSS rule se ΔAOB ≅ ΔAOC. CPCT se ∠BAO = ∠CAO, matlab AO, ∠A ko bisect karta hai.

Question 2: In ΔABC, AD is the perpendicular bisector of BC (see Fig. 7.30). Show that ΔABC is an isosceles triangle in which AB = AC.

Solution:

Given: AD is perpendicular bisector of BC, so BD = DC and ∠ADB = ∠ADC = 90°

In ΔADB and ΔADC:

BD = DC (AD bisects BC)

∠ADB = ∠ADC = 90° (AD ⊥ BC)

AD = AD (Common side)

Therefore, ΔADB ≅ ΔADC (by SAS congruence rule)

So, AB = AC (CPCT)

Hence, ΔABC is isosceles with AB = AC

Answer: ΔABC is isosceles with AB = AC

Hinglish Explanation: Diya gaya hai ki AD, BC ka perpendicular bisector hai, matlab BD = DC aur ∠ADB = ∠ADC = 90°. Triangles ADB aur ADC mein, BD = DC, ∠ADB = ∠ADC = 90°, aur AD common. Isliye SAS rule se ΔADB ≅ ΔADC. CPCT se AB = AC, matlab ΔABC isosceles hai.

Question 6: ΔABC is an isosceles triangle in which AB = AC. Side BA is produced to D such that AD = AB (see Fig. 7.34). Show that ∠BCD is a right angle.

Solution:

Given: AB = AC, AD = AB

So, AB = AC = AD

In ΔABC: AB = AC, so ∠ABC = ∠ACB (Angles opposite to equal sides)

Let ∠ABC = ∠ACB = x

In ΔADC: AD = AC, so ∠ADC = ∠ACD (Angles opposite to equal sides)

Let ∠ADC = ∠ACD = y

In ΔBCD, sum of angles = 180°

So, ∠BCD + ∠BDC + ∠CBD = 180°

∠BCD = ∠BCA + ∠ACD = x + y

∠BDC = y

∠CBD = x

So, (x + y) + y + x = 180°

2x + 2y = 180°

x + y = 90°

Therefore, ∠BCD = x + y = 90°

Answer: ∠BCD is a right angle

Hinglish Explanation: Diya gaya hai ki AB = AC aur AD = AB, isliye AB = AC = AD. ΔABC mein AB = AC, isliye ∠ABC = ∠ACB = x. ΔADC mein AD = AC, isliye ∠ADC = ∠ACD = y. ΔBCD ke angles ka sum 180° hai: ∠BCD + ∠BDC + ∠CBD = (x+y) + y + x = 2x+2y = 180°, isliye x+y = 90°. Toh ∠BCD = x+y = 90°.

Question 7: ABC is a right angled triangle in which ∠A = 90° and AB = AC. Find ∠B and ∠C.

Solution:

Given: ∠A = 90°, AB = AC

Since AB = AC, ∠B = ∠C (Angles opposite to equal sides)

Sum of angles in ΔABC = 180°

So, ∠A + ∠B + ∠C = 180°

90° + ∠B + ∠B = 180° (Since ∠B = ∠C)

2∠B = 90°

∠B = 45°

Therefore, ∠C = 45°

Answer: ∠B = 45°, ∠C = 45°

Hinglish Explanation: Diya gaya hai ki ∠A = 90° aur AB = AC. AB = AC se ∠B = ∠C. Triangle ke angles ka sum 180° hai: 90° + ∠B + ∠C = 180°. Par ∠B = ∠C, isliye 90° + 2∠B = 180°, yaani 2∠B = 90°, ∠B = 45°. Toh ∠C = 45°.

Question 8: Show that the angles of an equilateral triangle are 60° each.

Solution:

Let ΔABC be an equilateral triangle with AB = BC = CA

Since AB = BC, ∠C = ∠A (Angles opposite to equal sides)

Since BC = CA, ∠A = ∠B (Angles opposite to equal sides)

So, ∠A = ∠B = ∠C

Sum of angles in ΔABC = 180°

So, ∠A + ∠B + ∠C = 180°

3∠A = 180° (Since all angles are equal)

∠A = 60°

Therefore, ∠A = ∠B = ∠C = 60°

Answer: All angles are 60°

Hinglish Explanation: Equilateral triangle mein sabhi sides equal hain: AB = BC = CA. AB = BC se ∠C = ∠A. BC = CA se ∠A = ∠B. Toh sabhi angles equal hain: ∠A = ∠B = ∠C. Triangle ke angles ka sum 180° hai, isliye 3∠A = 180°, ∠A = 60°. Toh sabhi angles 60° ke hain.

Question 1: ΔABC and ΔDBC are two isosceles triangles on the same base BC and vertices A and D are on the same side of BC (see Fig. 7.39). If AD is extended to intersect BC at P, show that (i) ΔABD ≅ ΔACD (ii) ΔABP ≅ ΔACP (iii) AP bisects ∠A as well as ∠D. (iv) AP is the perpendicular bisector of BC.

Solution:

Given: ΔABC and ΔDBC are isosceles on same base BC

So, AB = AC and DB = DC

(i) In ΔABD and ΔACD:

AB = AC (Given)

BD = CD (Given)

AD = AD (Common side)

Therefore, ΔABD ≅ ΔACD (by SSS congruence rule)

(ii) In ΔABP and ΔACP:

AB = AC (Given)

∠BAP = ∠CAP (Since ΔABD ≅ ΔACD, so ∠BAD = ∠CAD)

AP = AP (Common side)

Therefore, ΔABP ≅ ΔACP (by SAS congruence rule)

(iii) From (i), ∠BAD = ∠CAD, so AP bisects ∠A

From (i), ∠BDA = ∠CDA, so AP bisects ∠D

(iv) From (ii), BP = CP (CPCT)

And ∠APB = ∠APC (CPCT)

But ∠APB + ∠APC = 180° (Linear pair)

So, 2∠APB = 180°, ∠APB = 90°

Therefore, AP ⊥ BC and BP = CP, so AP is perpendicular bisector of BC

Answer: All four parts proved

Hinglish Explanation: Diya gaya hai ki ΔABC aur ΔDBC isosceles hain same base BC par. (i) Triangles ABD aur ACD mein, AB = AC, BD = CD, aur AD common. Isliye SSS rule se ΔABD ≅ ΔACD. (ii) Triangles ABP aur ACP mein, AB = AC, ∠BAP = ∠CAP (part i se), aur AP common. Isliye SAS rule se ΔABP ≅ ΔACP. (iii) Part i se ∠BAD = ∠CAD, isliye AP, ∠A ko bisect karta hai. Part i se ∠BDA = ∠CDA, isliye AP, ∠D ko bisect karta hai. (iv) Part ii se BP = CP aur ∠APB = ∠APC. Linear pair se ∠APB + ∠APC = 180°, isliye ∠APB = 90°. Toh AP ⊥ BC aur BP = CP, matlab AP, BC ka perpendicular bisector hai.

AB = PQ (Given)
