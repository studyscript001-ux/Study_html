Question 2: The triangular side walls of a flyover have been used for advertisements. The sides of the walls are 122 m, 22 m and 120 m. The advertisements yield an earning of ₹ 5000 per m² per year. A company hired one of its walls for 3 months. How much rent did it pay?

Solution:

Sides of triangle: a = 122 m, b = 22 m, c = 120 m

Semi-perimeter s =

122 + 22 + 120 2
=
264 2
= 132 m

s - a = 132 - 122 = 10 m

s - b = 132 - 22 = 110 m

s - c = 132 - 120 = 12 m

Area = √[s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)] = √[132 × 10 × 110 × 12]

= √[132 × 10 × 110 × 12] = √[1742400] = 1320 m²

Annual earning = Area × Rate = 1320 × 5000 = ₹66,00,000

Rent for 3 months =

3 12
× 66,00,000 =
1 4
× 66,00,000 = ₹16,50,000

Answer: ₹16,50,000

Hinglish Explanation: Pehle triangle ka area Heron's formula se nikalte hain. Sides 122 m, 22 m, 120 m hain. Semi-perimeter 132 m hai. Area = 1320 m². Annual earning = 1320 × 5000 = ₹66,00,000. 3 months ka rent = (3/12) × 66,00,000 = ₹16,50,000.

Question 3: There is a slide in a park. One of its side walls has been painted in some colour with a message "KEEP THE PARK GREEN AND CLEAN". If the sides of the wall are 15 m, 11 m and 6 m, find the area painted in colour.

Solution:

Sides of triangle: a = 15 m, b = 11 m, c = 6 m

Semi-perimeter s =

15 + 11 + 6 2
=
32 2
= 16 m

s - a = 16 - 15 = 1 m

s - b = 16 - 11 = 5 m

s - c = 16 - 6 = 10 m

Area = √[s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)] = √[16 × 1 × 5 × 10]

= √[800] = 20√2 m²

Answer: 20√2 m²

Hinglish Explanation: Triangle ki sides 15 m, 11 m, 6 m hain. Semi-perimeter 16 m hai. Heron's formula lagane par area = √[16 × 1 × 5 × 10] = √800 = 20√2 m². Yeh painted area hai.

Question 4: Find the area of a triangle two sides of which are 18cm and 10cm and the perimeter is 42cm.

Solution:

Given: a = 18 cm, b = 10 cm, Perimeter = 42 cm

Third side c = Perimeter - (a + b) = 42 - (18 + 10) = 42 - 28 = 14 cm

Semi-perimeter s =

42 2
= 21 cm

s - a = 21 - 18 = 3 cm

s - b = 21 - 10 = 11 cm

s - c = 21 - 14 = 7 cm

Area = √[s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)] = √[21 × 3 × 11 × 7]

= √[4851] = 21√11 cm²

Answer: 21√11 cm²

Hinglish Explanation: Do sides 18 cm aur 10 cm hain, perimeter 42 cm hai. Toh third side = 42 - (18+10) = 14 cm. Semi-perimeter 21 cm. Heron's formula lagane par area = √[21 × 3 × 11 × 7] = √4851 = 21√11 cm².

Question 5: Sides of a triangle are in the ratio of 12:17:25 and its perimeter is 540cm. Find its area.

Solution:

Let the sides be 12x, 17x, and 25x

Perimeter = 12x + 17x + 25x = 54x

Given perimeter = 540 cm

So, 54x = 540 ⇒ x = 10

Sides: a = 12 × 10 = 120 cm, b = 17 × 10 = 170 cm, c = 25 × 10 = 250 cm

Semi-perimeter s =

540 2
= 270 cm

s - a = 270 - 120 = 150 cm

s - b = 270 - 170 = 100 cm

s - c = 270 - 250 = 20 cm

Area = √[s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)] = √[270 × 150 × 100 × 20]

= √[81000000] = 9000 cm²

Answer: 9000 cm²

Hinglish Explanation: Sides ratio 12:17:25 hai, perimeter 540 cm. Pehle x find karte hain: 12x+17x+25x=54x=540, so x=10. Sides: 120 cm, 170 cm, 250 cm. Semi-perimeter 270 cm. Heron's formula lagane par area = √[270×150×100×20] = √81000000 = 9000 cm².

Question 6: An isosceles triangle has perimeter 30 cm and each of the equal sides is 12 cm. Find the area of the triangle.

Solution:

Given: Equal sides = 12 cm each, Perimeter = 30 cm

Third side = Perimeter - (12 + 12) = 30 - 24 = 6 cm

Sides: a = 12 cm, b = 12 cm, c = 6 cm

Semi-perimeter s =

30 2
= 15 cm

s - a = 15 - 12 = 3 cm

s - b = 15 - 12 = 3 cm

s - c = 15 - 6 = 9 cm

Area = √[s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)] = √[15 × 3 × 3 × 9]

= √[1215] = 9√15 cm²

Answer: 9√15 cm²

Hinglish Explanation: Isosceles triangle mein do equal sides 12 cm hain, perimeter 30 cm hai. Toh third side = 30 - (12+12) = 6 cm. Semi-perimeter 15 cm. Heron's formula lagane par area = √[15×3×3×9] = √1215 = 9√15 cm².
