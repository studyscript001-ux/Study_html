Question 1: If the diagonals of a parallelogram are equal, then show that it is a rectangle.

Solution:

Let ABCD be a parallelogram with diagonals AC and BD equal.

In ΔABC and ΔDCB:

AB = DC (Opposite sides of parallelogram)

BC = BC (Common side)

AC = DB (Given)

Therefore, ΔABC ≅ ΔDCB (SSS congruence rule)

So, ∠ABC = ∠DCB (CPCT)

But ∠ABC + ∠DCB = 180° (Adjacent angles of parallelogram)

So, 2∠ABC = 180° ⇒ ∠ABC = 90°

Similarly, we can show that all angles are 90°.

Therefore, ABCD is a rectangle.

Hinglish Explanation: Maan lete hain ABCD ek parallelogram hai jismein diagonals AC aur BD equal hain. ΔABC aur ΔDCB mein, AB = DC, BC common hai, aur AC = DB. Isliye ΔABC ≅ ΔDCB. Isse ∠ABC = ∠DCB. Par ∠ABC + ∠DCB = 180°, toh 2∠ABC = 180° ⇒ ∠ABC = 90°. Aise hi sab angles 90° dikhaye ja sakte hain. Isliye ABCD ek rectangle hai.

Question 2: Show that the diagonals of a square are equal and bisect each other at right angles.

Solution:

Let ABCD be a square with diagonals AC and BD intersecting at O.

In ΔABC and ΔDCB:

AB = DC (Sides of square)

BC = BC (Common side)

∠ABC = ∠DCB = 90° (Angles of square)

Therefore, ΔABC ≅ ΔDCB (SAS congruence rule)

So, AC = BD (CPCT)

Hence, diagonals are equal.

Now, in ΔAOB and ΔCOD:

∠OAB = ∠OCD (Alternate angles)

∠OBA = ∠ODC (Alternate angles)

AB = CD (Sides of square)

Therefore, ΔAOB ≅ ΔCOD (AAS congruence rule)

So, AO = OC and BO = OD (CPCT)

Hence, diagonals bisect each other.

In ΔAOB and ΔCOB:

AO = OC (Proved above)

OB = OB (Common side)

AB = CB (Sides of square)

Therefore, ΔAOB ≅ ΔCOB (SSS congruence rule)

So, ∠AOB = ∠COB (CPCT)

But ∠AOB + ∠COB = 180° (Linear pair)

So, 2∠AOB = 180° ⇒ ∠AOB = 90°

Hence, diagonals bisect each other at right angles.

Hinglish Explanation: ABCD ek square hai jiske diagonals AC aur BD O par intersect karte hain. ΔABC aur ΔDCB congruent hain, isliye AC = BD. ΔAOB aur ΔCOD congruent hain, isliye AO = OC aur BO = OD. ΔAOB aur ΔCOB congruent hain, isliye ∠AOB = ∠COB. Par ∠AOB + ∠COB = 180°, toh 2∠AOB = 180° ⇒ ∠AOB = 90°. Isliye diagonals equal hain, ek doosre ko bisect karte hain, aur right angles par bisect karte hain.

Question 3: Diagonal AC of a parallelogram ABCD bisects ∠A (see Fig. 8.11). Show that

(i) it bisects ∠C also

Given: ABCD is a parallelogram and AC bisects ∠A.

So, ∠DAC = ∠BAC ...(1)

Now, AB || DC and AC is a transversal

So, ∠BAC = ∠DCA ...(2) (Alternate angles)

Also, AD || BC and AC is a transversal

So, ∠DAC = ∠BCA ...(3) (Alternate angles)

From (1), (2), and (3):

∠DCA = ∠BCA

Hence, AC bisects ∠C.

Hinglish Explanation: ABCD ek parallelogram hai jismein AC, ∠A ko bisect karta hai. AB || DC aur AC transversal hai, isliye ∠BAC = ∠DCA. AD || BC aur AC transversal hai, isliye ∠DAC = ∠BCA. Par ∠DAC = ∠BAC, isliye ∠DCA = ∠BCA. Yani AC, ∠C ko bhi bisect karta hai.

(ii) ABCD is a rhombus

From above, we have ∠DAC = ∠DCA

In ΔADC, ∠DAC = ∠DCA

So, AD = DC (Sides opposite to equal angles)

But in parallelogram ABCD, AB = DC and AD = BC

So, AB = BC = CD = DA

Hence, ABCD is a rhombus.

Hinglish Explanation: ΔADC mein ∠DAC = ∠DCA, isliye AD = DC. Par parallelogram mein AB = DC aur AD = BC, isliye AB = BC = CD = DA. Yani sab sides equal hain, isliye ABCD ek rhombus hai.

Question 4: ABCD is a rectangle in which diagonal AC bisects ∠A as well as ∠C. Show that:

(i) ABCD is a square

Given: ABCD is a rectangle and AC bisects ∠A and ∠C.

Since AC bisects ∠A, ∠DAC = ∠BAC

But ∠DAC + ∠BAC = ∠A = 90° (Angle of rectangle)

So, 2∠DAC = 90° ⇒ ∠DAC = 45°

Similarly, since AC bisects ∠C, ∠DCA = ∠BCA = 45°

In ΔADC, ∠DAC = 45° and ∠DCA = 45°

So, ∠ADC = 180° - (45° + 45°) = 90°

Similarly, all angles are 90°.

Now, in ΔADC and ΔABC:

AD = BC (Opposite sides of rectangle)

AC = AC (Common side)

∠DAC = ∠BCA = 45°

So, ΔADC ≅ ΔABC (ASA congruence rule)

Therefore, AB = AD (CPCT)

So, adjacent sides are equal.

Hence, ABCD is a square.

Hinglish Explanation: ABCD ek rectangle hai jismein AC, ∠A aur ∠C dono ko bisect karta hai. ∠A = 90°, isliye ∠DAC = 45°. Similarly ∠DCA = 45°. ΔADC mein ∠ADC = 90°. ΔADC aur ΔABC congruent hain, isliye AB = AD. Yani adjacent sides equal hain. Isliye ABCD ek square hai.

(ii) diagonal BD bisects ∠B as well as ∠D

Since ABCD is a square (proved above), and in a square, diagonals bisect the angles.

Therefore, BD bisects ∠B and ∠D.

Alternatively, we can prove similarly as we did for AC.

Hinglish Explanation: ABCD square hai, aur square mein diagonals angles ko bisect karte hain. Isliye BD, ∠B aur ∠D ko bisect karega.

(ii) AP = CQ

Since ΔAPD ≅ ΔCQB (proved above)

So, AP = CQ (CPCT)

Hinglish Explanation: ΔAPD ≅ ΔCQB, isliye AP = CQ.

In ΔAQB and ΔCPD:

AB = CD (Opposite sides of parallelogram)

∠ABQ = ∠CDP (Alternate angles as AB || CD)

Hinglish Explanation: ΔAQB aur ΔCPD mein, AB = CD, ∠ABQ = ∠CDP (alternate angles), aur BQ = DP. Isliye ΔAQB ≅ ΔCPD.

(iv) AQ = CP

Since ΔAQB ≅ ΔCPD (proved above)

So, AQ = CP (CPCT)

Hinglish Explanation: ΔAQB ≅ ΔCPD, isliye AQ = CP.

We have AP = CQ (from ii) and AQ = CP (from iv)

In quadrilateral APCQ, opposite sides are equal.

Hinglish Explanation: APCQ mein AP = CQ aur AQ = CP, yani opposite sides equal hain. Isliye APCQ ek parallelogram hai.

Question 7: ABCD is a trapezium in which AB || CD and AD = BC (see Fig. 8.14). Show that

(i) ∠A = ∠B

Extend AB and draw a line through C parallel to DA intersecting AB produced at E.

So, AD || EC (By construction)

Now, AECD is a parallelogram (Opposite sides are parallel)

So, AD = EC (Opposite sides of parallelogram)

But AD = BC (Given)

So, BC = EC

In ΔBCE, BC = EC

So, ∠CBE = ∠CEB (Angles opposite to equal sides)

But ∠CEB = ∠A (Corresponding angles as AD || EC)

And ∠CBE = ∠B

Therefore, ∠A = ∠B

Hinglish Explanation: AB ko extend karte hain aur C se DA ke parallel ek line draw karte hain jo AB ko E par intersect karti hai. AECD ek parallelogram hai, isliye AD = EC. Par AD = BC, isliye BC = EC. ΔBCE mein BC = EC, isliye ∠CBE = ∠CEB. Par ∠CEB = ∠A (corresponding angles) aur ∠CBE = ∠B. Isliye ∠A = ∠B.

(ii) ∠C = ∠D

AB || CD (Given)

So, ∠A + ∠D = 180° and ∠B + ∠C = 180° (Co-interior angles)

But ∠A = ∠B (Proved above)

So, ∠A + ∠D = ∠B + ∠C

Therefore, ∠D = ∠C

Hinglish Explanation: AB || CD, isliye ∠A + ∠D = 180° aur ∠B + ∠C = 180°. Par ∠A = ∠B, isliye ∠A + ∠D = ∠B + ∠C ⇒ ∠D = ∠C.

(iii) ΔABC ≅ ΔBAD

In ΔABC and ΔBAD:

AB = BA (Common side)

BC = AD (Given)

∠ABC = ∠BAD (Proved in i)

Therefore, ΔABC ≅ ΔBAD (SAS congruence rule)

Hinglish Explanation: ΔABC aur ΔBAD mein, AB common hai, BC = AD, aur ∠ABC = ∠BAD. Isliye ΔABC ≅ ΔBAD.

(iv) diagonal AC = diagonal BD

Since ΔABC ≅ ΔBAD (proved above)

So, AC = BD (CPCT)

Hinglish Explanation: ΔABC ≅ ΔBAD, isliye AC = BD.

Question 4: ABCD is a trapezium in which AB || DC, BD is a diagonal and E is the mid-point of AD. A line is drawn through E parallel to AB intersecting BC at F (see Fig. 8.21). Show that F is the mid-point of BC.

Solution:

Let EF intersect BD at G.

In ΔABD, E is the mid-point of AD and EG || AB (since EF || AB)

By converse of mid-point theorem, G is the mid-point of BD.

Now, in ΔBCD, G is the mid-point of BD and GF || DC (since AB || DC and EF || AB, so EF || DC)

By converse of mid-point theorem, F is the mid-point of BC.

Hinglish Explanation: EF, BD ko G par intersect karti hai. ΔABD mein E mid-point hai AD ka aur EG || AB, isliye G mid-point hai BD ka. ΔBCD mein G mid-point hai BD ka aur GF || DC, isliye F mid-point hai BC ka.
