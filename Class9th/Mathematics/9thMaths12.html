Question 1: A survey conducted by an organisation for the cause of illness and death among the women between the ages 15 - 44 (in years) worldwide, found the following figures (in %):

S.No. Causes Female fatality rate (%)
1. Reproductive health conditions 31.8
2. Neuropsychiatric conditions 25.4
3. Injuries 12.4
4. Cardiovascular conditions 4.3
5. Respiratory conditions 4.1
6. Other causes 22.0

(i) Represent the information given above graphically.

To represent this data graphically, we can use a bar graph.

Steps to draw the bar graph:

  1. Draw two perpendicular lines (x-axis and y-axis)
  2. On the x-axis, mark the causes of illness and death
  3. On the y-axis, mark the percentage values from 0 to 35
  4. Draw rectangular bars of equal width with heights corresponding to the percentages
  5. Label the graph appropriately

(ii) Which condition is the major cause of women's ill health and death worldwide?

From the data, we can see that:

Reproductive health conditions have the highest percentage at 31.8%

Answer: Reproductive health conditions

(iii) Try to find out, with the help of your teacher, any two factors which play a major role in the cause in (ii) above being the major cause.

Two factors that play a major role in reproductive health conditions being the major cause:

  1. Lack of access to proper healthcare facilities during pregnancy and childbirth
  2. Inadequate nutrition and healthcare for women, especially in developing countries
Hinglish Explanation: Is data ko bar graph mein dikhaya ja sakta hai. Reproductive health conditions (31.8%) women ki illness aur death ka sabse bada karan hai. Iske do mukhya karan hain: pregnancy aur childbirth ke dauran proper healthcare ki kami, aur developing countries mein women ke liye adequate nutrition aur healthcare ki kami.

Question 4: The length of 40 leaves of a plant are measured correct to one millimetre, and the obtained data is represented in the following table:

Length (in mm) Number of leaves
118 - 126 3
127 - 135 5
136 - 144 9
145 - 153 12
154 - 162 5
163 - 171 4
172 - 180 2

(i) Draw a histogram to represent the given data. [Hint: First make the class intervals continuous]

First, we need to make the class intervals continuous:

The gap between classes is 1 (e.g., 126 and 127)

To make them continuous, we subtract 0.5 from lower limits and add 0.5 to upper limits

Continuous class intervals:

Length (in mm) Number of leaves
117.5 - 126.5 3
126.5 - 135.5 5
135.5 - 144.5 9
144.5 - 153.5 12
153.5 - 162.5 5
162.5 - 171.5 4
171.5 - 180.5 2

Now, draw a histogram with these continuous class intervals on the x-axis and frequencies on the y-axis.

(ii) Is there any other suitable graphical representation for the same data?

Yes, we can also represent this data using a frequency polygon.

To draw a frequency polygon:

  1. Find the class marks (midpoints) of each class interval
  2. Plot points with class marks on x-axis and frequencies on y-axis
  3. Join these points with line segments
  4. Extend the line to the x-axis at both ends

(iii) Is it correct to conclude that the maximum number of leaves are 153 mm long? Why?

No, it is not correct to conclude that the maximum number of leaves are 153 mm long.

The class 145-153 has the highest frequency (12 leaves), but this means the leaves in this class have lengths between 145 mm and 153 mm.

We cannot say that all these 12 leaves are exactly 153 mm long.

Answer: No, because the class 145-153 represents leaves with lengths between 145 mm and 153 mm, not exactly 153 mm.

Hinglish Explanation: Pehle class intervals ko continuous banayein, phir histogram banayein. Is data ke liye frequency polygon bhi suitable hai. Yeh galat hai ki maximum leaves 153 mm lambi hain, kyunki class 145-153 mein 145 mm se 153 mm tak ki leaves hain, sab exactly 153 mm ki nahi hain.

900 - 1000 48

Age (in years) Number of children
1 - 2 5
2 - 3 3
3 - 5 6
5 - 7 12
7 - 10 9

Draw a histogram to represent the data above.

Since the class intervals are of varying widths, we need to adjust the heights of the rectangles.

Steps to draw the histogram:

  1. Find the minimum class size: 1 year (for classes 1-2 and 2-3)
  2. Adjust the frequencies for classes with different widths:
    • For class 3-5 (width = 2): Adjusted frequency =
      6 2

    • For class 5-7 (width = 2): Adjusted frequency =
      12 2
      × 1 = 6

      9 3

Number of letters Number of surnames
1 - 4 6
4 - 6 30
6 - 8 44

  2. Adjust the frequencies for classes with different widths:
    • For class 1-4 (width = 3): Adjusted frequency =
      6 3
      × 2 = 4
