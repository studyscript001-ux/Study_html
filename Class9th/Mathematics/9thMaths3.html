Exercise 3.1

Question 1: How will you describe the position of a table lamp on your study table to another person?

Solution:

To describe the position of a table lamp on a study table, we can use a coordinate system with two perpendicular reference lines:

1. Consider one edge of the table as the x-axis and the adjacent perpendicular edge as the y-axis.

2. Measure the perpendicular distance of the lamp from the y-axis (x-coordinate).

3. Measure the perpendicular distance of the lamp from the x-axis (y-coordinate).

4. The position can be described using these two coordinates (x, y).

For example, if the lamp is 30 cm from the left edge (y-axis) and 40 cm from the bottom edge (x-axis), its position would be (30, 40).

Hinglish Explanation: Table lamp ki position batane ke liye hum do perpendicular lines use kar sakte hain. Ek edge ko x-axis aur adjacent edge ko y-axis maan kar, lamp ki distance dono axes se measure karenge. Jaise agar lamp left edge se 30 cm aur bottom edge se 40 cm hai, toh position (30, 40) hogi.

Question 2: (Street Plan) A city has two main roads which cross each other at the centre of the city. These two roads are along the North-South direction and East-West direction. All the other streets of the city run parallel to these roads and are 200 m apart. There are 5 streets in each direction. Using 1cm = 200 m, draw a model of the city on your notebook. Represent the roads/streets by single lines.

(i) How many cross-streets can be referred to as (4, 3)?

According to the convention, a cross-street is referred to as (m, n) where:

▪️ m is the number of the street running in the North-South direction

▪️ n is the number of the street running in the East-West direction

The cross-street (4, 3) is formed by the intersection of:

▪️ 4th street in North-South direction

▪️ 3rd street in East-West direction

Since there's only one such intersection, there is exactly 1 cross-street that can be referred to as (4, 3).

Answer: 1

Hinglish Explanation: Cross-street (4, 3) ka matlab hai 4th street North-South direction mein aur 3rd street East-West direction mein. In dono streets ka intersection ek hi point par hoga, isliye exactly 1 cross-street (4, 3) kehlayega.

(ii) How many cross-streets can be referred to as (3, 4)?

The cross-street (3, 4) is formed by the intersection of:

▪️ 3rd street in North-South direction

▪️ 4th street in East-West direction

This is a different intersection from (4, 3).

Since there's only one such intersection, there is exactly 1 cross-street that can be referred to as (3, 4).

Answer: 1

Hinglish Explanation: Cross-street (3, 4) ka matlab hai 3rd street North-South direction mein aur 4th street East-West direction mein. Ye (4, 3) se alag intersection hai, lekin iska bhi exactly 1 cross-street hoga.

Exercise 3.2

Question 1: Write the answer of each of the following questions:

(i) What is the name of horizontal and the vertical lines drawn to determine the position of any point in the Cartesian plane?

The horizontal line is called the x-axis.

The vertical line is called the y-axis.

Answer: x-axis and y-axis

Hinglish Explanation: Cartesian plane mein horizontal line ko x-axis aur vertical line ko y-axis kehte hain.

(ii) What is the name of each part of the plane formed by these two lines?

The two axes divide the plane into four parts.

Each part is called a quadrant.

Answer: Quadrant

Hinglish Explanation: Dono axes milkar plane ko char parts mein divide karte hain, jinhe quadrant kehte hain.

(iii) Write the name of the point where these two lines intersect.

The point where the x-axis and y-axis intersect is called the origin.

Answer: Origin

Hinglish Explanation: x-axis aur y-axis jis point par intersect karte hain, use origin kehte hain.
