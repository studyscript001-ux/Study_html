Exercise 9.1

Question 1: Recall that two circles are congruent if they have the same radii. Prove that equal chords of congruent circles subtend equal angles at their centres.

Solution:

Let there be two congruent circles with centers O and O', and equal chords AB and CD in each circle respectively.

Since the circles are congruent, their radii are equal: OA = O'C and OB = O'D.

Given that AB = CD (equal chords).

In triangles AOB and C'O'D':

OA = O'C (radii of congruent circles)

OB = O'D (radii of congruent circles)

AB = CD (given)

Therefore, ΔAOB ≅ ΔC'O'D' (by SSS congruence rule)

Hence, ∠AOB = ∠C'O'D' (corresponding parts of congruent triangles)

Therefore, equal chords of congruent circles subtend equal angles at their centres.

Theorem Used: Equal chords of a circle subtend equal angles at the centre (Theorem 9.1).
Hinglish Explanation: Do congruent circles lete hain jinki radii same hai. Unme equal chords AB aur CD lete hain. Triangles AOB aur C'O'D' congruent hote hain (SSS rule se). Isliye ∠AOB = ∠C'O'D'. Yani equal chords congruent circles ke centres par equal angles subtend karte hain.

Question 2: Prove that if chords of congruent circles subtend equal angles at their centres, then the chords are equal.

Solution:

Let there be two congruent circles with centers O and O', and chords AB and CD in each circle respectively.

Given that ∠AOB = ∠C'O'D' (equal angles at centres)

Since the circles are congruent, their radii are equal: OA = O'C and OB = O'D.

In triangles AOB and C'O'D':

OA = O'C (radii of congruent circles)

OB = O'D (radii of congruent circles)

∠AOB = ∠C'O'D' (given)

Therefore, ΔAOB ≅ ΔC'O'D' (by SAS congruence rule)

Hence, AB = CD (corresponding parts of congruent triangles)

Therefore, if chords of congruent circles subtend equal angles at their centres, then the chords are equal.

Theorem Used: If the angles subtended by the chords of a circle at the centre are equal, then the chords are equal (Theorem 9.2).
Hinglish Explanation: Do congruent circles lete hain. Maan lete hain chords AB aur CD centres par equal angles subtend karte hain. Triangles AOB aur C'O'D' congruent hote hain (SAS rule se). Isliye AB = CD. Yani agar chords congruent circles ke centres par equal angles subtend karte hain, toh chords equal hote hain.

Exercise 9.2

Question 1: Two circles of radii 5 cm and 3 cm intersect at two points and the distance between their centres is 4 cm. Find the length of the common chord.

Solution:

Let O and O' be the centers of the circles with radii 5 cm and 3 cm respectively.

Let AB be the common chord, and M be the midpoint of AB.

Given: OO' = 4 cm, OA = 5 cm, O'A = 3 cm

OM ⊥ AB and O'M ⊥ AB (perpendicular from center to chord bisects the chord)

Let OM = x, then O'M = 4 - x

In right triangle OMA: AM² = OA² - OM² = 25 - x²

In right triangle O'MA: AM² = O'A² - O'M² = 9 - (4 - x)²

Equating both expressions for AM²:

25 - x² = 9 - (16 - 8x + x²)

25 - x² = 9 - 16 + 8x - x²

25 = -7 + 8x

8x = 32

x = 4

Then AM² = 25 - 16 = 9, so AM = 3 cm

Therefore, AB = 2 × AM = 2 × 3 = 6 cm

Answer: The length of the common chord is 6 cm.

Theorem Used: The perpendicular from the centre of a circle to a chord bisects the chord (Theorem 9.3).
Hinglish Explanation: Do circles hain jinki radii 5 cm aur 3 cm hain. Unke centres ke beech ki distance 4 cm hai. Common chord AB hai. Midpoint M par perpendicular draw karte hain. Pythagoras theorem use karke AM nikalte hain jo 3 cm aata hai. Toh poori chord AB = 2 × 3 = 6 cm.

Question 2: If two equal chords of a circle intersect within the circle, prove that the segments of one chord are equal to corresponding segments of the other chord.

Solution:

Let AB and CD be two equal chords of a circle with center O, intersecting at point E.

Draw perpendiculars OM and ON from O to AB and CD respectively.

Since AB = CD, and equal chords are equidistant from the center, OM = ON.

In right triangles OME and ONE:

OM = ON (proved above)

OE = OE (common)

∠OME = ∠ONE = 90°

Therefore, ΔOME ≅ ΔONE (by RHS congruence rule)

Hence, ME = NE (CPCT)

Now, since OM and ON are perpendiculars from center to chords, they bisect the chords:

AM = MB and CN = ND

Therefore, AE = AM - ME and CE = CN - NE

Since AM = CN (half of equal chords) and ME = NE, we get AE = CE

Similarly, BE = MB - ME and DE = ND - NE

Since MB = ND and ME = NE, we get BE = DE

Hence, the segments of one chord are equal to corresponding segments of the other chord.

Theorem Used: Equal chords of a circle are equidistant from the centre (Theorem 9.5).
Hinglish Explanation: Do equal chords AB aur CD circle mein intersect karte hain point E par. Centre se perpendiculars draw karte hain. Equal chords centre se equal distance par hote hain, isliye OM = ON. Triangles OME aur ONE congruent hote hain (RHS rule se). Isliye ME = NE. Chords bisected hote hain, isliye AE = CE aur BE = DE. Yani segments equal hote hain.

Question 3: If two equal chords of a circle intersect within the circle, prove that the line joining the point of intersection to the centre makes equal angles with the chords.

Solution:

Let AB and CD be two equal chords of a circle with center O, intersecting at point E.

Join OE.

Draw perpendiculars OM and ON from O to AB and CD respectively.

Since AB = CD, and equal chords are equidistant from the center, OM = ON.

In right triangles OME and ONE:

OM = ON (proved above)

OE = OE (common)

∠OME = ∠ONE = 90°

Therefore, ΔOME ≅ ΔONE (by RHS congruence rule)

Hence, ∠OEM = ∠OEN (CPCT)

But ∠OEM is the angle between OE and AB, and ∠OEN is the angle between OE and CD.

Therefore, the line joining the point of intersection to the centre makes equal angles with the chords.

Theorem Used: Equal chords of a circle are equidistant from the centre (Theorem 9.5).
Hinglish Explanation: Do equal chords AB aur CD circle mein intersect karte hain point E par. Centre O se E ko join karte hain. Centre se perpendiculars draw karte hain. Equal chords centre se equal distance par hote hain, isliye OM = ON. Triangles OME aur ONE congruent hote hain (RHS rule se). Isliye ∠OEM = ∠OEN. Yani line OE chords ke saath equal angles banata hai.

Question 4: If a line intersects two concentric circles (circles with the same centre) with centre O at A, B, C and D, prove that AB = CD (see Fig. 9.12).

Solution:

Let there be two concentric circles with center O.

A line intersects the circles at points A, B, C, D, where A and D are on the outer circle, and B and C are on the inner circle.

Draw perpendicular OM from O to the line.

Since OM is perpendicular to the line, it bisects both chords AD and BC.

Therefore, AM = MD and BM = MC

Now, AB = AM - BM and CD = MD - MC

But since AM = MD and BM = MC, we have AB = CD

Hence, AB = CD

Theorem Used: The perpendicular from the centre of a circle to a chord bisects the chord (Theorem 9.3).
Hinglish Explanation: Do concentric circles hain jinka centre O hai. Ek line inhe A, B, C, D par intersect karti hai. Centre se line par perpendicular draw karte hain. Yeh perpendicular chords AD aur BC ko bisect karta hai. Isliye AM = MD aur BM = MC. Toh AB = AM - BM = MD - MC = CD. Yani AB = CD.

Question 5: Three girls Reshma, Salma and Mandip are playing a game by standing on a circle of radius 5m drawn in a park. Reshma throws a ball to Salma, Salma to Mandip, Mandip to Reshma. If the distance between Reshma and Salma and between Salma and Mandip is 6m each, what is the distance between Reshma and Mandip?

Solution:

Let R, S, M be the positions of Reshma, Salma and Mandip on the circle with center O and radius 5m.

Given: RS = SM = 6m

Join OS and draw perpendicular from O to chord RS.

Let the perpendicular meet RS at N.

Then RN = NS = 3m (perpendicular from center bisects the chord)

In right triangle ONS: ON² = OS² - NS² = 25 - 9 = 16, so ON = 4m

Similarly, for chord SM, we get the same perpendicular distance from center.

Since RS = SM and both are chords of the same circle, the perpendicular from O will bisect both chords at the same point.

Therefore, R, S, M form an isosceles triangle with RS = SM = 6m.

The angle RSM is twice the angle at the center subtended by chord RM.

But we can find RM using the chord length formula:

For chord RS: 6 = 2 × 5 × sin(∠ROS/2) ⇒ sin(∠ROS/2) = 6/10 = 0.6

Similarly for chord SM.

The angle between RS and SM is 2 × ∠ROS/2 = ∠ROS

In triangle RSM, using cosine rule:

RM² = RS² + SM² - 2 × RS × SM × cos(∠RSM)

But ∠RSM = 180° - ∠ROM (cyclic quadrilateral property)

Alternatively, we can find the central angle between R and M:

Since R, S, M are equally spaced on the circle (as RS = SM), the arc RM is twice the arc RS.

Chord length RM = 2 × R × sin(∠ROM/2)

We need to find ∠ROM.

From chord RS: 6 = 2 × 5 × sin(∠ROS/2) ⇒ sin(∠ROS/2) = 0.6 ⇒ ∠ROS/2 ≈ 36.87° ⇒ ∠ROS ≈ 73.74°

Similarly, ∠SOM ≈ 73.74°

Therefore, ∠ROM = ∠ROS + ∠SOM ≈ 73.74° + 73.74° = 147.48°

Then RM = 2 × 5 × sin(147.48°/2) = 10 × sin(73.74°) ≈ 10 × 0.9563 = 9.563m

Answer: The distance between Reshma and Mandip is approximately 9.56m.

Theorem Used: The perpendicular from the centre of a circle to a chord bisects the chord (Theorem 9.3).
Hinglish Explanation: Teen ladkiyan 5m radius ke circle par khadi hain. Reshma se Salma ki distance 6m hai, Salma se Mandip ki bhi 6m hai. Centre se chords par perpendicular draw karte hain. Pythagoras theorem use karke perpendicular distance nikalte hain. Phir chord length formula use karke Reshma aur Mandip ke beech ki distance nikalte hain jo approximately 9.56m aati hai.

Question 6: A circular park of radius 20m is situated in a colony. Three boys Ankur, Syed and David are sitting at equal distance on its boundary each having a toy telephone in his hands to talk each other. Find the length of the string of each phone.

Solution:

The three boys are sitting at equal distances on the boundary of a circle of radius 20m.

This means they form an equilateral triangle inscribed in the circle.

For an equilateral triangle inscribed in a circle, the relationship between side length (a) and radius (R) is:

a = √3 × R

Given R = 20m

Therefore, a = √3 × 20 = 20√3 m

The length of the string of each phone is the side length of this equilateral triangle.

Answer: The length of the string of each phone is 20√3 m ≈ 34.64m.

Concept Used: For an equilateral triangle inscribed in a circle, side = √3 × radius.
Hinglish Explanation: Teen ladke circle ke boundary par equal distance par baithe hain, iska matlab woh equilateral triangle banate hain. Equilateral triangle ke liye side = √3 × radius. Radius 20m hai, toh side = 20√3 m ≈ 34.64m. Yani har phone ki string ki length 34.64m hai.

Question 3: In Fig. 9.24, ∠PQR = 100°, where P, Q and R are points on a circle with centre O. Find ∠OPR.

Solution:

Given: ∠PQR = 100°

∠PQR is the angle subtended by arc PR at point Q on the circle.

According to Theorem 9.7, the angle at the center is double the angle at the circumference.

So, ∠POR = 2 × ∠PQR = 2 × 100° = 200° (reflex angle)

The other angle at center O, i.e., the minor angle POR = 360° - 200° = 160°

Now, in triangle OPR:

OP = OR (radii)

So, triangle OPR is isosceles.

Therefore, ∠OPR = ∠ORP

Sum of angles in triangle OPR = 180°

So, ∠OPR + ∠ORP + ∠POR = 180°

2∠OPR + 160° = 180°

2∠OPR = 20°

∠OPR = 10°

Answer: ∠OPR = 10°

Theorem Used: The angle subtended by an arc at the centre is double the angle subtended by it at any point on the remaining part of the circle (Theorem 9.7).
Hinglish Explanation: ∠PQR = 100° diya hai. Arc PR centre par 2 × 100° = 200° ka angle banata hai (reflex angle). Minor angle POR = 360° - 200° = 160°. Triangle OPR isosceles hai kyunki OP = OR. Isliye ∠OPR = ∠ORP. Triangle ke angles ka sum 180° hai, isliye 2∠OPR + 160° = 180° ⇒ 2∠OPR = 20° ⇒ ∠OPR = 10°.

Question 5: In Fig. 9.26, A, B, C and D are four points on a circle. AC and BD intersect at a point E such that ∠BEC = 130° and ∠ECD = 20°. Find ∠BAC.

Now, ∠CDB = ∠CDE = 110°

∠BAC = ∠BDC (angles in the same segment)

But ∠BDC = ∠CDB = 110°

Answer: ∠BAC = 110°

Hinglish Explanation: ∠BEC = 130° aur ∠ECD = 20° diye hain. Linear pair se ∠CED = 180° - 130° = 50°. Triangle CED ke angles ka sum 180° hai, isliye ∠CDE = 180° - (50° + 20°) = 110°. Cyclic quadrilateral mein same segment ke angles equal hote hain, isliye ∠BAC = ∠BDC = 110°.

Question 6: ABCD is a cyclic quadrilateral whose diagonals intersect at a point E. If ∠DBC = 70°, ∠BAC is 30°, find ∠BCD. Further, if AB = BC, find ∠ECD.

Solution:

Given: ∠DBC = 70°, ∠BAC = 30°

In cyclic quadrilateral ABCD:

∠BDC = ∠BAC = 30° (angles in the same segment)

In triangle BCD:

∠BCD = 180° - (∠DBC + ∠BDC) = 180° - (70° + 30°) = 80°

Now, if AB = BC:

In triangle ABC, AB = BC, so it's isosceles

Therefore, ∠BCA = ∠BAC = 30°

Now, ∠BCD = ∠BCA + ∠ACD

80° = 30° + ∠ACD

So, ∠ACD = 50°

But ∠ACD = ∠ECD (same angle)

Therefore, ∠ECD = 50°

Answer: ∠BCD = 80°, ∠ECD = 50°

Theorem Used: Angles in the same segment of a circle are equal (Theorem 9.8).
Hinglish Explanation: ∠DBC = 70° aur ∠BAC = 30° diye hain. Same segment ke angles equal hain, isliye ∠BDC = ∠BAC = 30°. Triangle BCD mein ∠BCD = 180° - (70° + 30°) = 80°. Agar AB = BC, toh triangle ABC isosceles hai, isliye ∠BCA = ∠BAC = 30°. ∠BCD = ∠BCA + ∠ACD, so 80° = 30° + ∠ACD ⇒ ∠ACD = 50°. Yani ∠ECD = 50°.

Question 7: If diagonals of a cyclic quadrilateral are diameters of the circle through the vertices of the quadrilateral, prove that it is a rectangle.

Solution:

Let ABCD be a cyclic quadrilateral with diagonals AC and BD as diameters of the circle.

Since AC is a diameter, ∠ABC = 90° (angle in a semicircle)

Similarly, since AC is a diameter, ∠ADC = 90° (angle in a semicircle)

Since BD is a diameter, ∠BAD = 90° (angle in a semicircle)

Similarly, since BD is a diameter, ∠BCD = 90° (angle in a semicircle)

So, all angles of quadrilateral ABCD are 90°.

Therefore, ABCD is a rectangle.

Theorem Used: Angle in a semicircle is a right angle.
Hinglish Explanation: Cyclic quadrilateral ABCD ki diagonals AC aur BD circle ke diameters hain. AC diameter hai, isliye ∠ABC = 90° aur ∠ADC = 90° (semicircle ke angles right hote hain). BD diameter hai, isliye ∠BAD = 90° aur ∠BCD = 90°. Saare angles 90° hain, isliye ABCD rectangle hai.

Question 8: If the non-parallel sides of a trapezium are equal, prove that it is cyclic.

Solution:

Let ABCD be a trapezium with AB || CD and AD = BC.

Draw perpendiculars from A and B to CD, meeting CD at E and F respectively.

Since AB || CD, AE = BF (distance between parallel lines)

In right triangles AED and BFC:

AD = BC (given)

AE = BF (proved above)

So, ΔAED ≅ ΔBFC (by RHS congruence rule)

Therefore, ∠ADE = ∠BCF (CPCT)

Now, ∠ADC = ∠ADE and ∠BCD = ∠BCF

So, ∠ADC = ∠BCD

In trapezium ABCD, AB || CD

So, ∠BAD + ∠ADC = 180° (co-interior angles)

But ∠ADC = ∠BCD

So, ∠BAD + ∠BCD = 180°

Therefore, the sum of opposite angles is 180°.

Hence, by Theorem 9.11, ABCD is cyclic.

Theorem Used: If the sum of a pair of opposite angles of a quadrilateral is 180°, the quadrilateral is cyclic (Theorem 9.11).
Hinglish Explanation: Trapezium ABCD mein AB || CD aur AD = BC. A aur B se CD par perpendiculars draw karte hain. Triangles AED aur BFC congruent hote hain (RHS rule se). Isliye ∠ADC = ∠BCD. AB || CD hai, isliye ∠BAD + ∠ADC = 180° (co-interior angles). Par ∠ADC = ∠BCD, so ∠BAD + ∠BCD = 180°. Opposite angles ka sum 180° hai, isliye quadrilateral cyclic hai.

Question 9: Two circles intersect at two points B and C. Through B, two line segments ABD and PBQ are drawn to intersect the circles at A, D and P, Q respectively (see Fig. 9.27). Prove that ∠ACP = ∠QCD.

Solution:

In the first circle, ABCD is a cyclic quadrilateral.

So, ∠ACP = ∠ABP (angles in the same segment) ...(1)

In the second circle, PBCQ is a cyclic quadrilateral.

So, ∠QCD = ∠QBD (angles in the same segment) ...(2)

But ∠ABP and ∠QBD are vertically opposite angles at point B.

So, ∠ABP = ∠QBD ...(3)

From (1), (2) and (3):

∠ACP = ∠ABP = ∠QBD = ∠QCD

Therefore, ∠ACP = ∠QCD

Theorem Used: Angles in the same segment of a circle are equal (Theorem 9.8).
Hinglish Explanation: Do circles B aur C par intersect karte hain. Pehle circle mein ABCD cyclic quadrilateral hai, isliye ∠ACP = ∠ABP (same segment). Doosre circle mein PBCQ cyclic quadrilateral hai, isliye ∠QCD = ∠QBD (same segment). Point B par ∠ABP aur ∠QBD vertically opposite angles hain, isliye equal hain. Toh ∠ACP = ∠ABP = ∠QBD = ∠QCD. Yani ∠ACP = ∠QCD.

Question 10: If circles are drawn taking two sides of a triangle as diameters, prove that the point of intersection of these circles lie on the third side.

Solution:

Let ABC be a triangle.

Draw circles with AB and AC as diameters.

Let these circles intersect at point A and another point D.

Since AB is diameter of first circle, ∠ADB = 90° (angle in a semicircle)

Since AC is diameter of second circle, ∠ADC = 90° (angle in a semicircle)

Now, ∠ADB + ∠ADC = 90° + 90° = 180°

So, points B, D, C are collinear (angles on the same side of AD sum to 180°)

Therefore, D lies on BC.

Hence, the point of intersection of the circles lies on the third side BC.

Theorem Used: Angle in a semicircle is a right angle.
Hinglish Explanation: Triangle ABC ki sides AB aur AC as diameters lekar circles draw karte hain. Ye circles A aur ek aur point D par intersect karte hain. AB diameter hai, isliye ∠ADB = 90°. AC diameter hai, isliye ∠ADC = 90°. ∠ADB + ∠ADC = 180°, iska matlab points B, D, C ek straight line par hain. Yani D third side BC par hai.

Question 11: ABC and ADC are two right triangles with common hypotenuse AC. Prove that ∠CAD = ∠CBD.

Solution:

Given: ABC and ADC are right triangles with common hypotenuse AC.

So, ∠ABC = 90° and ∠ADC = 90°

Since ∠ABC + ∠ADC = 90° + 90° = 180°

Therefore, ABCD is a cyclic quadrilateral (opposite angles sum to 180°)

In cyclic quadrilateral ABCD:

∠CAD = ∠CBD (angles in the same segment CD)

Hence proved.

Theorem Used: If the sum of a pair of opposite angles of a quadrilateral is 180°, the quadrilateral is cyclic (Theorem 9.11).
Hinglish Explanation: ABC aur ADC right triangles hain jinki common hypotenuse AC hai. ∠ABC = 90° aur ∠ADC = 90°, inka sum 180° hai. Isliye ABCD cyclic quadrilateral hai. Cyclic quadrilateral mein same segment ke angles equal hote hain, isliye ∠CAD = ∠CBD.

Question 12: Prove that a cyclic parallelogram is a rectangle.

Solution:

Let ABCD be a cyclic parallelogram.

Since ABCD is a parallelogram, opposite angles are equal.

So, ∠A = ∠C and ∠B = ∠D

Since ABCD is cyclic, the sum of opposite angles is 180°.

So, ∠A + ∠C = 180°

But ∠A = ∠C, so 2∠A = 180° ⇒ ∠A = 90°

Similarly, ∠B = 90°

So, all angles of parallelogram ABCD are 90°.

Therefore, ABCD is a rectangle.

Theorem Used: The sum of either pair of opposite angles of a cyclic quadrilateral is 180° (Theorem 9.10).
Hinglish Explanation: ABCD ek cyclic parallelogram hai. Parallelogram mein opposite angles equal hote hain, so ∠A = ∠C aur ∠B = ∠D. Cyclic quadrilateral mein opposite angles ka sum 180° hota hai, so ∠A + ∠C = 180°. Par ∠A = ∠C, isliye 2∠A = 180° ⇒ ∠A = 90°. Similarly ∠B = 90°. Saare angles 90° hain, isliye ABCD rectangle hai.
