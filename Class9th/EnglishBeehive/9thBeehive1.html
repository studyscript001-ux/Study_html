Chapter 1: The Fun They Had

Thinking about the Text

Activity

Calculate how many years and months ahead from now Margie's diary entry is.

Margie's diary entry is dated 17 May 2157. The current year is 2024. So, the diary entry is 133 years ahead (2157 - 2024 = 133 years).

I. Answer these questions in a few words or a couple of sentences each.

1. How old are Margie and Tommy?

Margie is eleven years old and Tommy is thirteen years old.

2. What did Margie write in her diary?

Margie wrote in her diary that Tommy found a real book that day.

3. Had Margie ever seen a book before?

No, Margie had never seen a book before. She had only seen telebooks.

4. What things about the book did she find strange?

Margie found it strange that the words in the book stood still instead of moving like they do on a screen, and that the pages were yellow and crinkly.

5. What do you think a telebook is?

A telebook is an electronic book that is displayed on a television or computer screen.

6. Where was Margie's school? Did she have any classmates?

Margie's school was in her house, right next to her bedroom. She did not have any classmates.

7. What subjects did Margie and Tommy learn?

Margie and Tommy learned geography and history. Margie was specifically having problems with geography.

II. Answer the following with reference to the story.

1. "I wouldn't throw it away."

(i) Who says these words?

(ii) What does 'it' refer to?

(iii) What is it being compared with by the speaker?

(i) Tommy says these words.

(ii) 'It' refers to the real book that Tommy found.

(iii) Tommy is comparing the real book with telebooks. He thinks that telebooks are better because they can hold millions of books and can be reused, unlike the paper book which gets thrown away after reading.

2. "Sure they had a teacher, but it wasn't a regular teacher. It was a man."

(i) Who does 'they' refer to?

(ii) What does 'regular' mean here?

(iii) What is it contrasted with?

(i) 'They' refers to the children who went to school centuries ago.

(ii) Here, 'regular' means normal or usual.

(iii) The human teacher is contrasted with the mechanical teachers that Margie and Tommy have.

III. Answer each of these questions in a short paragraph (about 30 words).

1. What kind of teachers did Margie and Tommy have?

Margie and Tommy had mechanical teachers. These were large, black, and ugly machines with a big screen that showed lessons and asked questions. They had a slot where students had to put homework and test papers.

2. Why did Margie's mother send for the County Inspector?

Margie's mother sent for the County Inspector because Margie had been performing poorly in geography. The mechanical teacher had been giving her test after test, and her performance was getting worse.

3. What did he do?

The County Inspector smiled at Margie, gave her an apple, and then took the mechanical teacher apart. He fixed the geography sector which was geared too quick and slowed it down to an average ten-year level.

4. Why was Margie doing badly in geography? What did the County Inspector do to help her?

Margie was doing badly in geography because the geography sector of her mechanical teacher was geared a little too quick. The County Inspector slowed it down to an average ten-year level to help her.

5. What had once happened to Tommy's teacher?

Tommy's teacher had once been taken away for nearly a month because the history sector had blanked out completely.

6. Did Margie have regular days and hours for school? If so, why?

Yes, Margie had regular days and hours for school. Her mechanical teacher was turned on at the same time every day except Saturdays and Sundays because her mother believed that little girls learned better if they learned at regular hours.

7. How does Tommy describe the old kind of school?

Tommy describes the old kind of school as a special building where all the kids went together. They learned the same things if they were the same age, and they had a human teacher who told them things, gave them homework, and asked them questions.

8. How does he describe the old kind of teachers?

Tommy describes the old kind of teachers as men who didn't live in the house but worked in a special building. They didn't teach at home but taught all the kids together in the school building.

IV. Answer each of these questions in two or three paragraphs (100-150 words).

1. What are the main features of the mechanical teachers and the schoolrooms that Margie and Tommy have in the story?

The mechanical teachers in the story are large, black, and ugly machines with a big screen. They display lessons and ask questions to the students. These teachers are personalized for each student and adjusted to fit their learning pace and level. They have a slot where students insert their homework and test papers written in a punch code.

The schoolrooms are located in the students' homes, right next to their bedrooms. Each student studies alone with their mechanical teacher at fixed hours every day. There are no classmates, and the learning experience is completely individualized and isolated compared to traditional schools.

2. Why did Margie hate school? Why did she think the old kind of school must have been fun?

Margie hated school because she found her mechanical teacher boring and frustrating. She was performing poorly in geography, which made her dislike school even more. The mechanical nature of her teacher and the isolated learning environment made the experience joyless for her.

She thought the old kind of school must have been fun because all the kids from the neighborhood went together, laughing and shouting in the schoolyard. They sat together in the classroom, learned the same things, could help each other with homework, and went home together at the end of the day. The human interaction and social aspect of traditional schools appealed to her.

3. Do you agree with Margie that schools today are more fun than the school in the story? Give reasons for your answer.

Yes, I agree with Margie that schools today are more fun than the school in the story. Traditional schools provide social interaction with classmates, which makes learning more enjoyable. Students can participate in group activities, sports, and extracurricular activities that develop social skills and friendships.

Human teachers in today's schools can understand students' emotions, provide personalized attention, and make learning engaging through various teaching methods. The school environment includes playgrounds, libraries, and laboratories that offer diverse learning experiences beyond just academic instruction, making education a more holistic and enjoyable process.
