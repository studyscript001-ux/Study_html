Thinking about the Text

1 Who does Lencho have complete faith in? Which sentences in the story tell you this?

Lencho has complete faith in God. This is shown in the story when he writes a letter to God asking for help, and when the narrator says: "But in the hearts of all who lived in that solitary house in the middle of the valley, there was a single hope: help from God."

Note: इस question में आपको बताना है कि Lencho को किस पर भरोसा था और story से वो sentences देनी हैं जो यह दिखाती हैं।
2 Why does the postmaster send money to Lencho? Why does he sign the letter 'God'?

The postmaster sends money to Lencho to keep his faith in God alive. He signs the letter 'God' to make Lencho believe that God himself has replied to his letter and sent the money.

Note: इस question में आपको postmaster के पैसे भेजने के कारण और God के नाम से sign करने का reason बताना है।
3 Did Lencho try to find out who had sent the money to him? Why/Why not?

No, Lencho did not try to find out who sent the money because he had complete faith that it was sent by God himself. He never doubted that God would help him.

Note: इस question में आपको बताना है कि क्या Lencho ने पैसे भेजने वाले को ढूंढने की कोशिश की और क्यों/क्यों नहीं।
4 Who does Lencho think has taken the rest of the money? What is the irony in the situation?

Lencho thinks the post office employees have taken the rest of the money. The irony is that these were the same people who had collected money to help him, but he suspects them of stealing.

Note: इस question में आपको बताना है कि Lencho किसे पैसे लेने वाला समझता है और situation में irony क्या है।
5 Are there people like Lencho in the real world? What kind of a person would you say he is?

Yes, there are people like Lencho in the real world. Lencho is a naive, faithful, and hardworking person who has complete trust in God but lacks understanding of how the world works.

Note: इस question में आपको बताना है क्या real world में Lencho जैसे लोग होते हैं और आप Lencho को कैसे person मानते हैं।
6 There are two kinds of conflict in the story: between humans and nature, and between humans themselves. How are these conflicts illustrated?

The conflict between humans and nature is shown when the hailstorm destroys Lencho's crops. The conflict between humans is shown when Lencho suspects the post office employees of stealing money, even though they helped him.

Note: इस question में आपको story में दो conflicts के बारे में बताना है और उन्हें examples के साथ explain करना है।

Thinking about the Language

I Match the following storms with their descriptions:

1. Cyclone - A violent tropical storm in which strong winds move in a circle

2. Gale - An extremely strong wind

3. Typhoon - A violent tropical storm with very strong winds

4. Tornado - A violent storm whose centre is a cloud in the shape of a funnel

5. Hurricane - A violent storm with very strong winds, especially in the western Atlantic Ocean

6. Whirlwind - A very strong wind that moves very fast in a spinning movement

Note: इस question में आपको storms के नाम उनकी descriptions से match करने हैं।
II Match the sentences with the meanings of 'hope':

1. Wanting something to happen (and thinking it quite possible) - "Will you get the subjects you want to study in college? I hope so."

2. Showing concern that what you say should not offend - "I hope you don't mind my saying this..."

3. A feeling that something good will probably happen - "This discovery will give new hope to HIV/AIDS sufferers."

4. Wishing for something to happen, although very unlikely - "We were hoping against hope that the judges would not notice our mistakes."

5. Thinking that this would happen - "I called early in the hope of speaking to her before she went to school."

6. Stopped believing that this good thing would happen - "Just when everybody had given up hope, the fishermen came back..."

Note: इस question में आपको sentences को 'hope' word के meanings से match करना है।
III Join the sentences using who, whom, whose, which:

1. I often go to Mumbai, which is the commercial capital of India.

2. My mother, who cooks very well, is going to host a TV show on cooking.

3. These sportspersons, whose performance has been excellent, are going to meet the President.

4. Lencho prayed to God, whose eyes see into our minds.

5. This man, whom I trusted, cheated me.

Note: इस question में आपको दिए गए sentences को who, whom, whose, या which का use करके join करना है।
IV Find sentences in the story with negative words that express these ideas emphatically:

1. "Not a leaf remained on the trees."

2. "It was nothing less than a letter to God."

3. "Never in his career as a postman had he known that address."

Note: इस question में आपको story से negative words वाले sentences ढूंढने हैं जो दिए गए ideas को emphasize करते हैं।
V Find metaphors from the story:

1. Raindrops - "New coins" (compared to money/value)

2. Hailstones - "New silver coins" (compared to silver coins)

3. Locusts - "A plague" (compared to a disease that causes destruction)

4. Lencho - "An ox of a man" (compared to an ox for his strength and hard work)

Note: इस question में आपको story से metaphors ढूंढने हैं और बताना है कि वे किसकी comparison कर रहे हैं।
