Comprehension Check - Part I

1. What tasks, do you think, were assigned to the dog and the ox?
The dog was assigned to fetch and carry things with his mouth. The ox was assigned to plough the fields with a yoke on his neck.
2. Why did the camel live in the middle of the desert?
The camel lived in the middle of the desert because he did not want to work. He was lazy and preferred to eat sticks, thorns, and prickles instead of working like other animals.
3. What made the dog, the horse and the ox very angry?
The dog, horse, and ox were angry because the camel refused to work, and as a result, they had to work double-time to make up for the camel's idleness. The man told them they must work extra because the camel wouldn't work.
4. How did the Djinn know the horse was complaining against the camel?
The Djinn was in charge of all deserts and had special powers. When the horse described an animal with a long neck and long legs who hadn't worked since Monday, the Djinn immediately recognized it was the camel he was responsible for.

Comprehension Check - Part II

1. The camel was looking at his own reflection in the pool. What does it suggest to you about the camel?
This suggests that the camel was proud and vain. He was admiring himself and his appearance instead of working like the other animals.
2. The camel said, "Humph" repeatedly. How did it affect him?
Every time the camel said "Humph", it caused his back to puff up into a hump. When he said it to the Djinn after being warned not to, his back swelled into a big hump that became permanent.
3. What, according to the Djinn, was the use of the "humph"?
According to the Djinn, the hump would allow the camel to work for three days without eating. The camel could live on his hump, which stored energy, making up for the three days of work he had missed at the beginning of the world.
4. "...he has never yet learnt to behave". In the light of this, what is the writer's opinion about the camel?
The writer suggests that the camel never improved his lazy behavior. Even after getting the hump and being forced to work, the camel remained ill-mannered and didn't change his attitude toward work.

Exercise Questions

1. Can this story be factually true?
No, this story cannot be factually true. It is a fable - a fictional story meant to teach a moral lesson. The story uses animals that talk and a magical Djinn to explain how the camel got its hump, which is not scientifically accurate but serves to convey an important message about the value of hard work.
2. What, according to you, is the story about?
This story is primarily about why everyone should do their share of work seriously. It teaches us that:
  • Laziness has consequences
  • When one person doesn't do their work, others have to work harder
  • Everyone should contribute their fair share
  • There's a reason for physical features of animals (in this case, the camel's hump)
3. What did you do over the weekend? Were you generally active or idle? Please check your back before starting to discuss or answer the question.
[This is a personal question. Students should answer based on their own experiences. Example answer:] Over the weekend, I was quite active. I completed my homework, helped my parents with household chores, played outdoor games with friends, and read a storybook. I made sure to balance work and fun activities.
4. There are broadly two categories of workers — those who prefer to do today what they can do tomorrow, and those who prefer to do tomorrow what they can do today. Where do you belong?
[This is a personal question. Students should answer based on their own work habits. Example answer:] I try to belong to the category that prefers to do today what can be done tomorrow. This helps me stay ahead of my tasks and reduces stress. However, sometimes when I have too much work, I might procrastinate, but I'm working on improving this habit.
