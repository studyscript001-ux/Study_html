Comprehension Check - Part I

1. Why were travellers attracted towards India?
Travellers were attracted towards India because it was considered a land of wonder with rich culture, wealth, religions, philosophies, art, architecture, and educational practices that had spread far and wide.
2. What were the sources of the ancient education system?
The sources of the ancient education system included the Vedas, Brahmanas, Upanishads, Dharmasutras, writings of scholars like Aryabhata, Panini, Katyayana, Patanjali, and medical treatises of Charaka and Sushruta. Learning was also drawn from various disciplines like Itihas (history), Anviksiki (logic), Mimamsa (interpretation), Shilpashastra (architecture), Arthashastra (polity), Varta (agriculture, trade, commerce, animal husbandry), and Dhanurvidya (archery).
3. What were the features of education system in ancient India?
The ancient education system focused on the holistic development of individuals, addressing both inner and outer self. It emphasized moral, physical, spiritual, and intellectual aspects of life, with values like humility, truthfulness, discipline, self-reliance, and respect for all creations. The system focused on both learning and physical development, aiming for a healthy mind and healthy body. Education was pragmatic, achievable, and complementary to life.
4. What was the role of guru in pupils' lives?
Gurus played a central role in pupils' lives. They lived together with their shishyas (students), helping each other in day-to-day life. The main objective was to have complete learning, lead a disciplined life, and realize one's inner potential. Gurus had complete autonomy in selecting students and designing their syllabi. They taught through debate and discussions and were assisted by advanced level students.

Comprehension Check - Part II

1. Where did nuns and monks receive their education?
Nuns and monks received their education in monasteries/viharas, which were set up for them to meditate, debate, and discuss with learned scholars for their quest for knowledge.
2. What is Panini known for?
Panini is known as a legendary Indian grammarian who was an expert in language and grammar. He authored one of the greatest works on grammar called Ashtadhyayi.
3. Which university did Xuan Zang and I-Qing study at?
Xuan Zang and I-Qing studied at Nalanda University.
4. Which subject did Xuan Zang study in India?
Xuan Zang studied yogashastra (yoga philosophy) in India at Nalanda University under Chancellor Shilabhadra.
5. How did society help in the education of the students?
Society helped in education by considering knowledge sacred and not charging fees. Contributions towards education were considered the highest form of donation. Financial support came from rich merchants, wealthy parents, and society. Besides gifts of buildings, universities received gifts of land. All members of society contributed in some form or the other to support education.

Exercise Questions

1. Which salient features of the ancient education system of India made it globally renowned?
The ancient education system of India became globally renowned due to its focus on holistic development, emphasis on moral and spiritual values, practical approach to learning, and the presence of world-class universities like Takshashila and Nalanda. The system's emphasis on debate, discussion, and critical thinking, along with its comprehensive curriculum covering diverse subjects, attracted students from all over the world.
2. Why do you think students from other countries came to India to study at that time?
Students from other countries came to India because it was a center of excellence in education with renowned teachers and universities. India offered knowledge in diverse fields like medicine, astronomy, mathematics, philosophy, and arts that was not available elsewhere. The reputation of Indian teachers and the quality of education attracted international students despite the long and arduous journeys required.
3. Why is education considered 'a way of life'?
Education was considered 'a way of life' in ancient India because it was not limited to formal instruction but encompassed all aspects of life. It focused on the holistic development of individuals, emphasizing values, discipline, self-reliance, and respect for all creations. Education was integrated into daily living through the guru-shishya tradition, where students lived with their teachers and learned through practical experience and observation.
4. What do you understand by holistic education?
Holistic education refers to an approach that focuses on the development of the whole person - addressing moral, physical, spiritual, and intellectual aspects of life. It emphasizes values like humility, truthfulness, discipline, self-reliance, and respect for all creations. Holistic education aims to create a balance between human beings and nature, and between learning and physical development, resulting in healthy minds and healthy bodies.
5. Why do you think Takshashila and Nalanda have been declared heritage sites?
Takshashila and Nalanda have been declared heritage sites because they represent exceptional centers of learning from ancient times that made significant contributions to human knowledge. These universities attracted students from all over the world and were renowned for their expertise in various fields. Their archaeological remains provide valuable insights into ancient education systems and architectural excellence, making them worthy of preservation for future generations.
