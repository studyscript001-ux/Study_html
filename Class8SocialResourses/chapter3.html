1. Answer the following questions.

(i) What is agriculture?

Agriculture is a primary activity that includes the science and art of cultivating soil, growing crops (like fruits, vegetables, flowers), and rearing livestock. It is also called farming.

(ii) Name the factors influencing agriculture.

The main factors influencing agriculture are:

  • Favourable topography of soil: The quality and type of soil.
  • Climate: Factors like temperature, rainfall, and sunshine.

(iii) What is shifting cultivation? What are its disadvantages?

Shifting cultivation is a type of farming where a plot of land is cleared by cutting and burning trees. The ashes are mixed with the soil, and crops are grown for a few years until the soil loses its fertility. The farmer then abandons the land and moves to a new plot.

Disadvantages:

  • Deforestation: It involves cutting down forests on a large scale.
  • Soil Erosion: When the land is left bare, the topsoil is easily washed away by rain.
  • Loss of fertility: The method is not sustainable as the soil becomes infertile after a few years.

(iv) What is plantation agriculture?

Plantation agriculture is a type of commercial farming where a single crop is grown on a large scale. Examples include tea, coffee, sugarcane, rubber, and banana. It requires a large amount of capital and labour, and a good transport network is essential.

(v) Name the fibre crops and name the climatic conditions required for their growth.

The two main fibre crops mentioned are Cotton and Jute.

  • Cotton: Requires high temperature, light rainfall, 210 frost-free days, and bright sunshine. It grows best on black and alluvial soils.
  • Jute (Golden Fibre): Requires high temperature, heavy rainfall, and a humid climate. It grows well on alluvial soil.

3. Give reasons.

(i) In India agriculture is a primary activity.

Agriculture is a primary activity in India because it is directly concerned with the extraction and production of natural resources (crops and livestock). A large portion of India's population (two-thirds, according to the chapter) is still dependent on agriculture for their livelihood.

(ii) Different crops are grown in different regions.

Different crops are grown in different regions because each crop requires specific geographical and climatic conditions to grow well. Factors like soil type, temperature, rainfall, and topography vary from one region to another, making certain areas suitable for specific crops (e.g., rice in wet regions, millets in dry regions).
