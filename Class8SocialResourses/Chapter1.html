1. Answer the following questions.

(i) Why are resources distributed unequally over the earth?

The distribution of natural resources is unequal because it depends on physical factors like terrain, climate, and altitude, which vary across the Earth.

(ii) What is resource conservation?

Resource conservation is using resources carefully and giving them time to get renewed. It ensures that resources are not depleted and are available for the future.

(iii) Why are human resources important?

Human resources are important because people, with their knowledge and skills, can create more resources from natural substances. They are essential for development.

(iv) What is sustainable development?

Sustainable development is balancing the need to use resources for present needs while also conserving them for future generations.

üé® Activity

"Rahiman paani raakhiye, Bin paani sab soon..." What kind of resource is the poet referring to? Write in 100 words what would happen if this resource disappeared?

The poet is referring to water, which is a vital natural and renewable resource.

"Says Rahim, keep water, as without water there is nothing. Without water pearl, swan and dough cannot exist."

If water disappeared, life on Earth would end. Plants and animals would die, leading to the collapse of all ecosystems. There would be no agriculture, so no food could be grown. Industries would shut down, and daily human activities like drinking, cooking, and cleaning would become impossible. The planet would become a barren desert, incapable of supporting any form of life. Essentially, the disappearance of water means the end of our world as we know it.

1. Imagine living in prehistoric times on a windy plateau. What are the uses for the fast winds? Now, imagine it's the year 2138. How can you use the winds now?

Yes, in both scenarios, wind is a resource because it has utility.

In Prehistoric Times:

  • Drying clothes or food faster.
  • Driving away insects or predators with smoke from a fire.
  • Winnowing grain (separating grain from chaff).
  • As a simple form of recreation, like flying primitive kites.

In the Year 2138:

With advanced technology, wind is a much more important resource.

  • Generating clean electricity using highly efficient wind turbines.
  • Powering advanced transportation systems, like high-speed sail-powered vehicles.
  • Helping in weather modification and climate control systems.

The wind itself hasn't changed, but our knowledge and technology (human resources) have turned it into a far more valuable resource over time.
