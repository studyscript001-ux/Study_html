1. Answer the following questions.

(i) Which are the two main climatic factors responsible for soil formation?

The two main climatic factors responsible for soil formation are:

  • Temperature
  • Rainfall

These factors influence the rate of weathering and humus formation.

(ii) Write any two reasons for land degradation today.

Two major reasons for land degradation are:

  • Deforestation: Cutting down forests for agriculture and construction exposes the land to erosion.
  • Overgrazing: Excessive grazing by cattle removes the vegetation cover, making the land vulnerable.

(iii) Why is land considered an important resource?

Land is considered an important resource because it provides the surface for almost all human activities. We use land for:

  • Agriculture and Forestry
  • Building houses, roads, and industries
  • Mining

It is fundamental for our survival and development.

(iv) Name any two steps that government has taken to conserve plants and animals.

Two steps taken by the government are:

  • Establishing National Parks and Wildlife Sanctuaries: These are protected areas to preserve natural habitats.
  • Enacting Laws: Passing laws like the Wildlife Protection Act, which makes poaching and killing of many species (like tigers and lions) illegal.

(v) Suggest three ways to conserve water.

Three ways to conserve water are:

  • Rainwater Harvesting: Collecting rainwater from rooftops and storing it for future use.
  • Using Sprinklers and Drip Irrigation: These methods water plants efficiently and reduce water loss through evaporation and seepage.
  • Afforestation: Planting more trees and vegetation slows down surface runoff and helps replenish underground water levels.

5. Activity

Discuss some more reasons which are responsible for changes of land use pattern.

Land use patterns change over time due to several reasons:

  • Growing Population: More people need more houses to live in and more food to eat, which leads to converting forests and common lands into housing complexes and agricultural fields.
  • Urbanization: The expansion of cities requires land for infrastructure like roads, metro lines, commercial buildings, and factories.
  • Technological Advancement: New technology can make previously unusable land (like arid areas) productive, or it can increase the demand for land for new types of industries (like data centers).
  • Changing Lifestyles: As living standards rise, the demand for recreational areas like parks, golf courses, and shopping malls increases, which alters land use.
