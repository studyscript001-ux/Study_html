Question 4

Can microorganisms be seen with the naked eye? If not, how can they be seen?

No, microorganisms cannot be seen with the unaided eye because they are so small. Some, like the fungus that grows on bread, can be seen with a magnifying glass. Others require a microscope to be seen.

Question 5

What are the major groups of microorganisms?

The four major groups of microorganisms are bacteria, fungi, protozoa, and some algae. Viruses are also microscopic but are considered different from other microorganisms.

Question 6

Name the microorganisms which can fix atmospheric nitrogen in the soil.

Some bacteria and blue-green algae can fix atmospheric nitrogen in the soil. These are commonly called biological nitrogen fixers. The bacterium Rhizobium is specifically mentioned as being involved in nitrogen fixation in leguminous plants.

Question 8

Write a short paragraph on the harmful effects of microorganisms.

Microorganisms are harmful in many ways. Some cause diseases in humans, plants, and animals; these are called pathogens. Pathogens can enter our bodies through the air we breathe, the water we drink, or the food we eat. For example, viruses cause common ailments like colds, flu, polio, and chickenpox. Bacteria cause diseases like typhoid and tuberculosis, while protozoa cause dysentery and malaria. Microorganisms also spoil food, clothing, and leather. The consumption of food spoiled by microbes can lead to food poisoning, which can cause serious illness and even death, as some microbes produce toxic substances in food.

Question 9

What are antibiotics? What precautions must be taken while taking antibiotics?

Antibiotics are medicines that kill or stop the growth of disease-causing microorganisms. They are produced from bacteria and fungi.

When taking antibiotics, you must follow these precautions:

• Only take antibiotics on the advice of a qualified doctor.

• You must complete the full course prescribed by the doctor.

• Taking antibiotics when not needed or in incorrect doses can make the medicine less effective in the future.

• Taking them unnecessarily can also kill the beneficial bacteria in your body.

• Antibiotics are not effective against diseases like colds and flu because these are caused by viruses.
