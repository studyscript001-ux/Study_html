1. Why do you think our national movement supported the idea that all adults have a right to vote?

Our national movement supported the idea that all adults have a right to vote because:

  • The freedom struggle was inspired by the ideas of equality and participation in decision-making for all.
  • Under British rule, Indians were not allowed to participate in government decisions and lived in fear.
  • The nationalists wanted a free India where the government would be sensitive to people's needs and every citizen could take part in building the nation.
  • Granting everyone the right to vote (universal adult franchise) was the best way to ensure a truly democratic government where all citizens are equal.

  • Blue/Grey Constituencies: These are seats reserved for Scheduled Castes (SC).
  • Uncolored/Pink Constituencies: These are General (GEN) constituencies, open for all candidates.

4. Describe the different ways in which womenâ€™s groups worked to make the new law on domestic violence happen.

Women's groups used several methods over many years to get the Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act (2005) passed. The key steps they took were:

  • Raising Awareness: They travelled across the country, held public hearings and meetings to collect stories from women who had faced violence.
  • Drafting a Bill: A group of lawyers and activists (Lawyers Collective) drafted a bill that addressed the needs of women.
  • Public Campaign: They circulated this draft bill widely and held meetings to build public support for the new law.
  • Lobbying Parliament: They made submissions to the Parliamentary Standing Committee to ensure the final law included their suggestions.
  • Using Media: They used press conferences and online petitions to put pressure on the government to introduce and pass the bill in Parliament.
