1. Why does a democratic country need a Constitution?

A democratic country needs a Constitution because it:

  • Lays down the basic ideals of the society.
  • Defines the nature of the country's political system.
  • Provides rules to prevent the misuse of power by leaders.
  • Protects the rights of minorities against the majority.
  • Guarantees Fundamental Rights to protect citizens' freedom.

2. What is the difference in who exercises ‘Executive Power’ in the two Constitutions of Nepal?

The main difference is the shift of power from a monarch to an elected body.

  • 1990 Constitution: Executive power was held by the King and the Council of Ministers.
  • 2015 Constitution: Executive power is held only by the Council of Ministers, who are the people's elected representatives.

3. What would happen if there were no restrictions on the power of elected representatives?

If there were no restrictions, elected representatives could:

  • Easily misuse their power and authority.
  • Become dictatorial and act against the public interest.
  • Take away the Fundamental Rights of the citizens.
  • Make unfair decisions that harm minority groups.

4. Identify the minority in the following situations and state one reason why their views should be respected.

(a) In a school with 30 teachers, 20 of them are male.

Minority: The 10 female teachers.
Reason: To ensure a fair work environment and make balanced decisions for the school.

(b) In a city, 5 per cent of the population are Buddhists.

Minority: The Buddhist population.
Reason: To protect their religious and cultural freedom from majority domination.

(c) In a factory mess for all employees, 80 per cent are vegetarians.

Minority: The 20 per cent non-vegetarian employees.
Reason: To ensure their food choices are respected and they are treated fairly.

(d) In a class of 50 students, 40 belong to more well-off families.

Minority: The 10 students from less well-off families.
Reason: To ensure they get equal opportunities and do not feel excluded.

5. Explain the importance of the key features of the Indian Constitution.

Key Feature Significance (Why it is important)
Federalism It creates government at both central and state levels to properly govern a large and diverse country like India.
Separation of Powers It divides power among the legislature, executive, and judiciary to prevent misuse of power by any single branch.
Fundamental Rights These rights protect citizens from the state's misuse of power and guarantee a life of dignity and freedom for everyone.
Parliamentary Form of Government It allows all adult citizens to vote and elect their representatives, making the government accountable to the people.
