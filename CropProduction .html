4. Write a paragraph in your own
words on each of the following:
(a) Preparation of soil: The preparation
of soil is crucial for crop growth. It
involves tilling and leveling the soil to
create a conducive environment for
seed germination. Loosening the soil
allows roots to penetrate deep and
access nutrients and water, which are
essential for healthy plant growth.
(b) Sowing: Sowing is the process of
planting seeds in the prepared soil. It is
important to use healthy seeds and sow
them at the right depth and distance to
avoid overcrowding, which can hinder
growth. Proper sowing ensures that the
plants receive adequate sunlight and
nutrients.

(c) Weeding: Weeding involves
removing unwanted plants that compete
with crops for resources. Weeds can
affect the growth of crops by consuming
water and nutrients. Regular weeding,
either manually or through the use of
chemicals, is necessary to ensure the
health of the crop.
(d) Threshing: Threshing is the process
of separating grains from the chaff after
harvesting. This can be done manually
or using machines like combines. It is an
important step in preparing the produce
for storage and consumption.

5. Explain how fertilisers are different
from manure?

Answer: Fertilisers are man-made
inorganic substances rich in specific
nutrients, while manure is a natural
organic substance derived from the
decomposition of plant and animal
waste. Fertilisers provide concentrated
nutrients but do not add humus to the
soil, whereas manure enhances soil
structure, increases water retention, and
promotes microbial activity.

6. What is irrigation? Describe two
methods of irrigation which conserve
water.
Answer: Irrigation is the supply of water
to crops at regular intervals to ensure
their growth. Two methods of irrigation
that conserve water include:

1. Drip irrigation, which delivers water
directly to the roots of the plants,
minimizing wastage.
2. Sprinkler irrigation, where water is
sprayed over the crops like rain, which
is effective for uneven land.

7. If wheat is sown in the kharif
season, what would happen?
Discuss.
Answer: If wheat is sown in the kharif
season, it would not thrive, as wheat is a
rabi crop that requires cooler
temperatures for proper growth. The
warm conditions of the kharif season
would not be suitable for wheat
cultivation.

8. Explain how soil gets affected by
the continuous plantation of crops
ina field.
Answer: Continuous plantation of crops
in a field can lead to soil nutrient
depletion, making it poor in essential
minerals. This can adversely affect crop
yield and soil health. Farmers often
need to replenish nutrients through the
use of organic manure or chemical
fertilisers.

9. What are weeds? How can we
control them?
Answer: Weeds are undesired plants
that grow alongside crops and compete

for resources. They can be controlled
through manual removal, chemical
weedicides, or by maintaining proper
crop management practices.

Previous Years Q&A
MP Board (2022-25)

1. Define Agriculture.
Answer: Agriculture is the practice of cultivating
soil, growing crops, and rearing animals to
provide food and other products useful to
humans.

2. What is soil? Name its constituents.
Answer: Soil is the uppermost weathered layer
of the earth’s crust that supports plant growth.
Constituents of soil: Minerals, water, air, organic
matter (humus), and microorganisms.

3. What is humus? State its importance.
Answer: Humus is the dark organic material
formed by the decomposition of dead plants and
animals.

Importance:
1. Improves soil fertility.
2. Makes soil porous for aeration.
3. Increases water-holding capacity of soil.
4. Supplies essential nutrients to plants.

4. How do microbes help in soil fertility?
Answer: Microbes decompose organic matter in
the soil and form humus. Some microbes (e.g.,
Rhizobium bacteria) fix atmospheric nitrogen,
enriching the soil with nutrients and improving
fertility.

5. Differentiate between Kharif and Rabi
crops.
Answer: Kharif crops: Sown in rainy season
(June–September). Example: Paddy, Maize.

Rabi crops: Sown in winter season (October–
March). Example: Wheat, Gram.

6. What is manuring? Why is it necessary?
Answer: The process of adding organic manure
to soil is called manuring.
Necessity: Continuous cropping reduces soil
nutrients. Manuring replenishes nutrients,
improves soil texture, and ensures healthy plant
growth.

7. Distinguish between Fertilisers and
Manure.
Answer: Fertilisers are man-made chemicals,
rich in nutrients like NPK, prepared in factories,
and give immediate results but reduce soil
fertility on overuse.
Manure is natural (from plant/animal waste), less
nutrient-rich, but improves soil texture, adds

humus, and is eco-friendly.

8. What is irrigation? Name two modern
methods which save water.
Answer: Irrigation is the supply of water to crops
at regular intervals.
Modern methods conserving water:
1. Sprinkler system – Sprays water like rainfall,
suitable for uneven lands.
2. Drip system – Supplies water drop by drop
near roots; no wastage of water.

9. What are weeds? How can they be
controlled?
Answer: Unwanted plants growing with crops
are called weeds.
Control methods:
1. Manual removal by khurpi.

2. Tilling before sowing.
3. Using weedicides (like 2,4-D).

10. Why is paddy grown in rainy season
only?
Answer: Paddy requires a lot of water for
growth, which is naturally available during the
rainy season.

11. Why does continuous cropping make soil
infertile? How can it be improved?
Answer: Continuous cropping reduces essential
nutrients from soil.
Improvement methods: Manuring, adding
fertilisers, and crop rotation with legumes.

12. Why should seeds be sown at proper
depth and spacing?

Answer: Seeds at proper depth are safe from
birds and get moisture for germination. Proper
spacing avoids overcrowding, ensuring enough
sunlight, water, and nutrients for each plant.
