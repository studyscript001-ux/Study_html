Question 4

Write a paragraph in your own words on each of the following:

(a) Preparation of soil:

The preparation of soil is crucial for crop growth. It involves tilling and leveling the soil to create a conducive environment for seed germination. Loosening the soil allows roots to penetrate deep and access nutrients and water.

(b) Sowing:

Sowing is the process of planting seeds in the prepared soil. It is important to use healthy seeds and sow them at the right depth and distance to avoid overcrowding.

Question 5

Explain how fertilisers are different from manure?

Fertilisers are man-made inorganic substances rich in specific nutrients, while manure is a natural organic substance derived from the decomposition of plant and animal waste. Fertilisers provide concentrated nutrients but do not add humus to the soil, whereas manure enhances soil structure, increases water retention, and promotes microbial activity.

Question 6

What is irrigation? Describe two methods of irrigation which conserve water.

Irrigation is the supply of water to crops at regular intervals to ensure their growth. Two methods of irrigation that conserve water include:

1. Drip irrigation, which delivers water directly to the roots of the plants, minimizing wastage.

2. Sprinkler irrigation, where water is sprayed over the crops like rain, which is effective for uneven land.

Previous Years Q&A

MP Board (2022-25)

Question 1

Define Agriculture.

Agriculture is the practice of cultivating soil, growing crops, and rearing animals to provide food and other products useful to humans.

Question 2

What is soil? Name its constituents.

Soil is the uppermost weathered layer of the earth's crust that supports plant growth.

Constituents of soil: Minerals, water, air, organic matter (humus), and microorganisms.

Question 7

Distinguish between Fertilisers and Manure.

Fertilisers are man-made chemicals, rich in nutrients like NPK, prepared in factories, and give immediate results but reduce soil fertility on overuse.

Manure is natural (from plant/animal waste), less nutrient-rich, but improves soil texture, adds humus, and is eco-friendly.

Question 8

What is irrigation? Name two modern methods which save water.

Irrigation is the supply of water to crops at regular intervals.

Modern methods conserving water:

1. Sprinkler system – Sprays water like rainfall, suitable for uneven lands.

2. Drip system – Supplies water drop by drop near roots; no wastage of water.
