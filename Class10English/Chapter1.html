Read and Find Out (Page 1)

1 Why is Mrs Pumphrey worried about Tricki?

Mrs Pumphrey is worried about Tricki because he has become extremely fat, lethargic, and inactive. He looks like a "bloated sausage" with bloodshot eyes and seems to have no energy.

2 What does she do to help him? Is she wise in this?

She gives him extra food like malt, cod-liver oil, and Horlicks, thinking he is malnourished. No, she is not wise as overfeeding is the actual cause of Tricki's problems, not malnutrition.

3 Who does 'I' refer to in this story?

The 'I' refers to the narrator, Mr. Herriot, who is a veterinary surgeon.

Read and Find Out (Page 3)

1 Is the narrator as rich as Tricki's mistress?

No, the narrator is not as rich as Mrs. Pumphrey. This is evident from his reaction to the luxurious items she sends for Tricki and how he enjoys the eggs, wine, and brandy meant for the dog.

2 How does he treat the dog?

He treats Tricki with a strict diet plan (no food for two days, only water) and plenty of exercise with other dogs, which helps him lose weight and regain health naturally.

3 Why is he tempted to keep Tricki on as a permanent guest?

He is tempted because of the luxurious items Mrs. Pumphrey sends for Tricki - fresh eggs, wine, and brandy - which the narrator and his partners enjoy.

4 Why does Mrs Pumphrey think the dog's recovery is "a triumph of surgery"?

Mrs Pumphrey believes it's a surgical triumph because she mistakenly attributes the recovery to medical procedures. In reality, Tricki recovered through natural methods like proper diet and exercise.

Think About It

1 What kind of a person do you think the narrator is? Would you say he is tactful as well as full of common sense?

The narrator is practical, caring, and sensible. He correctly diagnoses Tricki's problem as overfeeding and lack of exercise. He is tactful in handling Mrs. Pumphrey, understanding her attachment while insisting on what's best for the dog.

2 Do you think Tricki was happy to go home? What do you think will happen now?

Tricki was happy to see his mistress but might miss the active lifestyle with other dogs. Without continued proper diet and exercise, he might again become overweight if Mrs. Pumphrey returns to her overindulgent ways.

3 Do you think this is a real-life episode, or mere fiction? Or is it a mixture of both?

This story is likely a mixture of both real-life and fiction. While the characters and situations represent real behaviors, some elements like excessive gifts for a dog are exaggerated for humor and to make a point about overindulgence.

Talk About It

1 This episode describes the silly behaviour of a rich woman who is foolishly indulgent. Do you think such people are merely silly, or can their actions cause harm to others?

Mrs. Pumphrey's behavior is not merely silly but harmful. Her overindulgence nearly kills Tricki due to extreme obesity. This shows how misguided actions, even with good intentions, can have serious negative consequences.

2 Do you think there are also parents like Mrs Pumphrey?

Yes, there are certainly parents like Mrs. Pumphrey who overindulge their children with too many possessions, rich food, or excessive freedom without setting proper boundaries, which can spoil children and prevent development of important life skills.

3 What would you have done if you were: (i) a member of the staff in Mrs Pumphrey's household, (ii) a neighbour?

(i) As staff: I would have gently advised Mrs. Pumphrey about Tricki's health, showing her information about proper pet care.

(ii) As neighbor: I would have expressed concern and suggested a veterinary consultation, offering to walk Tricki for exercise.

4 What would you have done if you were in the narrator's place?

I would have insisted on taking Tricki for treatment but been more explicit that no surgery was needed - just proper diet and exercise. I would have provided clear instructions for maintaining Tricki's health after returning home.
