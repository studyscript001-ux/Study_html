Exercise 6.1

Question 1: How many 3-digit numbers can be formed from the digits 1, 2, 3, 4 and 5 assuming that
(i) repetition of the digits is allowed?
(ii) repetition of the digits is not allowed?

Solution:

(i) When repetition is allowed:

For a 3-digit number, we have 3 places: hundreds, tens, and units.

Each place can be filled in 5 ways (any of the digits 1,2,3,4,5).

By multiplication principle, total numbers = 5 × 5 × 5 = 125

(ii) When repetition is not allowed:

Hundreds place can be filled in 5 ways.

Tens place can be filled in 4 ways (remaining digits).

Units place can be filled in 3 ways (remaining digits).

By multiplication principle, total numbers = 5 × 4 × 3 = 60

Answer: (i) 125, (ii) 60

Hinglish Explanation: (i) Jab repetition allowed hai, toh har place (hundreds, tens, units) par 5 options hain, isliye 5×5×5=125 numbers. (ii) Jab repetition allowed nahi hai, toh pehle place par 5 options, fir second par 4, fir third par 3, isliye 5×4×3=60 numbers.

Question 2: How many 3-digit even numbers can be formed from the digits 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6 if the digits can be repeated?

Solution:

For a number to be even, the units digit must be even.

From digits 1,2,3,4,5,6, even digits are 2,4,6.

So, units place can be filled in 3 ways.

Since repetition is allowed:

Hundreds place can be filled in 6 ways.

Tens place can be filled in 6 ways.

By multiplication principle, total numbers = 6 × 6 × 3 = 108

Answer: 108

Hinglish Explanation: Even number ke liye units digit even honi chahiye. Digits 1-6 mein even digits hain 2,4,6 (3 options). Hundreds aur tens place par koi bhi digit (1-6) aa sakti hai (6 options each). Isliye total numbers = 6×6×3=108.

Question 1: Evaluate
(i) 8!
(ii) 4! - 3!

Solution:

(i) 8! = 8 × 7 × 6 × 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 40320

(ii) 4! = 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 24

3! = 3 × 2 × 1 = 6

So, 4! - 3! = 24 - 6 = 18

Answer: (i) 40320, (ii) 18

Hinglish Explanation: (i) 8 factorial = 8×7×6×5×4×3×2×1=40320. (ii) 4 factorial = 24, 3 factorial = 6, difference = 24-6=18.

Question 2: Is 3! + 4! = 7!?

Solution:

3! = 3 × 2 × 1 = 6

4! = 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 24

So, 3! + 4! = 6 + 24 = 30

7! = 7 × 6 × 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 5040

Clearly, 30 ≠ 5040

Answer: No, 3! + 4! ≠ 7!

Hinglish Explanation: 3! + 4! = 6+24=30, aur 7! = 5040. Dono equal nahi hain.

Question 3: Compute 8! 6! × 2!

Solution:

8! 6! × 2! = 8 × 7 × 6! 6! × 2!

Cancelling 6! from numerator and denominator:

= 8 × 7 2! = 56 2 = 28

Answer: 28

Hinglish Explanation: 8! = 8×7×6!, aur denominator mein 6!×2! hai. 6! cancel ho jata hai, bachta hai (8×7)/2! = 56/2 = 28.

Question 4: If 1 6! + 1 7! = x 8! , find x

Solution:

We have: 1 6! + 1 7! = x 8!

Note that 7! = 7 × 6! and 8! = 8 × 7 × 6!

So, LHS = 1 6! + 1 7 × 6! = 7 + 1 7 × 6! = 8 7 × 6!

RHS = x 8 × 7 × 6!

Equating both sides:

8 7 × 6! = x 8 × 7 × 6!

Cancelling 7 × 6! from both sides:

8 1 = x 8

So, x = 8 × 8 = 64

Answer: 64

Hinglish Explanation: Equation ko solve karte hain: 1/6! + 1/7! = x/8!. 7! = 7×6! aur 8! = 8×7×6! likh kar simplify karte hain. Finally x=64 milta hai.

Question 5: Evaluate n! (n - r)! , when
(i) n = 6, r = 2
(ii) n = 9, r = 5

Solution:

(i) When n = 6, r = 2:

n! (n - r)! = 6! (6 - 2)! = 6! 4! = 6 × 5 × 4! 4! = 6 × 5 = 30

(ii) When n = 9, r = 5:

n! (n - r)! = 9! (9 - 5)! = 9! 4! = 9 × 8 × 7 × 6 × 5 × 4! 4! = 9 × 8 × 7 × 6 × 5 = 15120

Answer: (i) 30, (ii) 15120

Hinglish Explanation: (i) n=6, r=2: 6!/(4!) = (6×5×4!)/4! = 6×5=30. (ii) n=9, r=5: 9!/(4!) = (9×8×7×6×5×4!)/4! = 9×8×7×6×5=15120.

Question 1: How many 3-digit numbers can be formed by using the digits 1 to 9 if no digit is repeated?

Solution:

We need to form 3-digit numbers using digits 1 to 9 with no repetition.

This is equivalent to permutations of 9 digits taken 3 at a time.

Number of 3-digit numbers = 9P3

9P3 = 9! (9 - 3)! = 9! 6! = 9 × 8 × 7 = 504

Answer: 504

Hinglish Explanation: Digits 1-9 se 3-digit numbers banane hain, repetition nahi allowed. Ye permutations of 9 objects taken 3 at a time hai: 9P3 = 9×8×7=504.

Question 2: How many 4-digit numbers are there with no digit repeated?

Solution:

For a 4-digit number:

Thousands place cannot be 0 (would make it a 3-digit number).

Thousands place can be filled in 9 ways (digits 1-9).

Hundreds place can be filled in 9 ways (remaining 9 digits, including 0).

Tens place can be filled in 8 ways.

Units place can be filled in 7 ways.

By multiplication principle, total numbers = 9 × 9 × 8 × 7 = 4536

Answer: 4536

Hinglish Explanation: 4-digit number ke liye thousands place par 0 nahi aa sakta, isliye 9 options (1-9). Fir hundreds place par 9 options (0 aur bache hue digits), tens par 8, units par 7. Total = 9×9×8×7=4536.

Question 3: How many 3-digit even numbers can be made using the digits 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 7, if no digit is repeated?

Solution:

For a number to be even, units digit must be even.

From given digits 1,2,3,4,6,7, even digits are 2,4,6.

We have two cases:

Case 1: Units digit is 2

Then hundreds digit can be filled in 5 ways (remaining digits except 2)

Tens digit can be filled in 4 ways

Numbers ending with 2: 5 × 4 = 20

Case 2: Units digit is 4

Similarly, numbers ending with 4: 5 × 4 = 20

Case 3: Units digit is 6

Similarly, numbers ending with 6: 5 × 4 = 20

Total even numbers = 20 + 20 + 20 = 60

Answer: 60

Hinglish Explanation: Even number ke liye units digit even honi chahiye. Digits 1,2,3,4,6,7 mein even digits hain 2,4,6. Har case mein (units digit 2,4,6) hundreds place ke liye 5 options, tens place ke liye 4 options. Total = 20+20+20=60.

Question 4: Find the number of 4-digit numbers that can be formed using the digits 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 if no digit is repeated. How many of these will be even?

Solution:

Part 2: Even numbers

Case 1: Units digit is 2

Then thousands, hundreds, tens places can be filled from remaining 4 digits in 4 × 3 × 2 = 24 ways

Case 2: Units digit is 4

Total even numbers = 24 + 24 = 48

Hinglish Explanation: Total 4-digit numbers: digits 1-5 se banenge, no repetition: 5P4 = 5×4×3×2=120. Even numbers ke liye units digit 2 ya 4 hona chahiye. Har case mein bache 4 digits se 3 places fill karne ke 4×3×2=24 ways. Total even numbers = 24+24=48.

Question 6: Find n if n-1P3 : nP4 = 1 : 9

Solution:

Given: n-1P3 : nP4 = 1 : 9

So, n-1P3 nP4 = 1 9

(n-1)!/(n-1-3)! n!/(n-4)! = 1 9

(n-1)!/(n-4)! n!/(n-4)! = 1 9

Cancelling (n-4)! from numerator and denominator:

(n-1)! n! = 1 9

1 n = 1 9

So, n = 9

Answer: n = 9

Hinglish Explanation: Ratio diya hua hai: n-1P3 : nP4 = 1:9. Permutations ke formulae use karke equation banate hain aur solve karte hain. Finally n=9 milta hai.

Question 7: Find r if
(i) 5Pr = 2 6Pr-1
(ii) 5Pr = 6Pr-1

Solution:

(i) 5Pr = 2 6Pr-1

5! (5-r)! = 2 × 6! (6-(r-1))!

5! (5-r)! = 2 × 6! (7-r)!

1 (5-r)! = 2 × 6 × 5! (7-r)(6-r)(5-r)!

Cancelling 5! and (5-r)!:

1 = 2 × 6 (7-r)(6-r)

(7-r)(6-r) = 12

r² - 13r + 42 = 12

r² - 13r + 30 = 0

(r-3)(r-10) = 0

r = 3 or r = 10

But r cannot be 10 (since we're taking 5Pr and r ≤ 5)

So, r = 3

(ii) 5Pr = 6Pr-1

5! (5-r)! = 6! (7-r)!

1 (5-r)! = 6 × 5! (7-r)(6-r)(5-r)!

Cancelling 5! and (5-r)!:

1 = 6 (7-r)(6-r)

(7-r)(6-r) = 6

r² - 13r + 42 = 6

r² - 13r + 36 = 0

(r-4)(r-9) = 0

r = 4 or r = 9

But r cannot be 9 (since we're taking 5Pr and r ≤ 5)

So, r = 4

Answer: (i) r = 3, (ii) r = 4

Hinglish Explanation: Dono parts mein permutations ke formulae use karke equations banate hain aur solve karte hain. (i) mein r=3 aur (ii) mein r=4 milta hai, kyunki r maximum 5 ho sakta hai (5Pr ke liye).

Question 9: How many words, with or without meaning can be made from the letters of the word MONDAY, assuming that no letter is repeated, if.

Question 10: In how many of the distinct permutations of the letters in MISSISSIPPI do the four I's not come together?

Solution:

Word: MISSISSIPPI

Total letters: 11

M: 1, I: 4, S: 4, P: 2

Total distinct permutations = 11! 4! × 4! × 2! = 34650

Now, permutations where all I's are together:

Treat all I's as a single object: IIII

Now we have: M, (IIII), S, S, S, S, P, P (8 objects)

But S appears 4 times, P appears 2 times

Permutations = 8! 4! × 2! = 840

Permutations where I's are not together = Total - (I's together)

= 34650 - 840 = 33810

Answer: 33810

Hinglish Explanation: MISSISSIPPI ke total distinct permutations 34650 hain. Jab sab I's saath honge, toh unhe ek object maan kar permutations 840 hain. Isliye I's ke saath nahi hone wale permutations = 34650-840=33810.

Question 11: In how many ways can the letters of the word PERMUTATIONS be arranged if the
(i) words start with P and end with S
(ii) vowels are all together
(iii) there are always 4 letters between P and S?

Solution:

Word: PERMUTATIONS

Total letters: 12

Letters: P, E, R, M, U, T, A, T, I, O, N, S

T appears 2 times, rest are unique

Total distinct arrangements = 12! 2!

(i) Words start with P and end with S:

Fix P at start and S at end.

Remaining 10 letters to arrange: E, R, M, U, T, A, T, I, O, N

T appears 2 times

Number of arrangements = 10! 2!

(ii) Vowels are all together:

Vowels: E, U, A, I, O (5 vowels)

Treat vowels as a single object

Now we have: (vowels), P, R, M, T, T, N, S (8 objects, with T repeated twice)

Arrangements of these 8 objects = 8! 2!

Vowels can be arranged among themselves in 5! ways

Total arrangements = 8! 2! × 5!

(iii) There are always 4 letters between P and S:

P and S can be arranged in 2 ways: P followed by S or S followed by P

When there are 4 letters between P and S, they occupy positions like (1,6), (2,7), ..., (7,12) - total 7 such pairs

For each such pair, remaining 10 letters can be arranged in 10! 2! ways (T appears twice)

Total arrangements = 2 × 7 × 10! 2!

Answer: (i) 10!/2!, (ii) (8!/2!) × 5!, (iii) 2 × 7 × (10!/2!)

Hinglish Explanation: (i) P start aur S end fixed hain, bache 10 letters arrange karne hain (T 2 baar). (ii) Sab vowels ko ek object maan kar arrangements, fir vowels ko apas mein arrange. (iii) P aur S ke beech 4 letters hone chahiye, 7 positions possible, aur P aur S ke order ke 2 ways.

Question 1: If nC8 = nC2, find nC2

Solution:

Given: nC8 = nC2

We know that nCr = nCn-r

So, if nC8 = nC2, then either:

8 = 2 or 8 = n - 2

8 = 2 is not possible, so 8 = n - 2

n = 10

Now, nC2 = 10C2 = 10! 2! × 8! = 10 × 9 2 = 45

Answer: 45

Hinglish Explanation: nC8 = nC2 se ya toh 8=2 (possible nahi) ya 8=n-2, isliye n=10. 10C2 = 45.

Question 2: Determine n if
(i) 2nC3 : nC3 = 12 : 1
(ii) 2nC3 : nC3 = 11 : 1

Solution:

(i) 2nC3 : nC3 = 12 : 1

2nC3 nC3 = 12 1

(2n)!/(3!(2n-3)!) n!/(3!(n-3)!) = 12

(2n)!/(2n-3)! n!/(n-3)! = 12

2n(2n-1)(2n-2) n(n-1)(n-2) = 12

2 × 2n(2n-1)(n-1) n(n-1)(n-2) = 12

4(2n-1) (n-2) = 12

4(2n-1) = 12(n-2)

8n - 4 = 12n - 24

4n = 20

n = 5

(ii) 2nC3 : nC3 = 11 : 1

Similarly,

4(2n-1) (n-2) = 11

4(2n-1) = 11(n-2)

8n - 4 = 11n - 22

3n = 18

n = 6

Answer: (i) n = 5, (ii) n = 6

Hinglish Explanation: Dono parts mein combination ratios diye hain. Combinations ke formulae use karke equations banate hain aur solve karte hain. (i) mein n=5 aur (ii) mein n=6 milta hai.
