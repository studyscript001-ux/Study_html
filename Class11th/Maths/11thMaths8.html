r - 1/r³1/r - r³ = 12

Cross-multiplying: 2(r - 1/r³) = 1/r - r³

2r - 2/r³ = 1/r - r³

Multiply by r³: 2r⁴ - 2 = r² - r⁶

r⁶ + 2r⁴ - r² - 2 = 0

(r² + 2)(r⁴ - 1) = 0 ⇒ r⁴ = 1 ⇒ r = ±1

But r = 1 gives all terms equal, which doesn't satisfy the conditions

So, r = -1

From (1): a(-1) - a/(-1) = 9 ⇒ -a + a = 9 ⇒ 0 = 9 (contradiction)

Let's try the standard form: a, ar, ar², ar³

Second term - fourth term = 18: ar - ar³ = 18 ⇒ ar(1 - r²) = 18 ...(2)

Dividing (2) by (1): ar(1 - r²)a(r² - 1) = 189

-r1 = 2 ⇒ r = -2

The four numbers: 3, -6, 12, -24

Answer: The four numbers are 3, -6, 12, -24

Hinglish Explanation: Four terms ko standard form mein lete hain aur given conditions use karke equations banate hain. Unhe solve karke a aur r nikalte hain.
