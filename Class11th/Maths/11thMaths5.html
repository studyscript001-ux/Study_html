Question 1: Solve 24x < 100, when
(i) x is a natural number
(ii) x is an integer

Solution:

We have: 24x < 100

Dividing both sides by 24: x < 100 24 = 25 6 ≈ 4.1667

(i) When x is a natural number:

Natural numbers less than 4.1667 are: 1, 2, 3, 4

Solution set: {1, 2, 3, 4}

(ii) When x is an integer:

Integers less than 4.1667 are: ..., -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, 4

Solution set: {..., -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, 4}

Answer: (i) {1, 2, 3, 4}; (ii) {..., -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, 4}

Hinglish Explanation: Pehle inequality solve karte hain: 24x < 100 ⇒ x < 25/6 ≈ 4.1667. (i) Natural numbers ke liye: 1, 2, 3, 4. (ii) Integers ke liye: ...-3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, 4.

Question 2: Solve -12x > 30, when

(ii) x is an integer

(ii) When x is an integer:

Integers less than -2.5 are: ..., -6, -5, -4, -3

Solution set: {..., -6, -5, -4, -3}

Answer: (i) ∅; (ii) {..., -6, -5, -4, -3}

Hinglish Explanation: Inequality solve karte hain: -12x > 30 ⇒ x < -2.5 (negative se divide karne par sign change). (i) Natural numbers mein koi number -2.5 se chhota nahi hai, isliye empty set. (ii) Integers ke liye: ...-6, -5, -4, -3.

Question 11: Solve 3(x - 2) 5 ≤ 5(2 - x) 3 for real x

9(x - 2) ≤ 25(2 - x)

Adding 25x to both sides: 34x - 18 ≤ 50

Dividing both sides by 34: x ≤ 2

Solution set: (-∞, 2]

Answer: (-∞, 2]

Question 12: Solve 1 2 ( 3x 5 + 4 ) ≥ 1 3 (x - 6) for real x

-x 30 + 2 ≥ -2

Multiplying both sides by 30: -x ≥ -120

Multiplying both sides by -1 (sign reverses): x ≤ 120

Question 21: Ravi obtained 70 and 75 marks in first two unit test. Find the minimum marks he should get in the third test to have an average of at least 60 marks.

Solution:

Let x be the marks obtained in the third test.

Average of three tests = 70 + 75 + x 3

According to the problem:

70 + 75 + x 3 ≥ 60

145 + x ≥ 180

x ≥ 180 - 145

x ≥ 35

Minimum marks required in third test = 35

Answer: 35

Hinglish Explanation: Maan lete hain third test mein x marks milte hain. Average = (70+75+x)/3 ≥ 60 ⇒ 145+x ≥ 180 ⇒ x ≥ 35. Isliye third test mein kam se kam 35 marks chahiye.

Question 23: Find all pairs of consecutive odd positive integers both of which are smaller than 10 such that their sum is more than 11.

Solution:

Let the consecutive odd positive integers be x and x + 2

Given conditions:

x + 2 < 10 ⇒ x < 8 ...(1)

x + (x + 2) > 11 ⇒ 2x + 2 > 11 ⇒ 2x > 9 ⇒ x > 4.5 ...(2)

x is an odd positive integer ...(3)

From (1), (2) and (3): x = 5, 7

Possible pairs: (5, 7) and (7, 9)

Check: (5, 7): 5 + 7 = 12 > 11 ✓

(7, 9): 7 + 9 = 16 > 11 ✓

Required pairs: (5, 7) and (7, 9)

Answer: (5, 7) and (7, 9)

Hinglish Explanation: Maan lete hain consecutive odd positive integers x aur x+2 hain. Conditions: x+2 < 10 ⇒ x < 8, aur x+(x+2) > 11 ⇒ 2x+2 > 11 ⇒ x > 4.5. x odd positive integer hai, isliye x = 5, 7. Possible pairs: (5,7) aur (7,9).

Question 25: The longest side of a triangle is 3 times the shortest side and the third side is 2 cm shorter than the longest side. If the perimeter of the triangle is at least 61 cm, find the minimum length of the shortest side.

Solution:

Let the shortest side be x cm

Then longest side = 3x cm

Third side = 3x - 2 cm

Perimeter = x + 3x + (3x - 2) = 7x - 2

Given: Perimeter ≥ 61

7x - 2 ≥ 61

7x ≥ 63

x ≥ 9

Minimum length of shortest side = 9 cm

Answer: 9 cm

Hinglish Explanation: Maan lete hain shortest side x cm hai. Longest side = 3x cm, third side = 3x-2 cm. Perimeter = x+3x+(3x-2) = 7x-2. Perimeter ≥ 61 ⇒ 7x-2 ≥ 61 ⇒ 7x ≥ 63 ⇒ x ≥ 9. Isliye shortest side ki minimum length 9 cm hai.

Question 26: A man wants to cut three lengths from a single piece of board of length 91cm. The second length is to be 3cm longer than the shortest and the third length is to be twice as long as the shortest. What are the possible lengths of the shortest board if the third piece is to be at least 5cm longer than the second?

Solution:

Let the shortest length be x cm

Then second length = x + 3 cm

Third length = 2x cm

Total length = x + (x + 3) + 2x = 4x + 3

Given: Total length ≤ 91

4x + 3 ≤ 91

4x ≤ 88

x ≤ 22 ...(1)

Also, third piece ≥ second piece + 5

2x ≥ (x + 3) + 5

2x ≥ x + 8

x ≥ 8 ...(2)

From (1) and (2): 8 ≤ x ≤ 22

Possible lengths of shortest board: from 8 cm to 22 cm

Answer: 8 cm to 22 cm

Hinglish Explanation: Maan lete hain shortest length x cm hai. Second length = x+3 cm, third length = 2x cm. Total length = x+(x+3)+2x = 4x+3 ≤ 91 ⇒ 4x ≤ 88 ⇒ x ≤ 22. Third piece ≥ second piece+5 ⇒ 2x ≥ (x+3)+5 ⇒ 2x ≥ x+8 ⇒ x ≥ 8. Isliye shortest board ki length 8 cm se 22 cm tak ho sakti hai.
