Question 1

Explain the importance of reproduction in organisms.

Reproduction is essential for the continuation of a species. It ensures that similar kinds of individuals continue to exist, generation after generation. Without reproduction, organisms would not be able to survive as a species.

Question 2

Describe the process of fertilisation in human beings.

Fertilisation in human beings is the fusion of a sperm and an ovum (egg). In this process, when sperms come into contact with an egg, one of the sperms may fuse with the egg. The nuclei of the sperm and the egg fuse to form a single nucleus, which results in the formation of a fertilised egg or zygote. This process is known as internal fertilisation because it takes place inside the female body. The zygote then develops into a new individual, inheriting characteristics from both the mother and the father.

Question 5

Give two differences between a zygote and a foetus.

Zygote: A zygote is a single cell formed by the fusion of a male gamete (sperm) and a female gamete (ovum). It is the very first stage of development of a new individual.

Foetus: A foetus is a later stage of the embryo in which all the major body parts, such as hands, legs, head, and eyes, can be identified. It develops from the repeatedly dividing zygote.

Question 6

Define asexual reproduction. Describe two methods of asexual reproduction in animals.

Asexual reproduction is a type of reproduction that involves only a single parent.

Two methods of asexual reproduction in animals are:

Budding: In this method, new individuals develop as outgrowths, or "buds," from a single parent. This is seen in organisms like hydra.

Binary fission: In this method, a single-celled organism, like an amoeba, begins the process of reproduction by its nucleus dividing into two. This is followed by the division of its body into two parts, with each part receiving a nucleus. Finally, two new individuals are produced from one parent amoeba.

Question 7

In which female reproductive organ does the embryo get embedded?

The embryo gets embedded in the uterus wall for further development.

Question 8

What is metamorphosis? Give examples.

Metamorphosis is the transformation of a larva into an adult through drastic changes. An example of this is the life cycle of a frog, where a tadpole (larva) undergoes significant changes to become an adult frog.
