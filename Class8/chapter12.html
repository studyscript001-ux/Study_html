Exercise Questions

1. Which of the following cannot be charged easily by friction?
(a) A plastic scale
(b) A copper rod
(c) An inflated balloon
(d) A woollen cloth
Explanation: Metals like copper are good conductors of electricity. When we try to charge them by friction, the charges immediately flow to the ground through our body. Therefore, they cannot be charged easily by friction.
2. When a glass rod is rubbed with a piece of silk cloth the rod
(a) and the cloth both acquire positive charge
(b) becomes positively charged while the cloth has a negative charge
(c) and the cloth both acquire negative charge
(d) becomes negatively charged while the cloth has a positive charge
Explanation: When a glass rod is rubbed with silk, the glass rod loses electrons and becomes positively charged, while the silk cloth gains electrons and becomes negatively charged.
3. Write T against true and F against false in the following statements.
(a) Like charges attract each other (F)
(b) A charged glass rod attracts a charged plastic straw (T)
(c) Lightning conductor cannot protect a building from lightning (F)
(d) Earthquakes can be predicted in advance (F)
Explanation:
  • (a) Like charges repel each other, unlike charges attract
  • (b) A charged glass rod (positive) attracts a charged plastic straw (negative)
  • (c) Lightning conductors provide a safe path for lightning to reach the ground, protecting buildings
  • (d) Earthquakes cannot be predicted accurately in advance with current technology
4. Sometimes, a crackling sound is heard while taking off a sweater during winters. Explain.
The crackling sound is produced due to the discharge of static electricity. When we take off a sweater (especially woolen or synthetic), friction between the sweater and our body or other clothes causes the transfer of electrons, leading to the buildup of static charges. When these charges accumulate and then discharge, they create tiny sparks that produce the crackling sound.
5. Explain why a charged body loses its charge if we touch it with our hand.
When we touch a charged body with our hand, the electric charges transfer from the charged body to our body. Since our body is a conductor of electricity and is connected to the ground (earth), the charges flow through our body to the ground. This process is called earthing, and it causes the charged body to lose its charge.
6. Name the scale on which the destructive energy of an earthquake is measured. An earthquake measures 3 on this scale. Would it be recorded by a seismograph? Is it likely to cause much damage?
The destructive energy of an earthquake is measured on the Richter scale.

An earthquake measuring 3 on the Richter scale would be recorded by a seismograph, but it is considered a minor earthquake that is often felt by people but typically causes little to no damage. Earthquakes below magnitude 4.0 are generally not destructive.
7. Suggest three measures to protect ourselves from lightning.
Three measures to protect ourselves from lightning:
  1. Seek shelter inside a building or vehicle during a thunderstorm
  2. Avoid taking shelter under isolated tall trees, and instead squat low on the ground with hands on knees if caught in an open area
  3. Avoid contact with electrical appliances, wired phones, and plumbing during a thunderstorm
Additional measures include unplugging electronic devices and staying away from windows and doors.
8. Explain why a charged balloon is repelled by another charged balloon whereas an uncharged balloon is attracted by another charged balloon?
When both balloons are charged with the same type of charge (usually negative when rubbed with wool), they repel each other because like charges repel.

An uncharged balloon is attracted to a charged balloon because the charged balloon induces opposite charges on the side of the uncharged balloon nearest to it. The attractive force between these opposite charges is stronger than the repulsive force between the similar charges on the far side.
9. Describe with the help of a diagram an instrument which can be used to detect a charged body.
An electroscope is used to detect whether a body is charged or not. It consists of:
  • A metal rod with a disc or ball at the top
  • Two thin leaves of gold or aluminum attached to the bottom of the rod
  • The rod is inserted in a flask with the leaves inside and the disc outside

When a charged body is brought near the disc, charge is transferred through the rod to the leaves. Since both leaves receive the same charge, they repel each other and diverge. The extent of divergence indicates the amount of charge.
10. List three states in India where earthquakes are more likely to strike.
Three states in India where earthquakes are more likely to strike:
  1. Jammu and Kashmir
  2. Gujarat
  3. Uttarakhand (and other Himalayan states)
Other earthquake-prone regions include the entire Northeast India, Himachal Pradesh, and the Rann of Kutch region.
11. Suppose you are outside your home and an earthquake strikes. What precaution would you take to protect yourself?
If I'm outside during an earthquake, I would:
  1. Move to an open area away from buildings, trees, streetlights, and utility wires
  2. Drop to the ground and take cover by crouching low
  3. If in a vehicle, stop in a clear area and stay inside until shaking stops
  4. Protect my head and neck with my arms
  5. Stay away from bridges, overpasses, and other structures that might collapse
  6. Be prepared for aftershocks
12. The weather department has predicted that a thunderstorm is likely to occur on a certain day. Suppose you have to go out on that day. Would you carry an umbrella? Explain.
No, I would not carry an umbrella if a thunderstorm is predicted. Here's why:
  • An umbrella with a metal shaft can act as a lightning conductor, increasing the risk of being struck by lightning
  • Holding an umbrella makes me the tallest object in an open area, which is dangerous during lightning
  • It's safer to wear a raincoat or waterproof jacket instead
  • If possible, I would postpone my trip or find indoor shelter until the storm passes
Safety during a thunderstorm involves avoiding being the highest point in an area and staying away from metal objects.
Safety Note: Always follow safety guidelines during natural phenomena like thunderstorms and earthquakes. Stay informed about weather alerts and have an emergency plan ready.
