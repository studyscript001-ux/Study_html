Question 1

What are the advantages of using CNG and LPG as fuels?

Using CNG (Compressed Natural Gas) and LPG (Liquefied Petroleum Gas) as fuels has several advantages:

• They are less polluting than other fossil fuels like petrol and diesel.

• They are cleaner fuels.

• CNG can be supplied directly to homes and factories through a network of pipes, making it convenient to use.

Question 2

Name the petroleum product used for surfacing of roads.

Bitumen is the petroleum product used for surfacing roads. It is also used in paints.

Question 3

Describe how coal is formed from dead vegetation. What is this process called?

How coal is formed: About 300 million years ago, Earth had dense forests in low-lying wetland areas. These forests were buried under the soil due to natural processes like flooding. As more soil piled up, they were compressed and the temperature also increased as they sank deeper. Under high pressure and high temperature, the dead plants slowly turned into coal.

The process name: Since coal is mainly composed of carbon, this slow process of converting dead vegetation into coal is called carbonisation.

Question 6

Explain why fossil fuels are exhaustible natural resources.

Fossil fuels are considered exhaustible natural resources because their amount in nature is limited and they can be depleted by human activities. They were formed over millions of years from the remains of dead organisms, and their known reserves are only expected to last for a few hundred years. Since they are finite and take a very long time to form, they cannot be replaced once they are used up.

Question 7

Describe characteristics and uses of coke.

Characteristics: Coke is a tough, porous, and black substance. It is an almost pure form of carbon.

Uses: Coke is used in the manufacturing of steel and in the extraction of many metals.

Question 8

Explain the process of formation of petroleum.

Petroleum was formed from organisms that lived in the sea. When these organisms died, their bodies sank to the bottom of the sea and were covered by layers of sand and clay. Over millions of years, the absence of air, along with high temperature and high pressure, transformed these dead organisms into petroleum and natural gas.
