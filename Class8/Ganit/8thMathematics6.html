Exercise 6.1

Question 1: Which of the following numbers are not perfect cubes?
(i) 216 (ii) 128 (iii) 1000 (iv) 100 (v) 46656

Solution:

(i) 216

Prime factorization of 216:

216 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 3 × 3 × 3 = 2³ × 3³

All prime factors are in groups of three.

Answer: 216 is a perfect cube.

Hinglish Explanation: 216 ko prime factors mein break karo, sabhi factors triple groups mein hain, isliye yeh perfect cube hai.

(ii) 128

Prime factorization of 128:

128 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 = 2⁷

2 appears seven times, which is not in groups of three (7 ÷ 3 = 2 remainder 1).

Answer: 128 is not a perfect cube.

Hinglish Explanation: 128 ke prime factors mein 2 seven times aa raha hai, jo triple groups mein nahi hai, isliye perfect cube nahi hai.

(iii) 1000

Prime factorization of 1000:

1000 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 5 × 5 × 5 = 2³ × 5³

All prime factors are in groups of three.

Answer: 1000 is a perfect cube.

Hinglish Explanation: 1000 ke prime factors triple groups mein hain, isliye perfect cube hai.

(iv) 100

Prime factorization of 100:

100 = 2 × 2 × 5 × 5 = 2² × 5²

Both 2 and 5 appear twice, which is not in groups of three.

Answer: 100 is not a perfect cube.

Hinglish Explanation: 100 ke prime factors double groups mein hain, triple groups mein nahi, isliye perfect cube nahi hai.

(v) 46656

Prime factorization of 46656:

46656 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 3 × 3 × 3 × 3 × 3 × 3 = 2⁶ × 3⁶

All prime factors are in groups of three (6 ÷ 3 = 2).

Answer: 46656 is a perfect cube.

Hinglish Explanation: 46656 ke prime factors triple groups mein hain, isliye perfect cube hai.

Question 2: Find the smallest number by which each of the following numbers must be multiplied to obtain a perfect cube:
(i) 243 (ii) 256 (iii) 72 (iv) 675 (v) 100

Solution:

(i) 243

Prime factorization of 243:

243 = 3 × 3 × 3 × 3 × 3 = 3⁵

3 appears five times. To make groups of three, we need one more 3 (5 + 1 = 6, 6 ÷ 3 = 2).

Answer: Must multiply by 3.

Hinglish Explanation: 243 ke prime factors mein 3 five times hai, triple groups banane ke liye ek aur 3 ki jaroorat hai.

(ii) 256

Prime factorization of 256:

256 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 = 2⁸

2 appears eight times. To make groups of three, we need one more 2 (8 + 1 = 9, 9 ÷ 3 = 3).

Answer: Must multiply by 2.

Hinglish Explanation: 256 ke prime factors mein 2 eight times hai, triple groups banane ke liye ek aur 2 ki jaroorat hai.

(iii) 72

Prime factorization of 72:

72 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 3 × 3 = 2³ × 3²

2 appears three times (okay), but 3 appears twice. To make groups of three, we need one more 3.

Answer: Must multiply by 3.

Hinglish Explanation: 72 mein 2 triple group mein hai, lekin 3 double group mein hai, triple group banane ke liye ek aur 3 ki jaroorat hai.

(iv) 675

Prime factorization of 675:

675 = 3 × 3 × 3 × 5 × 5 = 3³ × 5²

3 appears three times (okay), but 5 appears twice. To make groups of three, we need one more 5.

Answer: Must multiply by 5.

Hinglish Explanation: 675 mein 3 triple group mein hai, lekin 5 double group mein hai, triple group banane ke liye ek aur 5 ki jaroorat hai.

(v) 100

Prime factorization of 100:

100 = 2 × 2 × 5 × 5 = 2² × 5²

Both 2 and 5 appear twice. To make groups of three, we need one more 2 and one more 5.

Answer: Must multiply by 2 × 5 = 10.

Hinglish Explanation: 100 ke prime factors mein dono 2 aur 5 double groups mein hain, triple groups banane ke liye ek 2 aur ek 5 ki jaroorat hai.

Question 4: Parikshit makes a cuboid of plasticine with sides 5 cm, 2 cm, and 5 cm. How many such cuboids will he need to form a cube?

Solution:

Volume of cuboid = length × width × height

Volume of cuboid = 5 cm × 2 cm × 5 cm = 50 cm³

To form a cube, we need a perfect cube number.

Prime factorization of 50:

50 = 2 × 5 × 5

To make it a perfect cube, we need 2 × 2 × 5 = 20 cuboids.

Total volume = 50 × 20 = 1000 cm³

Side of cube = ∛1000 = 10 cm

Answer: 20 cuboids will be needed.

Hinglish Explanation: Ek cuboid ka volume 50 cm³ hai. Perfect cube banane ke liye humein 1000 cm³ volume chahiye (kyunki ∛1000 = 10). Isliye 1000/50 = 20 cuboids ki jaroorat hai.

Question 2: State true or false:

Solution:

(i) Cube of any odd number is even.

Odd × odd = odd
Odd × odd × odd = odd

Answer: False

Hinglish Explanation: Odd number ka cube hamesha odd hi hota hai, even nahi.

(ii) A perfect cube does not end with two zeros.

For a perfect cube, the number of zeros must be a multiple of 3.

Answer: True

Hinglish Explanation: Perfect cube ke end mein zeros ki sankhya 3 ke multiple mein honi chahiye, do zeros nahi ho sakte.

(iii) If square of a number ends with 5, then its cube ends with 25.

For example: 5² = 25 (ends with 5), 5³ = 125 (ends with 25)
15² = 225 (ends with 5), 15³ = 3375 (ends with 75)

Answer: False

Hinglish Explanation: Square 5 par khatam ho toh cube 25 par khatam hona zaroori nahi hai, jaise 15 ka cube 3375 hai jo 75 par khatam hota hai.

(iv) There is no perfect cube which ends with 8.

2³ = 8, 12³ = 1728, 22³ = 10648 (all end with 8)

Answer: False

Hinglish Explanation: Perfect cube 8 par khatam ho sakta hai, jaise 2, 12, 22 ke cubes.

(v) The cube of a two-digit number may be a three-digit number.

4³ = 64 (two digits), 5³ = 125 (three digits)

Answer: True

Hinglish Explanation: Do-digit number ka cube teen-digit number ho sakta hai, jaise 5 ka cube 125 hai.

(vi) The cube of a two-digit number may have seven or more digits.

99³ = 970299 (six digits), 100³ = 1000000 (seven digits) but 100 is a three-digit number.

Answer: False

Hinglish Explanation: Do-digit number (99) ka cube sirf 6 digits ka hota hai, 7 digits ka nahi.

(vii) The cube of a single-digit number may be a single-digit number.

1³ = 1, 2³ = 8 (both are single-digit numbers)

Answer: True

Hinglish Explanation: Single-digit number ka cube single-digit number ho sakta hai, jaise 1 aur 2 ke cubes.
