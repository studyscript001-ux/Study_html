Exercise 11.1

Question 1: The parking charges of a car at a railway station are as follows:

Up to 4 hours - ₹60, Up to 8 hours - ₹100, Up to 12 hours - ₹140, Up to 24 hours - ₹180

Check if the parking charges are in direct proportion to the parking time.

Solution:

Let's find the ratio of parking time and charges:

For 4 hours: 60 4 = 15

For 8 hours: 100 8 = 12.5

For 12 hours: 140 12 = 11.67

For 24 hours: 180 24 = 7.5

Since all the ratios are not equal, the parking charges are not in direct proportion to the parking time.

Answer: No, the parking charges are not in direct proportion to the parking time.

Hinglish Explanation: Yahan humne dekha ki parking ka time badhne par charges ka ratio same nahi hai. Isliye ye direct proportion mein nahi hai.

Question 2: A mixture of paint is prepared by mixing 1 part of red pigment with 8 parts of base. In the following table, find the parts of base that need to be added:

Parts of red pigment: 1, 4, 7, 12, 20

Solution:

Ratio of red pigment to base = 1:8

For 1 part of red pigment, base required = 8 parts

For 4 parts of red pigment, base required = 4 × 8 = 32 parts

For 7 parts of red pigment, base required = 7 × 8 = 56 parts

For 12 parts of red pigment, base required = 12 × 8 = 96 parts

For 20 parts of red pigment, base required = 20 × 8 = 160 parts

Answer: For 1, 4, 7, 12, 20 parts of red pigment, 8, 32, 56, 96, 160 parts of base are required respectively.

Hinglish Explanation: Yahan red pigment aur base ka ratio 1:8 hai. Toh jab red pigment badhta hai, tab base bhi same ratio mein badhna chahiye. Isliye humne multiply karke nikal liya.

Question 3: In Question 2, if 1 part of red pigment requires 75 mL of base, how much red pigment should we add to 1800 mL of base?

Solution:

Ratio of red pigment to base = 1:8

For 1 part of red pigment, base required = 8 parts = 75 mL

So 1 part = 75 8 = 9.375 mL

Red pigment in 1800 mL base = 1800 9.375 = 192 parts

Alternative method: Let required red pigment = x parts

Then, 1 8 = x 1800

x = 1800 8 = 225 mL

Answer: 225 mL of red pigment should be added to 1800 mL of base.

Hinglish Explanation: Humne ratio use kiya. 1 part red pigment ke liye 8 parts base chahiye. Toh 1800 mL base ke liye, humne cross multiply karke x nikal liya.

Question 4: A machine in a soft drink factory fills 840 bottles in 6 hours. How many bottles will it fill in 5 hours?

Solution:

Bottles filled in 6 hours = 840

Bottles filled in 1 hour = 840 6 = 140

Bottles filled in 5 hours = 140 × 5 = 700

Answer: It will fill 700 bottles in 5 hours.

Hinglish Explanation: Pehle humne 1 hour mein kitni bottles fill hoti hai ye nikal liya. Phir usse 5 se multiply kar diya.

Question 5: A photograph of a bacteria is enlarged 50,000 times and its length becomes 5 cm. What is the actual length of the bacteria? If the photograph is enlarged only 20,000 times, what would be its enlarged length?

Solution:

Enlarged length = 5 cm, Enlargement = 50,000 times

Actual length = 5 50000 = 0.0001 cm = 1 × 10-4 cm

Length at 20,000 times enlargement = 0.0001 × 20000 = 2 cm

Answer: The actual length of bacteria is 0.0001 cm and at 20,000 times enlargement, the length would be 2 cm.

Hinglish Explanation: Actual length nikalne ke liye humne enlarged length ko enlargement factor se divide kiya. Dusra part mein humne actual length ko 20,000 se multiply kiya.

Question 6: In a model of a ship, the mast is 9 cm high, while the mast of the actual ship is 12 m high. If the length of the ship is 28 m, how long is the model ship?

Solution:

Ratio of mast height = Model : Actual = 9 cm : 12 m = 9 cm : 1200 cm = 9:1200 = 3:400

Model length Actual length = 3 400

Model length = 3 400 × 28 m = 3 400 × 2800 cm = 21 cm

Answer: The length of the model ship is 21 cm.

Hinglish Explanation: Humne mast height ka ratio nikala. Phir same ratio ko ship ki length par apply kiya. Model ki length nikalne ke liye actual length ko ratio se multiply kiya.

Question 7: Suppose 2 kg of sugar contains 9 × 106 crystals. How many sugar crystals are there in (i) 5 kg (ii) 1.2 kg of sugar?

Solution:

Crystals in 2 kg sugar = 9 × 106

Crystals in 1 kg sugar = 9 × 106 2 = 4.5 × 106

(i) Crystals in 5 kg sugar = 5 × 4.5 × 106 = 22.5 × 106 = 2.25 × 107

(ii) Crystals in 1.2 kg sugar = 1.2 × 4.5 × 106 = 5.4 × 106

Answer: (i) 2.25 × 107 crystals in 5 kg sugar, (ii) 5.4 × 106 crystals in 1.2 kg sugar

Hinglish Explanation: Pehle humne 1 kg sugar mein kitne crystals hai ye nikal liya. Phir usse 5 aur 1.2 se multiply kar diya.

Question 8: Rashmi has a road map with a scale of 1 cm representing 18 km. She drives 72 km on the road. What would be her distance on the map?

Solution:

Scale: 1 cm = 18 km

1 km = 1 18 cm

72 km = 72 × 1 18 = 4 cm

Answer: The distance on the map would be 4 cm.

Hinglish Explanation: Scale hai 1 cm = 18 km. Toh 72 km ko map par dikhane ke liye, humne 72 ko 18 se divide kiya.

Question 9: A 5 m 60 cm high vertical pole casts a shadow of length 3 m 20 cm. Find at the same time: (i) the length of the shadow cast by another pole 10 m 50 cm high (ii) the height of a pole which casts a shadow of length 5 m

Solution:

Pole height = 5 m 60 cm = 560 cm, Shadow = 3 m 20 cm = 320 cm

Ratio of height to shadow = 560 320 = 7 4

(i) Height of second pole = 10 m 50 cm = 1050 cm

Shadow = 4 7 × 1050 = 4 × 150 = 600 cm = 6 m

(ii) Shadow = 5 m = 500 cm

Height = 7 4 × 500 = 7 × 125 = 875 cm = 8 m 75 cm

Answer: (i) Shadow length is 6 m, (ii) Pole height is 8 m 75 cm

Hinglish Explanation: Humne pehle height aur shadow ka ratio nikala. Phir isi ratio ko dusre poles par apply kiya. Ratio same rahega kyunki same time hai.

Question 10: A loaded truck travels 14 km in 25 minutes. If the speed remains the same, how far can it travel in 5 hours?

Solution:

Distance in 25 minutes = 14 km

Distance in 1 minute = 14 25 km

5 hours = 5 × 60 = 300 minutes

Distance in 300 minutes = 300 × 14 25 = 12 × 14 = 168 km

Answer: It will travel 168 km in 5 hours.

Hinglish Explanation: Pehle humne 1 minute mein kitni distance cover karta hai ye nikal liya. Phir 5 hours ko minutes mein convert karke multiply kar diya.

Exercise 11.2

Question 1: Which of the following are in inverse proportion?

(i) The number of workers on a job and the time to complete the job.

(ii) The time taken for a journey with uniform speed and the distance travelled.

(iii) Area of cultivated land and the crop harvested.

(iv) The time taken for a fixed journey and the speed of the vehicle.

(v) The population of a country and the area of land per person.

Solution:

(i) Inverse proportion - Yes (more workers, less time)

(ii) Direct proportion - No (more time, more distance)

(iii) Direct proportion - No (more land, more crop)

(iv) Inverse proportion - Yes (more speed, less time)

(v) Inverse proportion - Yes (more population, less land per person)

Answer: (i), (iv) and (v) are in inverse proportion.

Hinglish Explanation: Inverse proportion mein ek cheez badhne par doosri cheez ghatti hai. Isliye (i), (iv) aur (v) inverse proportion mein hain.

Question 2: In a television game show, the prize money of ₹1,00,000 is to be divided equally among the winners. Complete the following table and find whether the prize money given to an individual winner is directly or inversely proportional to the number of winners?

Number of winners: 1, 2, 4, 5, 8, 10, 20

Solution:

Total prize money = ₹ 1,00,000

For 1 winner, prize = 1,00,000 1 = ₹ 1,00,000

For 2 winners, prize = 1,00,000 2 = ₹ 50,000

For 4 winners, prize = 1,00,000 4 = ₹ 25,000

For 5 winners, prize = 1,00,000 5 = ₹ 20,000

For 8 winners, prize = 1,00,000 8 = ₹ 12,500

For 10 winners, prize = 1,00,000 10 = ₹ 10,000

For 20 winners, prize = 1,00,000 20 = ₹ 5,000

As the number of winners increases, the prize money per winner decreases.

Answer: The prize money is inversely proportional to the number of winners.

Hinglish Explanation: Winners badhne par har winner ko milne wali prize money ghat rahi hai. Isliye ye inverse proportion hai.

Question 3: Rehman is making a wheel using spokes. He wants to fix equal spokes in such a way that the angles between any pair of consecutive spokes are equal. Help him by completing the following table:

Number of spokes: 4, 6, 8, 10, 12

Solution:

Angle of a complete circle = 360°

For 4 spokes, angle = 360 4 = 90°

For 6 spokes, angle = 360 6 = 60°

For 8 spokes, angle = 360 8 = 45°

For 10 spokes, angle = 360 10 = 36°

For 12 spokes, angle = 360 12 = 30°

(i) Yes, the number of spokes and the angle are inversely proportional.

(ii) For 15 spokes, angle = 360 15 = 24°

(iii) If angle is 40°, number of spokes = 360 40 = 9

Answer: (i) Yes, (ii) 24°, (iii) 9 spokes

Hinglish Explanation: Spokes badhne par angle between spokes ghat raha hai. Isliye ye inverse proportion hai. Angle nikalne ke liye 360° ko spokes ki sankhya se divide karte hain.

Question 4: If a box of sweets is divided among 24 children, each will get 5 sweets. How many would each get if the number of children is reduced by 4?

Solution:

Total sweets = 24 × 5 = 120

New number of children = 24 - 4 = 20

Sweets per child = 120 20 = 6

Answer: Each child will get 6 sweets.

Hinglish Explanation: Bachhon ki sankhya kam hone par har bachche ko zyada sweets milengi. Total sweets same hain, isliye humne total sweets ko bachhon ki sankhya se divide kiya.

Question 5: A farmer has enough food to feed 20 animals for 6 days. How long would the food last if there were 10 more animals?

Solution:

Total food = 20 × 6 = 120 animal-days

New number of animals = 20 + 10 = 30

Days food will last = 120 30 = 4

Answer: The food will last for 4 days.

Hinglish Explanation: Animals badhne par food kam din tak chalega. Total food same hai, isliye humne total food ko animals ki sankhya se divide kiya.

Question 6: A contractor estimates that 3 persons could complete the wiring in a house in 4 days. If he uses 4 persons instead of three, how long should they take to complete the job?

Solution:

Total work = 3 × 4 = 12 person-days

Time taken by 4 persons = 12 4 = 3 days

Answer: The work will be completed in 3 days.

Hinglish Explanation: Persons badhne par kaam jaldi ho jayega. Total work same hai, isliye humne total work ko persons ki sankhya se divide kiya.

Question 7: A batch of bottles is packed in 25 boxes with 12 bottles in each box. If the same batch is packed using 20 bottles in each box, how many boxes would be filled?

Solution:

Total bottles = 25 × 12 = 300

Bottles per box = 20

Number of boxes = 300 20 = 15

Answer: 15 boxes would be filled.

Hinglish Explanation: Ek box mein zyada bottles aane par kam boxes lagenge. Total bottles same hain, isliye humne total bottles ko bottles per box se divide kiya.

Question 8: A factory requires 42 machines to produce a given number of articles in 63 days. How many machines would be required to produce the same number of articles in 54 days?

Solution:

Total work = 42 × 63 = 2646 machine-days

Machines required in 54 days = 2646 54 = 49

Answer: 49 machines would be required.

Hinglish Explanation: Kam din mein same kaam karna hai toh zyada machines lagenge. Total work same hai, isliye humne total work ko days se divide kiya.

Question 9: A car takes 2 hours to reach a destination by travelling at 60 km/h. How long will it take when the car travels at 80 km/h?

Solution:

Total distance = 60 × 2 = 120 km

Time at 80 km/h = 120 80 = 1.5 hours = 1 hour 30 minutes

Answer: It will take 1 hour 30 minutes.

Hinglish Explanation: Speed badhne par time kam lagega. Distance same hai, isliye humne distance ko speed se divide kiya.

Question 10: Two persons could fit new windows in a house in 3 days.

(i) One of the persons fell ill before the work started. How long would the job take now?

(ii) How many persons would be needed to fit the windows in one day?

Solution:

Total work = 2 × 3 = 6 person-days

(i) Time taken by 1 person = 6 1 = 6 days

(ii) Persons needed in 1 day = 6 1 = 6 persons

Answer: (i) 6 days, (ii) 6 persons

Hinglish Explanation: Total work same hai. (i) Ek person kaam karega toh zyada time lagega. (ii) Ek din mein kaam khatam karna hai toh zyada persons lagenge.

Question 11: A school has 8 periods of 45 minutes each. Imagining that the school working hours remain the same, if the school has 9 periods of equal duration, how long would each period be?

Solution:

Total school time = 8 × 45 = 360 minutes

Duration of each period with 9 periods = 360 9 = 40 minutes

Answer: Each period would be 40 minutes long.

Hinglish Explanation: Periods badhne par har period ki duration ghat jayegi. Total school time same hai, isliye humne total time ko periods ki sankhya se divide kiya.
