Exercise 9.1

Question 1: The shape of the top surface of a table is a trapezium. Find its area if its parallel sides are 1 m and 1.2 m and the perpendicular distance between them is 0.8 m.

Solution:

Area of trapezium = 12 × (sum of parallel sides) × height

Given:

Parallel side 1, a = 1 m

Parallel side 2, b = 1.2 m

Height, h = 0.8 m

Area = 12 × (1 + 1.2) × 0.8

= 12 × 2.2 × 0.8

= 1.1 × 0.8

= 0.88 m²

Answer: The area of the table top is 0.88 m².

Hinglish Explanation: Yahan humne trapezium ka area formula use kiya hai. Trapezium ka area = 1/2 × (parallel sides ka sum) × height. Diye gaye values ko formula mein put karke humne answer nikal liya.

Question 2: The area of a trapezium is 34 cm² and the length of one of the parallel sides is 10 cm and its height is 4 cm. Find the length of the other parallel side.

Solution:

Area of trapezium = 12 × (a + b) × h

Given:

Area = 34 cm²

Height, h = 4 cm

One parallel side, a = 10 cm

Let the other parallel side = b

34 = 12 × (10 + b) × 4

34 = 2 × (10 + b)

34 = 20 + 2b

2b = 34 - 20

2b = 14

b = 7 cm

Answer: The length of the other parallel side is 7 cm.

Hinglish Explanation: Yahan humne trapezium ke area formula ko use karke equation solve ki hai. Area = 1/2 × (a + b) × h, isme values daal kar humne dusri parallel side nikal li.

Question 3: Length of the fence of a trapezium shaped field ABCD is 120 m. If BC = 48 m, CD = 17 m and AD = 40 m, find the area of this field. Side AB is perpendicular to the parallel sides AD and BC.

Solution:

Given:

Length of fence = 120 m

BC = 48 m, CD = 17 m, AD = 40 m

AB is perpendicular to AD and BC

Length of fence = AB + BC + CD + DA

120 = AB + 48 + 17 + 40

120 = AB + 105

AB = 120 - 105 = 15 m

Area of trapezium = 12 × (sum of parallel sides) × height

= 12 × (AD + BC) × AB

= 12 × (40 + 48) × 15

= 12 × 88 × 15

= 44 × 15 = 660 m²

Answer: The area of the field is 660 m².

Hinglish Explanation: Pehle humne perimeter formula se AB ki length nikal li. Phir trapezium ka area formula use kiya. AB height hai kyunki wo parallel sides AD aur BC ke perpendicular hai.

Question 4: The diagonal of a quadrilateral shaped field is 24 m and the perpendiculars dropped on it from the remaining opposite vertices are 8 m and 13 m. Find the area of the field.

Solution:

Area of quadrilateral = 12 × diagonal × (sum of perpendiculars)

Given:

Diagonal = 24 m

Perpendiculars = 8 m and 13 m

Area = 12 × 24 × (8 + 13)

= 12 × 21

= 252 m²

Answer: The area of the field is 252 m².

Hinglish Explanation: Quadrilateral ka area nikalne ka formula use kiya hai: 1/2 × diagonal × (perpendiculars ka sum). Values put karke answer mil gaya.

Question 5: The diagonals of a rhombus are 7.5 cm and 12 cm. Find its area.

Solution:

Area of rhombus = 12 × diagonal 1 × diagonal 2

Given:

Diagonal 1, d₁ = 7.5 cm

Diagonal 2, d₂ = 12 cm

Area = 12 × 7.5 × 12

= 7.5 × 6

= 45 cm²

Answer: The area of the rhombus is 45 cm².

Hinglish Explanation: Rhombus ka area formula hai: 1/2 × d₁ × d₂. Diye gaye diagonals ki values ko formula mein put karke area calculate kar liya.

Question 7: The floor of a building consists of 3000 tiles which are rhombus shaped and each of its diagonals are 45 cm and 30 cm in length. Find the total cost of polishing the floor if the cost per m² is ₹4.

Solution:

= 12 × 45 × 30

= 45 × 15 = 675 cm²

Total area of 3000 tiles = 3000 × 675 = 2,025,000 cm²

= 202.5 m² (∵ 1 m² = 10,000 cm²)

Cost of polishing = Area × Rate

= 202.5 × 4 = ₹810

Answer: The total cost of polishing the floor is ₹810.

Hinglish Explanation: Pehle ek tile ka area nikala, phir 3000 tiles ka total area. Area ko cm² se m² mein convert kiya (1 m² = 10,000 cm²). Phir cost nikalne ke liye area ko rate se multiply kiya.

Question 8: Mohan wants to buy a trapezium shaped field. Its side along the river is parallel to and twice the side along the road. If the area of this field is 10,500 m² and the perpendicular distance between the two parallel sides is 100 m, find the length of the side along the river.

Solution:

Then the side along the river = 2x m

10,500 = 12 × 3x × 100

10,500 = 150x

x = 10,500150 = 70 m

Side along the river = 2x = 2 × 70 = 140 m

Answer: The length of the side along the river is 140 m.

Hinglish Explanation: Road wali side ko x maan liya, river wali side 2x hogi. Trapezium ka area formula use karke equation solve ki aur x ki value nikal li. Phir river wali side 2x se calculate kar li.

Question 9: The top surface of a raised platform is in the shape of a regular octagon as shown in the figure. Find the area of the octagonal surface.

Solution:

The octagon can be divided into a square and four right-angled triangles.

Side of square = 5 m

Area of square = 5 × 5 = 25 m²

Each right triangle: base = 4 m, height = 3 m

Area of one triangle = 12 × 4 × 3 = 6 m²

Area of four triangles = 4 × 6 = 24 m²

Total area of octagon = Area of square + Area of four triangles

= 25 + 24 = 49 m²

Answer: The area of the octagonal surface is 49 m².

Hinglish Explanation: Octagon ko humne ek square aur 4 right-angled triangles mein divide kiya. Dono ke areas ko add karke total area nikal liya.

Question 1: There are two cuboidal boxes as shown in the adjoining figure. Which box requires the lesser amount of material to make?

Solution:

Surface area of cuboid = 2(lb + bh + hl)

First box: l = 60 cm, b = 40 cm, h = 50 cm

Surface area = 2(60×40 + 40×50 + 50×60)

= 2(2400 + 2000 + 3000)

= 2 × 7400 = 14800 cm²

Second box: l = 50 cm, b = 50 cm, h = 50 cm (cube)

Surface area = 6a² = 6 × 50² = 6 × 2500 = 15000 cm²

14800 cm² < 15000 cm²

Answer: The first box requires less material to make.

Hinglish Explanation: Dono boxes ka surface area calculate kiya. Jo box ka surface area kam hai, usko banane ke liye kam material chahiye.

Hinglish Explanation: Pehle ek suitcase ka surface area nikala, phir 100 suitcases ka total area. Cloth ki length nikalne ke liye total area ko cloth ki width se divide kiya. Length ko cm se m mein convert kiya.

Question 2: Cylinder A has a diameter of 7 cm and height 14 cm. Cylinder B has a diameter of 14 cm and height 7 cm. Without doing any calculations, can you suggest which one has greater volume? Verify by finding the volume of both cylinders. Check whether the cylinder with greater volume also has greater surface area.

Solution:

Volume of cylinder = πr²h

Cylinder A:

Radius, r = 72 = 3.5 cm

Height, h = 14 cm

Volume = 227 × 3.5² × 14

= 227 × 12.25 × 14

= 22 × 12.25 × 2 = 539 cm³

Cylinder B:

Radius, r = 142 = 7 cm

Height, h = 7 cm

Volume = 227 × 7² × 7

= 227 × 49 × 7

= 22 × 49 = 1078 cm³

1078 > 539

Answer: Cylinder B has greater volume.

Surface area verification:

Surface area of Cylinder A = 2πr(r+h) = 2 × 227 × 3.5(3.5+14)

= 2 × 227 × 3.5 × 17.5 = 385 cm²

Surface area of Cylinder B = 2πr(r+h) = 2 × 227 × 7(7+7)

= 2 × 227 × 7 × 14 = 616 cm²

616 > 385, so the cylinder with greater volume also has greater surface area.

Hinglish Explanation: Bina calculation ke hum keh sakte hain ki cylinder B ka volume zyada hoga kyunki uska radius double hai, aur volume radius ke square par depend karta hai. Calculation karne par bhi yehi result aaya. Zyada volume wale cylinder ka surface area bhi zyada hai.
