Chapter 1: Rational Numbers

Exercise 1.1 - Class 8 Mathematics (Solutions)

Question 1: Name the property under multiplication used in each of the following:

Solution:

(i) -4 5 × 1 = 1 × ( -4 5 ) = -4 5

Property: Multiplicative Identity

Explanation: The number 1 is called the multiplicative identity because when any rational number is multiplied by 1, we get the same number. (a × 1 = a)

Hinglish Explanation: 1 ko multiplicative identity kahte hain kyunki kisi bhi rational number ko 1 se multiply karne par wahi number milta hai. (a × 1 = a)

(ii) -13 17 × ( -2 7 ) = ( -2 7 ) × ( -13 17 )

Property: Commutativity of Multiplication

Explanation: Two rational numbers can be multiplied in any order, the result remains the same. (a × b = b × a)

Hinglish Explanation: Do rational numbers ko kisi bhi order mein multiply kiya ja sakta hai, result same rahta hai. (a × b = b × a)

(iii) -19 29 × 29 -19 = 1

Property: Multiplicative Inverse

Explanation: When a number is multiplied by its reciprocal, the result is 1. ((a/b) × (b/a) = 1)

Hinglish Explanation: Jab kisi number ko uska reciprocal se multiply kiya jata hai, to result 1 aata hai. ((a/b) × (b/a) = 1)

Question 2: Tell which property allows you to compute 1 3 × (6 × 4 3 ) as ( 1 3 × 6) × 4 3 .

Solution:

Property: Associativity of Multiplication

Explanation: This property allows us to group any two numbers first when multiplying three numbers, making calculation easier. (a × (b × c) = (a × b) × c)

Hinglish Explanation: Yeh property humein teen numbers ko multiply karte samay kisi bhi do numbers ko pehle group banane ki anumati deti hai, jisse calculation easy ho jati hai. (a × (b × c) = (a × b) × c)

Question 3: The product of two rational numbers is always a ________.

Solution:

The product of two rational numbers is always a rational number.

Explanation: When we multiply two rational numbers (numbers that can be expressed as fractions), the result is always another rational number. This is because the product of two fractions is also a fraction.

Hinglish Explanation: Jab hum do rational numbers (jo fractions ke form mein likhe ja sakte hain) ko multiply karte hain, to result hamesha ek rational number hi hota hai. Kyunki do fractions ka product bhi ek fraction hi hota hai.
