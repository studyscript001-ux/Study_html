Question 1: The following graph shows the temperature of a patient in a hospital, recorded every hour:

Solution:

(a) What was the patient's temperature at 1 p.m.?

From the graph: At 1 p.m., the patient's temperature was 36.5°C.

Answer: 36.5°C

Hinglish Explanation: Graph se dekh sakte hain ki 1 p.m. par patient ka temperature 36.5°C tha.

(b) When was the patient's temperature 38.5°C?

From the graph: The patient's temperature was 38.5°C at 12 noon.

Answer: 12 noon

Hinglish Explanation: Graph me dekhein to patient ka temperature 38.5°C 12 p.m. (dopahar) par tha.

(c) The patient's temperature was the same two times during the period shown. What were these two times?

From the graph: The patient's temperature was the same (36.5°C) at 1 p.m. and 2 p.m.

Answer: 1 p.m. and 2 p.m.

Hinglish Explanation: Pure time period mein, patient ka temperature do bar same tha - 1 p.m. aur 2 p.m. par, dono time 36.5°C.

(d) What was the temperature at 1:30 p.m.? How did you arrive at your answer?

From the graph: At 1:30 p.m., the patient's temperature was 36.5°C.

Reasoning: The time between 1 p.m. and 2 p.m. is 1:30 p.m., and during this time the graph shows a straight line at 36.5°C.

Answer: 36.5°C

Hinglish Explanation: 1:30 p.m. 1 p.m. aur 2 p.m. ke beech ka time hai, aur graph mein ye straight line dikhata hai 36.5°C par. Isliye temperature 36.5°C hi hoga.

(e) During which periods did the patient's temperature show an upward trend?

From the graph: The patient's temperature showed an upward trend between 9 a.m. to 10 a.m., 10 a.m. to 11 a.m., and 2 p.m. to 3 p.m.

Answer: 9-10 a.m., 10-11 a.m. and 2-3 p.m.

Hinglish Explanation: Patient ka temperature badhta hua dikhayi deta hai 9-10 a.m., 10-11 a.m., aur 2-3 p.m. ke periods mein.

Question 2: The following graph shows the sales of a manufacturing company in various years:

Solution:

(a) What were the sales in (i) 2002 (ii) 2006?

From the graph:

(i) Sales in 2002 = 4 crore rupees

(ii) Sales in 2006 = 8 crore rupees

Answer: (i) 4 crore rupees, (ii) 8 crore rupees

Hinglish Explanation: Graph se pata chalta hai ki 2002 mein sales 4 crore rupees thi aur 2006 mein 8 crore rupees.

(b) What were the sales in (i) 2003 (ii) 2005?

From the graph:

(i) Sales in 2003 = 7 crore rupees

(ii) Sales in 2005 = 10 crore rupees

Answer: (i) 7 crore rupees, (ii) 10 crore rupees

Hinglish Explanation: 2003 ki sales 7 crore rupees thi aur 2005 ki sales 10 crore rupees.

(c) Find the difference in sales between 2002 and 2006.

Calculation: Sales in 2006 - Sales in 2002 = 8 - 4 = 4 crore rupees

Answer: 4 crore rupees

Hinglish Explanation: 2006 ki sales (8 crore) mein se 2002 ki sales (4 crore) subtract karenge to difference 4 crore rupees aata hai.

(d) In which year was the difference in sales the maximum compared to its previous year?

From the graph: The difference in sales was maximum between 2004 and 2005.

Calculation: Sales in 2004 = 6.5 crore, Sales in 2005 = 10 crore, Difference = 3.5 crore

Answer: Between 2004 and 2005

Hinglish Explanation: 2004 (6.5 crore) se 2005 (10 crore) mein sales ka difference sabse zyada hai - 3.5 crore rupees.

Question 3: For an experiment in Botany, two plants A and B were grown under similar laboratory conditions. Their heights were measured at the end of each week for 3 weeks. The results are shown in the following graph:

Solution:

(a) How high was plant A after (i) 2 weeks (ii) 3 weeks?

From the graph:

(i) Height of plant A after 2 weeks = 7 cm

(ii) Height of plant A after 3 weeks = 9 cm

Answer: (i) 7 cm, (ii) 9 cm

Hinglish Explanation: Graph se pata chalta hai ki plant A ki height 2 weeks baad 7 cm aur 3 weeks baad 9 cm thi.

(b) How high was plant B after (i) 2 weeks (ii) 3 weeks?

From the graph:

(i) Height of plant B after 2 weeks = 7 cm

(ii) Height of plant B after 3 weeks = 10 cm

Answer: (i) 7 cm, (ii) 10 cm

Hinglish Explanation: Plant B ki height 2 weeks baad 7 cm aur 3 weeks baad 10 cm thi.

(c) How much did plant A grow during the 3rd week?

Calculation: Height at end of 3rd week - Height at end of 2nd week = 9 - 7 = 2 cm

Answer: 2 cm

Hinglish Explanation: Plant A 3rd week mein 2 cm badha (9 - 7 = 2 cm).

(d) How much did plant B grow from the end of the 2nd week to the end of the 3rd week?

Calculation: Height at end of 3rd week - Height at end of 2nd week = 10 - 7 = 3 cm

Answer: 3 cm

Hinglish Explanation: Plant B 2nd week ke end se 3rd week ke end tak 3 cm badha (10 - 7 = 3 cm).

(e) During which week did plant A grow the most?

From the graph: In the 1st week, plant A grew from 0 cm to 7 cm, i.e., an increase of 7 cm.

In the 2nd week, it grew from 7 cm to 7 cm, i.e., 0 cm increase.

In the 3rd week, it grew from 7 cm to 9 cm, i.e., 2 cm increase.

Answer: 1st week

Hinglish Explanation: Plant A sabse zyada 1st week mein badha (0 se 7 cm tak - 7 cm growth). 2nd week mein koi growth nahi hui, 3rd week mein 2 cm growth hui.

(f) During which week did plant B grow the least?

From the graph: In the 1st week, plant B grew from 0 cm to 4 cm, i.e., 4 cm increase.

In the 2nd week, it grew from 4 cm to 7 cm, i.e., 3 cm increase.

In the 3rd week, it grew from 7 cm to 10 cm, i.e., 3 cm increase.

Answer: 2nd and 3rd weeks (3 cm growth each)

Hinglish Explanation: Plant B ki sabse kam growth 2nd aur 3rd week mein hui (dono weeks mein 3-3 cm growth). 1st week mein 4 cm growth thi.

(g) Were the heights of both plants the same in any week? Identify.

From the graph: At the end of the 2nd week, both plants had the same height of 7 cm.

Answer: Yes, at the end of the 2nd week, both plants were 7 cm tall.

Hinglish Explanation: Haan, 2nd week ke end par dono plants ki height same thi - 7 cm.

Question 6: A courier goes on a bicycle to deliver a parcel to a merchant in a nearby museum. His distances from the city at different times are shown by the following graph:

Solution:

(a) What is the scale taken for the time axis?

From the graph: On the X-axis, 4 units = 1 hour

Answer: 4 units = 1 hour

Hinglish Explanation: X-axis (time axis) par 4 units 1 ghante ke barabar hain.

(b) How much time did he take to cover the whole journey?

From the graph: He left the city at 8 a.m. and returned at 12:30 p.m.

Calculation: 8:00 to 12:30 = 4.5 hours

Answer: 4.5 hours

Hinglish Explanation: Courier 8 a.m. se 12:30 p.m. tak journey ki, matlab 4.5 ghante.

(c) How far is the merchant's place from the city?

From the graph: The merchant's place is 22 km from the city.

Answer: 22 km

Hinglish Explanation: Merchant ki jagah city se 22 km door hai.

(d) Did the courier stop anywhere on the way? Give details.

From the graph: Yes, the courier stopped from 10 a.m. to 10:30 a.m.

Reasoning: During this time interval, the distance did not change.

Answer: Yes, from 10 a.m. to 10:30 a.m.

Hinglish Explanation: Haan, courier 10 a.m. se 10:30 a.m. tak ruka. Iska pata chalta hai kyunki is time period mein distance change nahi hua.

(e) During which period did he ride the fastest?

From the graph: He rode the fastest between 1 p.m. and 2 p.m.

Calculation: During this interval, he covered 10 km in 1 hour, speed = 10 km/h

Answer: Between 1 p.m. and 2 p.m.

Hinglish Explanation: Courier sabse tez 1 p.m. se 2 p.m. ke beech cycle chayi. Is time mein usne 1 ghante mein 10 km ki doori tai ki, matlab speed 10 km/h thi.

Question 1: Draw the graphs for the following tables of values, with appropriate scales on the axes:

Solution:

(a) Cost of apples

Number of apples: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5

Cost (in rupees): 5, 10, 15, 20, 25

Steps to draw the graph:

1. Mark number of apples on the X-axis and cost on the Y-axis

2. Scale: X-axis: 1 unit = 1 apple, Y-axis: 1 unit = 5 rupees

3. Plot the points: (1, 5), (2, 10), (3, 15), (4, 20), (5, 25)

4. Join these points with line segments

(i) Distance covered by the car between 7:30 a.m. and 8 a.m.:

Distance at 7:30 a.m. = 60 km, Distance at 8 a.m. = 120 km

Distance covered = 120 - 60 = 60 km

Answer: 60 km

Hinglish Explanation: 7:30 a.m. se 8 a.m. ke beech car ne 60 km ki doori tai ki (120 - 60 = 60 km).

(ii) At what time had the car covered a distance of 100 km?

From the graph: The car had covered 100 km at 7:30 a.m.

Answer: 7:30 a.m.

Hinglish Explanation: Car ne 100 km ki doori 7:30 a.m. tak tai kar li thi.

(b) Distance covered by a car

Time (in hours): 6 a.m., 7 a.m., 8 a.m., 9 a.m.

Distance (in km): 40, 80, 120, 160

Steps to draw the graph:

1. Mark time on the X-axis and distance on the Y-axis

2. Scale: X-axis: 2 units = 1 hour, Y-axis: 1 unit = 20 km

3. Plot the points: (6, 40), (7, 80), (8, 120), (9, 160)

4. Join these points with line segments

Hinglish Explanation: Is graph mein X-axis par time aur Y-axis par distance likhein. Diye gaye points ko plot karke unhe line segments se connect karein.

(c) Interest on deposits for a year

Deposit (in rupees): 1000, 2000, 3000, 4000, 5000

Simple interest (in rupees): 80, 160, 240, 320, 400

Steps to draw the graph:

1. Mark deposit on the X-axis and interest on the Y-axis

2. Scale: X-axis: 1 unit = 1000 rupees, Y-axis: 1 unit = 80 rupees

3. Plot the points: (1000, 80), (2000, 160), (3000, 240), (4000, 320), (5000, 400)

4. Join these points with line segments

(i) Does the graph pass through the origin?

Yes, because when deposit is 0, interest will also be 0.

Answer: Yes

Hinglish Explanation: Haan, graph origin (0,0) se hokar guzarta hai kyunki jab deposit 0 hai to interest bhi 0 hoga.

(ii) Use the graph to find the interest on ₹2500 for a year.

From the graph: For ₹2500 deposit, interest = ₹200

Answer: ₹200

Hinglish Explanation: Graph se pata chalta hai ki 2500 rupees ke deposit par interest 200 rupees hai.

(iii) To get an interest of ₹280 per year, how much money should be deposited?

From the graph: For ₹280 interest, deposit = ₹3500

Answer: ₹3500

Hinglish Explanation: 280 rupees interest pane ke liye 3500 rupees deposit karne honge.

Question 2: Draw graphs for the following tables:

Solution:

(i) Side of a square (in cm) and its perimeter (in cm)

Side (in cm): 2, 3, 3.5, 5, 6

Perimeter (in cm): 8, 12, 14, 20, 24

Steps to draw the graph:

1. Mark side on the X-axis and perimeter on the Y-axis

2. Scale: X-axis: 1 unit = 1 cm, Y-axis: 1 unit = 4 cm

3. Plot the points: (2, 8), (3, 12), (3.5, 14), (5, 20), (6, 24)

4. Join these points with line segments

Is it a linear graph?

Yes, because perimeter = 4 × side, which is a linear equation.

Answer: Yes

Hinglish Explanation: Haan, ye ek linear graph hai kyunki perimeter = 4 × side, jo ek linear equation hai.

(ii) Side of a square (in cm) and its area (in cm²)

Side (in cm): 2, 3, 4, 5, 6

Area (in cm²): 4, 9, 16, 25, 36

Steps to draw the graph:

1. Mark side on the X-axis and area on the Y-axis

2. Scale: X-axis: 1 unit = 1 cm, Y-axis: 1 unit = 5 cm²

3. Plot the points: (2, 4), (3, 9), (4, 16), (5, 25), (6, 36)

4. Join these points with line segments

Is it a linear graph?

No, because area = (side)², which is a quadratic equation.

Answer: No

Hinglish Explanation: Nahi, ye linear graph nahi hai kyunki area = (side)², jo ek quadratic equation hai.
