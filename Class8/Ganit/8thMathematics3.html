Q.2 What is a regular polygon? State the name of a regular polygon of (i) 3 sides (ii) 4 sides (iii) 6 sides.

Solution:

Regular Polygon: A polygon with all sides equal in length and all angles equal in measure is called a regular polygon.

Regular polygons with different number of sides:

  • (i) 3 sides: Equilateral Triangle
  • (ii) 4 sides: Square
  • (iii) 6 sides: Regular Hexagon

Characteristics:

  • All sides of a regular polygon are equal
  • All angles of a regular polygon are equal
  • Regular polygons have symmetry

Q.2 Find the measure of each exterior angle of a regular polygon of (i) 9 sides (ii) 15 sides.

Solution:

For a regular polygon, each exterior angle = 360° / number of sides

(i) 9 sides:

Each exterior angle = 360° / 9 = 40°

(ii) 15 sides:

Each exterior angle = 360° / 15 = 24°

Formula: Each exterior angle of a regular polygon = 360° / n, where n is the number of sides.

Q.3: How many sides does a regular polygon have if the measure of an exterior angle is 24°?

Solution:

For a regular polygon, number of sides = 360° / each exterior angle

Given: Each exterior angle = 24°

Number of sides = 360° / 24°

Number of sides = 15

Formula: n = 360° / each exterior angle, where n is the number of sides.

Q.4 How many sides does a regular polygon have if each of its interior angles is 165°?

Solution:

For a regular polygon, each exterior angle = 180° - each interior angle

Given: Each interior angle = 165°

Each exterior angle = 180° - 165° = 15°

Number of sides = 360° / each exterior angle

Number of sides = 360° / 15° = 24

Relation: Interior angle + Exterior angle = 180° (linear pair)

Q.5 (a) Is it possible to have a regular polygon with measure of each exterior angle as 22°?
(b) Can it be an interior angle of a regular polygon? Why?

Solution:

(a) Number of sides = 360° / 22° = 16.36

Since the number of sides is not a whole number, such a regular polygon is not possible.

(b) If interior angle = 22°, then exterior angle = 180° - 22° = 158°

Number of sides = 360° / 158° ≈ 2.27

Since the number of sides is not a whole number and is less than 3, this cannot be an interior angle of a regular polygon.

Condition: For a regular polygon, when 360° is divided by each exterior angle, the number of sides must be a whole number and it must be 3 or more.

Q.6 (a) What is the minimum interior angle possible for a regular polygon? Why?
(b) What is the maximum exterior angle possible for a regular polygon?

Solution:

(a) The minimum interior angle in a regular polygon occurs in a triangle.

Each interior angle in an equilateral triangle = 60°

Therefore, the minimum possible interior angle is 60°.

(b) The maximum exterior angle in a regular polygon occurs in a triangle.

Each exterior angle in an equilateral triangle = 120°

Therefore, the maximum possible exterior angle is 120°.

Reasoning: As the number of sides increases, the interior angle increases and the exterior angle decreases. A triangle has the minimum interior angle and maximum exterior angle.

(ii) AB = DC = 8 cm, AD = 4 cm and BC = 4.4 cm?

(ii) No, because in a parallelogram opposite sides are equal, but here AD ≠ BC (4 cm ≠ 4.4 cm).

(iii) No, because in a parallelogram opposite angles are equal, but here ∠A ≠ ∠C (70° ≠ 65°).

Question 5: The measures of two adjacent angles of a parallelogram are in the ratio 3:2. Find the measure of each of the angles of the parallelogram.

Solution:

Let the adjacent angles be 3x and 2x.

Sum of adjacent angles in a parallelogram = 180°

3x + 2x = 180°

5x = 180°

x = 36°

Angles = 3x = 3 × 36° = 108°

and 2x = 2 × 36° = 72°

Opposite angles of a parallelogram are equal, so the angles are: 108°, 72°, 108°, 72°

Question 6: Two adjacent angles of a parallelogram have equal measure. Find the measure of each of the angles of the parallelogram.

All angles of the parallelogram are 90°.

Question 3: Explain how a square is:
(i) a quadrilateral
(ii) a parallelogram
(iii) a rhombus
(iv) a rectangle

Solution:

(i) a quadrilateral: A square has four sides, so it is a quadrilateral.

(ii) a parallelogram: Opposite sides of a square are parallel and equal, so it is a parallelogram.

(iii) a rhombus: All sides of a square are equal, so it is a rhombus.

(iv) a rectangle: All angles of a square are 90°, so it is a rectangle.

Question 5: Explain why a rectangle is a convex quadrilateral.

Solution:

A rectangle is a convex quadrilateral because:

  • All its interior angles are less than 180° (each is 90°)
  • The line segment joining any two points lies completely inside the rectangle
  • Its diagonals lie inside the rectangle

Question 6: ABC is a right-angled triangle and 'O' is the midpoint of the side opposite to the right angle. Explain why 'O' is equidistant from A, B and C.

Solution:

ABC is a right-angled triangle with ∠B = 90°

O is the midpoint of AC (AC is the side opposite to the right angle)

In a right-angled triangle, the midpoint of the hypotenuse is equidistant from all three vertices.

Therefore, OA = OB = OC

Hence, point O is equidistant from A, B and C.
