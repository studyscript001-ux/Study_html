Exercise Solution

1. What is the term used for chemical secretions of endocrine glands responsible for changes taking place in the body?

The term used for chemical secretions of endocrine glands responsible for changes in the body is hormones.

Hormones are chemical messengers that regulate various bodily functions, including growth and development during adolescence.

2. Define adolescence.

Adolescence is the period of life between childhood and adulthood, from about 11 to 18 or 19 years of age, when the body undergoes changes leading to reproductive maturity.

This period is characterized by rapid physical, psychological, and emotional changes. Since it covers the "teens" (13 to 19 years), adolescents are also called "teenagers."

3. What is menstruation? Explain.

Menstruation is the process in which the uterus sheds its lining along with blood and mucus through the vagina when a fertilized egg does not implant in the uterine wall.

This process occurs in females of reproductive age and typically happens every 28-30 days. The menstrual cycle includes:

  • Maturation of an egg in the ovary
  • Release of the egg (ovulation)
  • Thickening of the uterine wall to prepare for pregnancy
  • Shedding of the uterine lining if fertilization doesn't occur

The first menstrual flow is called menarche, and it stops at menopause (around 45-50 years of age).

6. What are sex hormones? Why are they named so? State their function.

Sex hormones are chemical substances produced by testes in males and ovaries in females that control the development of secondary sexual characteristics and reproductive functions.

They are named "sex hormones" because they play a crucial role in sexual development and reproduction.

Functions:

  • Testosterone (male hormone):
    • Stimulates the development of male secondary sexual characteristics
    • Promotes growth of facial and body hair
    • Deepens the voice
    • Stimulates sperm production
  • Estrogen (female hormone):
    • Stimulates the development of female secondary sexual characteristics
    • Promotes breast development
    • Regulates the menstrual cycle
    • Prepares the uterus for pregnancy

8. Write notes on—

(a) Adam's apple

The Adam's apple is the protruding part of the throat seen in boys during puberty. It is formed by the growing voice box (larynx). As boys reach puberty, their voice box enlarges significantly, creating this visible bulge. The Adam's apple contributes to the deepening of the voice in males.

(b) Secondary sexual characters

Secondary sexual characters are physical features that develop during puberty which distinguish males from females but are not directly involved in reproduction. Examples include:

  • In boys: Facial hair, deeper voice, broader shoulders
  • In girls: Breast development, wider hips
  • In both: Pubic and underarm hair growth

These characteristics are controlled by sex hormones—testosterone in males and estrogen in females.

(c) Sex determination in the unborn baby

The sex of a baby is determined at the moment of fertilization by the type of sperm that fertilizes the egg:

  • All eggs carry an X chromosome
  • Sperms carry either an X or a Y chromosome
  • If an X-carrying sperm fertilizes the egg, the zygote will be XX → female child
  • If a Y-carrying sperm fertilizes the egg, the zygote will be XY → male child

Thus, the father's sperm determines the sex of the child, not the mother.
