Question 6

A blacksmith hammers a hot piece of iron while making a tool. How does the force due to hammering affect the piece of iron?

The force of hammering changes the shape of the hot iron piece. When the blacksmith hammers the hot iron, it flattens and takes the shape of the tool being made. The force applied by the hammer causes the iron to change its form without breaking because it is heated and malleable.

Question 7

An inflated balloon was pressed against a wall after it has been rubbed with a piece of synthetic cloth. It was found that the balloon sticks to the wall. What force might be responsible for the attraction between the balloon and the wall?

The electrostatic force is responsible for the attraction between the balloon and the wall. When the balloon is rubbed with synthetic cloth, it becomes electrically charged. This charged balloon attracts the uncharged wall due to electrostatic force, which is a non-contact force.

Question 8

Name the forces acting on a plastic bucket containing water held above ground level in your hand. Discuss why the forces acting on the bucket do not bring a change in its state of motion.

The forces acting on the bucket are:

1. Gravitational force (pulling the bucket downward)

2. Muscular force (your hand pushing the bucket upward)

These forces do not bring a change in the state of motion because they are balanced forces. The muscular force exerted by your hand is equal in magnitude but opposite in direction to the gravitational force. When balanced forces act on an object, they cancel each other out, and there is no change in the state of motion.

Question 9

A rocket has been fired upwards to launch a satellite in its orbit. Name the two forces acting on the rocket immediately after leaving the launching pad.

The two forces acting on the rocket are:

1. Gravitational force (pulling the rocket downward towards Earth)

2. Force of thrust (pushing the rocket upward due to the burning fuel)

The thrust force is greater than the gravitational force, which is why the rocket accelerates upward.

Question 10

When we press the bulb of a dropper with its nozzle kept in water, air in the dropper is seen to escape in the form of bubbles. Once we release the pressure on the bulb, water gets filled in the dropper. The rise of water in the dropper is due to:

(a) pressure of water.

(b) gravity of the earth.

(c) shape of rubber bulb.

(d) atmospheric pressure.

Correct answer: (d) atmospheric pressure

When we release the pressure on the bulb, the air pressure inside the dropper decreases. The higher atmospheric pressure outside pushes the water up into the dropper.
