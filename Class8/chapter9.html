Question 2

Four children were asked to arrange forces due to rolling, static and sliding frictions in a decreasing order. Their arrangements are given below. Choose the correct arrangement.

(a) rolling, static, sliding
(b) rolling, sliding, static
(c) static, sliding, rolling
(d) sliding, static, rolling

Correct answer: (c) static, sliding, rolling

Static friction is the strongest because it prevents objects from starting to move. Sliding friction is weaker than static friction but stronger than rolling friction. Rolling friction is the weakest of the three.

Question 3

Alida runs her toy car on dry marble floor, wet marble floor, newspaper and towel spread on the floor. The force of friction acting on the car on different surfaces in increasing order will be:

(a) wet marble floor, dry marble floor, newspaper and towel
(b) newspaper, towel, dry marble floor, wet marble floor
(c) towel, newspaper, dry marble floor, wet marble floor
(d) wet marble floor, dry marble floor, towel, newspaper

Correct answer: (a) wet marble floor, dry marble floor, newspaper and towel

Wet marble floor has the least friction because water acts as a lubricant. Dry marble floor has more friction than wet marble. Newspaper has even more friction, and towel has the most friction due to its rough surface.

Question 4

Suppose your writing desk is tilted a little. A book kept on it starts sliding down. Show the direction of frictional force acting on it.

When the book slides down the tilted desk, the frictional force acts in the opposite direction to the motion of the book. Since the book is sliding downward, the frictional force acts upward along the surface of the desk.

Question 5

You spill a bucket of soapy water on a marble floor accidently. Would it make it easier or more difficult for you to walk on the floor? Why?

It would make it more difficult to walk on the floor. Soapy water acts as a lubricant and reduces friction between your feet and the floor. With less friction, it becomes harder to get a good grip, and you might slip and fall.

Question 6

Explain why sportsmen use shoes with spikes.

Sportsmen use shoes with spikes to increase friction between their shoes and the ground. The spikes dig into the ground, providing better grip and preventing slipping. This helps them run faster, change direction quickly, and maintain balance during sports activities.

Question 7

Iqbal has to push a lighter box and Seema has to push a similar heavier box on the same floor. Who will have to apply a larger force and why?

Seema will have to apply a larger force. Friction depends on how hard the two surfaces press together. The heavier box presses harder against the floor, creating more friction. To overcome this greater friction, Seema needs to apply more force.

Question 8

Explain why sliding friction is less than static friction.

Sliding friction is less than static friction because:

1. When an object is at rest, the irregularities on the surfaces are more deeply interlocked.

2. Once the object starts moving, the contact points don't have enough time to lock into each other completely.

3. When sliding, only the tips of surface irregularities are in contact, reducing the friction.

Question 9

Give examples to show that friction is both a friend and a foe.

Friction as a friend (helpful):

1. It allows us to walk without slipping

2. It helps vehicles to move on roads without skidding

3. It enables us to hold objects in our hands

4. It helps in stopping vehicles when brakes are applied

Friction as a foe (harmful):

1. It causes wear and tear of machine parts

2. It produces heat which can damage machines

3. It makes it difficult to push heavy objects

4. It causes soles of shoes to wear out

Question 10

Explain why objects moving in fluids must have special shapes.

Objects moving in fluids (liquids or gases) must have special shapes to reduce fluid friction (drag). Special streamlined shapes help in:

1. Reducing the resistance offered by the fluid

2. Allowing the fluid to flow smoothly around the object

3. Minimizing energy loss due to friction

4. Increasing speed and efficiency of movement

Examples: Fish have streamlined bodies to move easily in water, and airplanes have special shapes to reduce air resistance.
