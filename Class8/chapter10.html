(b) solids only
(c) liquids only
(d) solids, liquids and gases
Explanation: Sound requires a medium to travel and can propagate through solids, liquids, and gases.
2. Voice of which of the following is likely to have minimum frequency?
(a) Baby girl
(b) Baby boy
(c) A man
(d) A woman
Explanation: Men typically have longer vocal cords (about 20mm) which vibrate with lower frequency, producing deeper voices.

5. A pendulum oscillates 40 times in 4 seconds. Find its time period and frequency.
Solution:
Number of oscillations = 40
Total time taken = 4 seconds
Time period = Total time taken / Number of oscillations = 4 / 40 = 0.1 seconds
Frequency = 1 / Time period = 1 / 0.1 = 10 Hz
6. The sound from a mosquito is produced when it vibrates its wings at an average rate of 500 vibrations per second. What is the time period of the vibration?
Solution:
Frequency = 500 Hz
Time period = 1 / Frequency = 1 / 500 = 0.002 seconds

(a) Dholak: Stretched membrane

8. What is the difference between noise and music? Can music become noise sometimes?
Difference: Music is a pleasant, ordered sound that is pleasing to the ear, while noise is an unpleasant, disordered sound that can cause discomfort.

Can music become noise? Yes, when music is too loud or played at inappropriate times, it can become noise that causes disturbance and discomfort.
9. List sources of noise pollution in your surroundings.
Sources of noise pollution include:
  • Vehicles (cars, buses, trucks, motorcycles)
  • Construction equipment
  • Industrial machinery
  • Loudspeakers
  • Firecrackers
  • Airplanes
  • Household appliances at high volumes
10. Explain in what way noise pollution is harmful to human.
Noise pollution can be harmful to humans in several ways:
  • Hearing loss or impairment
  • Sleep disturbances and insomnia
  • Stress and anxiety
  • High blood pressure (hypertension)
  • Cardiovascular problems
  • Reduced productivity and concentration
  • Headaches and fatigue
11. Your parents are going to buy a house. They have been offered one on the roadside and another three lanes away from the roadside. Which house would you suggest your parents should buy? Explain your answer.
Suggestion: The house three lanes away from the roadside would be better.

Explanation: The house away from the roadside would experience less noise pollution from traffic, resulting in a quieter and more peaceful living environment. This would lead to better sleep, less stress, and overall improved quality of life.
12. Sketch larynx and explain its function in your own words.
Function of Larynx: The larynx (voice box) is located at the upper end of the windpipe. It contains two vocal cords that stretch across it, leaving a narrow slit for air passage. When air from the lungs is forced through this slit, the vocal cords vibrate, producing sound. The muscles attached to the vocal cords can change their tension, affecting the pitch of the sound produced.
13. Lightning and thunder take place in the sky at the same time and at the same distance from us. Lightning is seen earlier and thunder is heard later. Can you explain why?
Explanation: Light travels much faster (approximately 300,000 km/s) than sound (approximately 343 m/s in air). Although lightning and thunder occur simultaneously, light reaches our eyes almost instantly, while sound takes much longer to travel the same distance to our ears. This is why we see lightning before we hear thunder.
