Q.23 Write the central idea of the poem 'The Last Bargain' written by Rabindranath Tagore.
The central idea of 'The Last Bargain' by Rabindranath Tagore is that true freedom and fulfillment cannot be bought with material wealth or power. The speaker in the poem rejects offers from a king, a rich man, and a beautiful woman, finding true liberation only when a child offers to hire him with nothing - representing the value of innocence, simplicity, and freedom from worldly attachments.

Q.25 Read the passage and answer the following questions.
The mother tongue is the first language that the child learns and picks up from the people around him. A foreign language is a language that one uses for a limited purpose. The mother tongue comes in a natural way, whereas a foreign language is learned so that one may be able to write and speak it. A language is learned with its structure, grammar and vocabulary. The mother tongue may have a different structure, grammar and vocabulary. The mother tongue may have a different sentence structure. Often the method of translation is adopted for easy understanding of a foreign language. This is not a good way of learning a foreign language. So instead of learning a foreign language through translation one should learn the correct structure, grammar and vocabulary of the new language. The exposure to a language is also an important factor in learning.
(a) Which is the first language that a child learns?
The mother tongue is the first language that a child learns.
(b) What is the purpose of a foreign language?
A foreign language is used for a limited purpose.
(c) How is the language learned?
A language is learned with its structure, grammar and vocabulary.
(d) Which method is often adopted to learn a foreign language?
The method of translation is often adopted to learn a foreign language.
(e) Which is the most important factor in learning a language?

Q.26 Write an essay on 'Importance of Computer'.
Importance of Computers

Computers have become an indispensable part of modern life, revolutionizing how we work, communicate, learn, and entertain ourselves. Their importance spans across various sectors including education, healthcare, business, and communication.

In education, computers have transformed traditional learning methods. Students can access vast information online, use educational software, and participate in virtual classrooms. They enable distance learning, making education accessible to people in remote areas.

In the business world, computers streamline operations, manage data, facilitate communication through email and video conferencing, and enable e-commerce. They have increased efficiency and productivity across industries.

In healthcare, computers assist in diagnosis, patient record management, medical research, and surgical procedures. They have significantly improved the accuracy and speed of medical services.

Computers have revolutionized communication, making it instant and global through the internet, social media, and messaging platforms. They have connected people across continents, fostering global understanding and collaboration.

Moreover, computers play a crucial role in scientific research, weather forecasting, space exploration, and national security. They process complex data that would be impossible for humans to handle manually.

While computers offer immense benefits, it's important to use them responsibly and be aware of challenges like cybersecurity threats and digital addiction. Nevertheless, their positive impact on society is undeniable, and they will continue to shape our future in profound ways.
