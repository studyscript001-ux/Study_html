Question 1:

A survey was made to find the type of music that a certain group of young people liked in a city. The adjoining pie chart shows the findings. From this pie chart, answer the following:

  • (i) If 20 people liked classical music, how many young people were surveyed?
  • (ii) Which type of music is liked by the maximum number of people?
  • (iii) If a cassette company were to make 1000 CDs, how many of each type would they make?

Solution:

Pie chart se pata chalta hai ki 10% log Classical music pasand karte hain.

(i) Finding the total number of people surveyed:

Let the total number of people surveyed be 'x'.

10% of x = 20 people (who liked classical music).

(10/100) * x = 20

x = 20 * (100/10)

x = 200 people.

Iska matlab, kul 200 logon ka survey kiya gaya tha.

(ii) Music liked by the maximum number of people:

The largest sector in the pie chart is for 'Light' music, which is 40%.

Therefore, Light music is liked by the maximum number of people.

Pie chart mein sabse bada hissa Light music ka hai (40%), isliye wo sabse zyada pasand kiya jata hai.

(iii) Number of CDs for each type (out of 1000):

  • Classical (10%): (10/100) * 1000 = 100 CDs
  • Semi Classical (20%): (20/100) * 1000 = 200 CDs
  • Folk (30%): (30/100) * 1000 = 300 CDs
  • Light (40%): (40/100) * 1000 = 400 CDs

Question 2:

A group of 360 people were asked to vote for their favourite season from the three seasons rainy, winter and summer.

  • (i) Which season got the most votes?
  • (ii) Find the central angle of each sector.
  • (iii) Draw a pie chart to show this information.

Solution:

(i) Season with the most votes:

Looking at the data, Winter has 150 votes, which is the highest.

Therefore, Winter season got the most votes.

(ii) Central angle for each sector:

Central angle nikalne ka formula hai: (Votes for season / Total Votes) * 360°.

  • Summer: (90 / 360) * 360° = 90°
  • Rainy: (120 / 360) * 360° = 120°
  • Winter: (150 / 360) * 360° = 150°

(iii) Drawing the Pie Chart:

To draw the pie chart, follow these steps:

  1. Draw a circle of any radius.
  2. Draw a radius to act as a starting line.
  3. Use a protractor to draw a sector of 90° for Summer.
  4. From the new line, draw a sector of 120° for Rainy.
  5. The remaining sector will be 150° for Winter.
  6. Label each sector with the season name.

Question 4:

The adjoining pie chart gives the marks scored in an examination. Total marks obtained were 540. Answer the following questions:

  • (i) In which subject did the student score 105 marks?
  • (ii) How many more marks were obtained in Mathematics than in Hindi?
  • (iii) Examine whether the sum of marks in Social Science and Mathematics is more than that in Science and Hindi.

Solution:

Total marks 540 hain, jo pie chart ke 360° ke barabar hain.

(i) Subject with 105 marks:

First, find the central angle for 105 marks.

Angle = (Marks Obtained / Total Marks) * 360°

Angle = (105 / 540) * 360° = 70°

The pie chart shows that the sector with 70° corresponds to Hindi.

(ii) Marks difference between Mathematics and Hindi:

Har subject ke marks nikalne ka formula: (Central Angle / 360°) * Total Marks.

Marks in Mathematics (90°): (90 / 360) * 540 = 135 marks.

Marks in Hindi (70°): (70 / 360) * 540 = 105 marks.

Difference = 135 - 105 = 30 marks.

The student obtained 30 more marks in Mathematics than in Hindi.

(iii) Comparing sum of marks:

Hum marks nikalne ke bajaye, seedhe central angles ko compare kar sakte hain. Jiska angle bada, uske marks zyada.

Sum of angles for Social Science (65°) and Mathematics (90°) = 65° + 90° = 155°

Sum of angles for Science (80°) and Hindi (70°) = 80° + 70° = 150°

Since 155° > 150°, the sum of marks in Social Science and Mathematics is more than in Science and Hindi.

Yes, the statement is correct.

Question 1:

List the outcomes you can see in these experiments.

  • (a) Spinning a wheel (with sectors A, B, C, D, A)
  • (b) Tossing two coins together

Solution:

Outcomes ka matlab hai ki experiment karne par kya-kya results aa sakte hain.

(a) Spinning a wheel:

The possible outcomes are the letters on which the pointer can stop. The outcomes are: A, B, C, D.

'A' do baar hai, lekin wo ek hi outcome hai.

(b) Tossing two coins together:

Let H = Head, T = Tail. The possible outcomes are:

  • Both coins show Heads: HH
  • First coin shows Head, second shows Tail: HT
  • First coin shows Tail, second shows Head: TH
  • Both coins show Tails: TT

Question 2:

When a die is thrown, list the outcomes of an event of getting:

  • (i) (a) a prime number (b) not a prime number
  • (ii) (a) a number greater than 5 (b) a number not greater than 5

Solution:

Ek die (pasa) fekne par total outcomes hote hain: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6.

(i) (a) a prime number:

Prime numbers on a die are 2, 3, 5. The outcomes are: 2, 3, 5.

Prime numbers wo hote hain jo sirf 1 se aur khud se divide hote hain. 1 prime nahi hota.

(i) (b) not a prime number:

The numbers that are not prime are 1, 4, 6. The outcomes are: 1, 4, 6.

(ii) (a) a number greater than 5:

The only number on a die greater than 5 is 6. The outcome is: 6.

(ii) (b) a number not greater than 5:

The numbers not greater than 5 are 1, 2, 3, 4, 5. The outcomes are: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5.

Question 3:

Find the:

  • (a) Probability of the pointer stopping on D in (Question 1-a)?
  • (b) Probability of getting an ace from a well-shuffled deck of 52 playing cards?
  • (c) Probability of getting a red apple. (From a basket of 4 red and 3 green apples)

Solution:

Probability (sambhavna) ka formula hai: (Favorable Outcomes) / (Total Possible Outcomes).

(a) Probability of stopping on D:

In the spinning wheel (A, B, C, D, A), there are 5 total sectors. Only one sector is 'D'.

Probability (D) = 1/5. The probability is 1/5.

(b) Probability of getting an ace:

A deck has 52 cards. There are 4 aces in a deck.

Probability (Ace) = 4/52 = 1/13. The probability is 1/13.

(c) Probability of getting a red apple:

Total apples = 4 (Red) + 3 (Green) = 7 apples. Number of red apples = 4.

Probability (Red Apple) = 4/7. The probability is 4/7.

Question 4:

Numbers 1 to 10 are written on ten separate slips. One slip is chosen from the box. What is the probability of:

  • (i) getting a number 6?
  • (ii) getting a number less than 6?
  • (iii) getting a number greater than 6?
  • (iv) getting a 1-digit number?

Solution:

Total slips (outcomes) = 10 (numbers 1 to 10).

(i) getting a number 6: There is only one slip with '6'. Probability = 1/10.

(ii) getting a number less than 6: Numbers are 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 (5 slips). Probability = 5/10 = 1/2.

(iii) getting a number greater than 6: Numbers are 7, 8, 9, 10 (4 slips). Probability = 4/10 = 2/5.

(iv) getting a 1-digit number: Numbers are 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9 (9 slips). Probability = 9/10.

Question 5:

If you have a spinning wheel with 3 green sectors, 1 blue sector, and 1 red sector, what is the probability of getting a green sector? What is the probability of getting a non-blue sector?

Solution:

Total sectors = 3 (Green) + 1 (Blue) + 1 (Red) = 5 sectors.

Probability of getting a green sector:

Number of green sectors = 3.

Probability (Green) = 3/5. The probability is 3/5.

Probability of getting a non-blue sector:

Non-blue sectors are the green and red ones. Number = 3 (Green) + 1 (Red) = 4.

Probability (Non-blue) = 4/5. The probability is 4/5.
