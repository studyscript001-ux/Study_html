Exercise 11.1 - Direct Proportion

Question 1

Following are the car parking charges near a railway station upto:
- 4 hours: ₹60
- 8 hours: ₹100
- 12 hours: ₹140
- 24 hours: ₹180
Check if the parking charges are in direct proportion to the parking time.

Hinglish Explanation: Humein check karna hai ki kya parking charges (paisa) parking time (ghante) ke saath direct proportion mein badh rahe hain. Agar C/T (Charges/Time) ka ratio hamesha same rahega, to ye direct proportion hai.

Solution:

For two quantities to be in direct proportion, the ratio between them must be constant. Let's calculate the ratio of charges to time (C/T) for each case.

Case 1: C₁/T₁ = 60 / 4 = 15

Case 2: C₂/T₂ = 100 / 8 = 12.5

Case 3: C₃/T₃ = 140 / 12 ≈ 11.67

Case 4: C₄/T₄ = 180 / 24 = 7.5

Since the ratios 15, 12.5, 11.67, 7.5 are not equal.

The parking charges are NOT in direct proportion to the parking time.

Question 2

A mixture of paint is prepared by mixing 1 part of red pigments with 8 parts of base. In the following table, find the parts of base that need to be added.

Parts of red pigment 1 4 7 12 20
Parts of base 8 ? ? ? ?
Hinglish Explanation: Yahan paint mixture ka ratio diya hai: 1 part red pigment ke liye 8 parts base. Yeh ek direct proportion ka case hai. Jaise red pigment badhega, waise hi base bhi badhega. Hum formula x₁/y₁ = x₂/y₂ use karke missing values nikalenge.

Solution:

Let the parts of red pigment be x and parts of base be y. The given ratio is x/y = 1/8. This is a case of direct proportion.

1. For 4 parts of red pigment (x₂ = 4):
1/8 = 4/y₂ => y₂ = 4 * 8 = 32

2. For 7 parts of red pigment (x₃ = 7):
1/8 = 7/y₃ => y₃ = 7 * 8 = 56

3. For 12 parts of red pigment (x₄ = 12):
1/8 = 12/y₄ => y₄ = 12 * 8 = 96

4. For 20 parts of red pigment (x₅ = 20):
1/8 = 20/y₅ => y₅ = 20 * 8 = 160

The completed table is: Base parts are 32, 56, 96, 160.

Hinglish Explanation: Pichle question jaisa hi hai, bas values alag hain. Yahan 1 part red pigment ke liye 75 mL base use hota hai. Pucha hai ki 1800 mL base ke liye kitna red pigment lagega. Solution:

Question 4

A machine in a soft drink factory fills 840 bottles in six hours. How many bottles will it fill in five hours?

Hinglish Explanation: Ek machine 6 ghante mein 840 bottles bharti hai. Pucha hai 5 ghante mein kitni bottles bharegi. Jitna zyada time, utni zyada bottles. Yeh direct proportion hai.

Solution:

Let the number of bottles be x and the time taken be y (in hours).

Given: x₁ = 840 bottles, y₁ = 6 hours.
We need to find x₂ when y₂ = 5 hours.

Using direct proportion: x₁/y₁ = x₂/y₂

840 / 6 = x₂ / 5

140 = x₂ / 5

x₂ = 140 * 5 = 700

The machine will fill 700 bottles in five hours.

Question 5

A photograph of a bacteria enlarged 50,000 times attains a length of 5 cm. What is the actual length of the bacteria? If the photograph is enlarged 20,000 times only, what would be its enlarged length?

Hinglish Explanation: Ek bacteria ko 50,000 guna bada karne par uski length 5 cm ho jaati hai. Humein do cheezein batani hain: (i) Bacteria ki asli (actual) length kya hai? (ii) Agar use sirf 20,000 guna bada karein, to uski length kya hogi? Jitna zyada enlarge karenge, utni zyada length hogi, isliye yeh direct proportion hai.

Solution:

Let the enlarged length be x and the enlargement factor be y.

Part (i): Actual Length

Given: x₁ = 5 cm, y₁ = 50,000 times. Actual length means enlargement is 1 time (y₂ = 1). Let the actual length be x₂.

x₁/y₁ = x₂/y₂

5 / 50000 = x₂ / 1

x₂ = 1 / 10000 = 0.0001 cm or 10⁻⁴ cm.

Part (ii): Enlarged length at 20,000 times

We need to find the new length (x₃) when enlargement (y₃) is 20,000 times.

x₁/y₁ = x₃/y₃

5 / 50000 = x₃ / 20000

x₃ = (5 * 20000) / 50000

x₃ = 100000 / 50000 = 2 cm.

(i) The actual length of the bacteria is 10⁻⁴ cm. (ii) The enlarged length would be 2 cm.

Question 6

In a model of a ship, the mast is 9 cm high, while the mast of the actual ship is 12 m high. If the length of the ship is 28 m, how long is the model ship?

Hinglish Explanation: Ek jahaj (ship) ka model banaya gaya hai. Model mein mast (danda) 9 cm ka hai, jabki asli jahaj mein woh 12 m ka hai. Agar asli jahaj 28 m lamba hai, to model jahaj kitna lamba hoga? Model ki size aur asli size direct proportion mein hote hain. Pehle sab units ko same (cm) mein convert kar lenge.

Solution:

Let the dimensions of the model be x and the actual ship be y. First, let's convert all units to cm. 1 m = 100 cm. Actual mast height = 12 m = 1200 cm. Actual ship length = 28 m = 2800 cm.

We can set up a table:

DimensionModel Ship (cm)Actual Ship (cm)
Mast Height91200
Ship LengthL2800

Using direct proportion (Ratio of model dimensions to actual dimensions is constant):

(Model Mast) / (Actual Mast) = (Model Length) / (Actual Length)

9 / 1200 = L / 2800

L = (9 * 2800) / 1200

L = (9 * 28) / 12 = 3 * 7 = 21 cm.

The length of the model ship is 21 cm.

Question 7

Suppose 2 kg of sugar contains 9 x 10⁶ crystals. How many sugar crystals are there in (i) 5 kg of sugar? (ii) 1.2 kg of sugar?

Hinglish Explanation: 2 kg cheeni mein 9 x 10⁶ daane (crystals) hain. Humein batana hai ki 5 kg aur 1.2 kg cheeni mein kitne daane honge. Jitni zyada cheeni, utne zyada daane. Yeh direct proportion hai.

Solution:

Let the number of crystals be x and the weight of sugar be y (in kg).

Given: x₁ = 9 x 10⁶, y₁ = 2 kg.

Part (i): Crystals in 5 kg of sugar

We need to find x₂ when y₂ = 5 kg.

(9 x 10⁶) / 2 = x₂ / 5

x₂ = (5 * 9 x 10⁶) / 2 = 45/2 x 10⁶ = 22.5 x 10⁶

In standard form: x₂ = 2.25 x 10⁷

Part (ii): Crystals in 1.2 kg of sugar

We need to find x₃ when y₃ = 1.2 kg.

(9 x 10⁶) / 2 = x₃ / 1.2

x₃ = (1.2 * 9 x 10⁶) / 2 = 10.8/2 x 10⁶ = 5.4 x 10⁶

(i) There are 2.25 x 10⁷ crystals in 5 kg of sugar. (ii) There are 5.4 x 10⁶ crystals in 1.2 kg of sugar.

Question 8

Rashmi has a road map with a scale of 1 cm representing 18 km. She drives on a road for 72 km. What would be her distance covered in the map?

Hinglish Explanation: Ek map (naksha) par 1 cm ka matlab asliyat mein 18 km hai. Agar Rashmi 72 km gaadi chalati hai, to map par yeh doori kitni hogi? Map ki doori aur asli doori direct proportion mein hoti hain.

Given: x₁ = 1 cm, y₁ = 18 km.

We need to find x₂ when y₂ = 72 km.

Question 9

A 5 m 60 cm high vertical pole casts a shadow 3 m 20 cm long. Find at the same time (i) the length of the shadow cast by another pole 10 m 50 cm high (ii) the height of a pole which casts a shadow 5 m long.

Hinglish Explanation: Ek 5m 60cm unche pole ki parchai (shadow) 3m 20cm lambi hai. Usi samay par, (i) 10m 50cm unche pole ki parchai kitni lambi hogi? (ii) 5m lambi parchai banane wale pole ki unchai kya hogi? Pole ki height aur shadow ki length direct proportion mein hote hain. Sabse pehle units ko cm mein convert karna aasan rahega.

Solution:

Let the height of the pole be x and the length of the shadow be y. First, convert all measurements to cm (1 m = 100 cm).

Initial pole height: x₁ = 5 m 60 cm = 560 cm.
Initial shadow length: y₁ = 3 m 20 cm = 320 cm.

Part (i): Find shadow for a 10 m 50 cm pole

New pole height: x₂ = 10 m 50 cm = 1050 cm.
We need to find the new shadow length y₂.

x₁/y₁ = x₂/y₂

560 / 320 = 1050 / y₂

7 / 4 = 1050 / y₂

y₂ = (1050 * 4) / 7 = 150 * 4 = 600 cm or 6 m.

Part (ii): Find pole height for a 5 m shadow

New shadow length: y₃ = 5 m = 500 cm.
We need to find the new pole height x₃.

x₁/y₁ = x₃/y₃

560 / 320 = x₃ / 500

7 / 4 = x₃ / 500

x₃ = (7 * 500) / 4 = 7 * 125 = 875 cm or 8 m 75 cm.

(i) The shadow length is 6 m. (ii) The pole height is 8 m 75 cm.

Question 10

A loaded truck travels 14 km in 25 minutes. If the speed remains the same, how far can it travel in 5 hours?

Hinglish Explanation: Ek truck 25 minute mein 14 km chalta hai. Agar speed wahi rahe, to woh 5 ghante mein kitni door jayega? Time aur distance direct proportion mein hain. Dono time units ko same (minutes) mein convert karna hoga.

Solution:

Let the distance travelled be x (in km) and time taken be y (in minutes).

First, convert 5 hours to minutes: 5 hours * 60 min/hour = 300 minutes.

Given: x₁ = 14 km, y₁ = 25 minutes.
We need to find x₂ when y₂ = 300 minutes.

x₁/y₁ = x₂/y₂

14 / 25 = x₂ / 300

x₂ = (14 * 300) / 25 = 14 * 12 = 168 km.

The truck can travel 168 km in 5 hours.

Exercise 11.2 - Inverse Proportion

Question 1

Which of the following are in inverse proportion?

  1. The number of workers on a job and the time to complete the job.
  2. The time taken for a journey and the distance travelled in a uniform speed.
  3. Area of cultivated land and the crop harvested.
  4. The time taken for a fixed journey and the speed of the vehicle.
  5. The population of a country and the area of land per person.
Hinglish Explanation: Humein batana hai ki inmein se kaun se cases inverse proportion ke hain. Inverse proportion ka matlab hai: jab ek cheez badhti hai, to doosri cheez घटती hai (aur unka product constant rehta hai).

Solution:

(i) Inverse Proportion: More workers will take less time to complete the job. (Agar workers badhenge to time kam lagega).

(ii) Direct Proportion: More time means more distance travelled (at uniform speed). (Agar time badhega to distance bhi badhegi).

(iii) Direct Proportion: More area cultivated means more crop harvested. (Agar area badhega to fasal bhi zyada hogi).

(iv) Inverse Proportion: For a fixed distance, higher speed means less time taken. (Agar speed badhegi to time kam lagega).

(v) Inverse Proportion: For a fixed total land area, if the population increases, the area of land per person decreases. (Agar log badhenge to har insaan ke hisse mein zameen kam aayegi).

The pairs in inverse proportion are (i), (iv), and (v).

Question 2

In a Television game show, the prize money of ₹1,00,000 is to be divided equally amongst the winners. Complete the following table and find whether the prize money given to an individual winner is directly or inversely proportional to the number of winners?

Number of winners 1 2 4 5 8 10 20
Prize for each (₹) 1,00,000 50,000 ... ... ... ... ...
Hinglish Explanation: Total prize ₹1,00,000 hai jo jeetne walon mein barabar banta jayega. Jitne zyada winners honge, har ek ko utna kam paisa milega. Iska matlab yeh inverse proportion hai. Hum table complete karenge, jahan (No. of winners) × (Prize for each) = 1,00,000 hamesha constant rahega.

Solution:

Let the number of winners be x and the prize for each winner be y. The total prize money is constant (k = 1,00,000). So, x * y = k.

As x increases, y must decrease, so this is a case of inverse proportion.

For x = 4: y = 100000 / 4 = 25,000

For x = 5: y = 100000 / 5 = 20,000

For x = 8: y = 100000 / 8 = 12,500

For x = 10: y = 100000 / 10 = 10,000

For x = 20: y = 100000 / 20 = 5,000

The prize money is inversely proportional to the number of winners. The prizes are: ₹25,000, ₹20,000, ₹12,500, ₹10,000, ₹5,000.

Question 3

Rehman is making a wheel using spokes. He wants to fix equal spokes in such a way that the angles between any pair of consecutive spokes are equal. Help him by completing the following table.

Number of spokes 4 6 8 10 12
Angle between spokes 90° 60° ... ... ...

(i) Are the number of spokes and the angles formed between the pairs of consecutive spokes in inverse proportion?
(ii) Calculate the angle between a pair of consecutive spokes on a wheel with 15 spokes.
(iii) How many spokes would be needed, if the angle between a pair of consecutive spokes is 40°?

Hinglish Explanation: Ek wheel (pahiya) mein spokes (tilliyan) lagani hain. Total angle ek circle ka 360° hota hai. Jitni zyada spokes hongi, unke beech ka angle utna chhota hoga. Yeh inverse proportion hai. (Number of spokes) × (Angle) = 360°. Isi formula se hum saare answers nikalenge.

Solution:

Let the number of spokes be x and the angle between them be y. The total angle in a circle is 360°. So, x * y = 360. This is a constant product, so it's inverse proportion.

Completing the table:

For x = 8: y = 360 / 8 = 45°

For x = 10: y = 360 / 10 = 36°

For x = 12: y = 360 / 12 = 30°

(i) Yes, they are in inverse proportion because as the number of spokes increases, the angle between them decreases, and their product remains constant (360°).

(ii) For 15 spokes:
Angle y = 360 / 15 = 24°.

(iii) If the angle is 40°:
Number of spokes x = 360 / 40 = 9.

Table values: 45°, 36°, 30°. (i) Yes. (ii) 24°. (iii) 9 spokes.

Question 4

If a box of sweets is divided among 24 children, they will get 5 sweets each. How many would each get, if the number of the children is reduced by 4?

Hinglish Explanation: Ek mithai ka dabba 24 bachchon mein baata to har ek ko 5 mithaiyan mili. Agar bachche 4 kam ho jayein (yaani 20 bachche), to har ek ko kitni mithaiyan milengi? Kam bachche honge to har ek ko zyada mithaiyan milengi. Yeh inverse proportion hai.

Solution:

Let the number of children be x and the number of sweets per child be y. The total number of sweets is constant. So, x * y = k. This is inverse proportion.

Given: x₁ = 24, y₁ = 5. Total sweets k = 24 * 5 = 120.

The number of children is reduced by 4, so the new number of children is x₂ = 24 - 4 = 20.

We need to find y₂. x₁ * y₁ = x₂ * y₂ 24 * 5 = 20 * y₂ 120 = 20 * y₂ y₂ = 120 / 20 = 6

Each child would get 6 sweets.

Question 9

A car takes 2 hours to reach a destination by travelling at the speed of 60 km/h. How long will it take when the car travels at the speed of 80 km/h?

Hinglish Explanation: Ek car 60 km/h ki speed se 2 ghante mein apni manzil par pahunchti hai. Agar car 80 km/h ki speed se chale, to kitna time lagega? Speed zyada hogi to time kam lagega. Yeh inverse proportion hai.

Solution:

Let the speed of the car be x (in km/h) and the time taken be y (in hours). The distance is constant. So, x * y = k (inverse proportion).

Given: x₁ = 60 km/h, y₁ = 2 hours.

New speed is x₂ = 80 km/h.

We need to find y₂. x₁ * y₁ = x₂ * y₂ 60 * 2 = 80 * y₂ 120 = 80 * y₂ y₂ = 120 / 80 = 12 / 8 = 3 / 2 = 1.5 hours.

1.5 hours is equal to 1 hour and 30 minutes.

It will take 1.5 hours (or 1 hour 30 minutes).

Question 10

Two persons could fit new windows in a house in 3 days.
(i) One of the persons fell ill before the work started. How long would the job take now?
(ii) How many persons would be needed to fit the windows in one day?

Solution:

Let the number of persons be x and the number of days be y. This is a case of inverse proportion. x * y = k.

New number of persons x₂ = 1. We need to find y₂. 1 * y₂ = 6 y₂ = 6 days.

(ii) To complete the work in one day:

New number of days y₃ = 1. We need to find x₃. x₃ * 1 = 6 x₃ = 6 persons.
