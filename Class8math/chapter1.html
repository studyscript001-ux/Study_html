Exercise 1.1

1. Name the property under multiplication used in each of the following.

(i) -4/5 × 1 = 1 × -4/5 = -4/5

Here, the rational number -4/5 is multiplied by 1, and the result is the number itself.

Property: 1 is the multiplicative identity.

(ii) -13/17 × -2/7 = -2/7 × -13/17

The order of the rational numbers being multiplied is changed, but the result remains the same. This is in the form a × b = b × a.

Property: Commutativity of multiplication.

(iii) -19/29 × 29/-19 = 1

One number is the reciprocal of the other, and their product is 1. This is in the form a × (1/a) = 1.

Property: Multiplicative inverse.

2. Tell what property allows you to compute 1/3 × (6 × 4/3) as (1/3 × 6) × 4/3.

The grouping of the three rational numbers being multiplied is changed.
This computation is in the form a × (b × c) = (a × b) × c.

Property: Associativity of multiplication.

3. The product of two rational numbers is always a ___.

As we learned in the chapter, rational numbers are closed under multiplication.
This means that when you multiply any two rational numbers, the result will also be a rational number.

Answer: Rational number.

Problems from Chapter Examples

Find: -2/3 × 3/5 + 5/2 - 3/5 × 1/6

First, identify the terms. We have three terms here: -2/3 × 3/5, 5/2, and -3/5 × 1/6.
Rearrange the terms using the Commutative Property to group similar fractions: -2/3 × 3/5 - 3/5 × 1/6 + 5/2
Now, use the Distributive Property (a × b + a × c = a × (b+c)). Take -3/5 as a common factor from the first two terms.
-3/5 × (2/3 + 1/6) + 5/2
Solve the bracket first. The LCM of 3 and 6 is 6. So, (4/6 + 1/6) = 5/6.
The expression becomes: -3/5 × 5/6 + 5/2
Now, multiply: -3/5 × 5/6 = -15/30, which simplifies to -1/2.
The final expression is: -1/2 + 5/2
Add the fractions: (-1 + 5) / 2 = 4/2.

Answer: 2

Explanation in Hinglish

Yahan sabhi questions ke solution ko aasan Hinglish bhasha me samjhaya gaya hai.

Question 1: Properties Pehchane

(i) -4/5 × 1 = -4/5: Jab hum kisi bhi number ko 1 se multiply karte hain, toh wahi number wapas milta hai. Isliye, 1 ko multiplicative identity kehte hain.

(ii) -13/17 × -2/7 = -2/7 × -13/17: Yahan par, humne numbers ki jagah (order) badal di hai, lekin unka product same rahega. Is property ko Commutativity kehte hain. Jaise 2×3 aur 3×2 dono 6 hote hain.

(iii) -19/29 × 29/-19 = 1: Ek number dusre ka ulta (reciprocal) hai. Jab hum kisi number ko uske reciprocal se multiply karte hain, toh answer hamesha 1 aata hai. Is property ko Multiplicative Inverse kehte hain.

Question 2: Kaunsi Property use hui?

Is question mein, teen numbers multiply ho rahe hain: 1/3, 6, aur 4/3. Humne sirf unki grouping change ki hai - pehle last ke do ko multiply kiya, fir pehle do ko. Answer dono tarah se same rehta hai. Is grouping waali property ko Associativity kehte hain.

Question 3: Khali jagah bharein

Jab bhi aap do rational numbers (jaise 1/2 aur 3/4) ko multiply karte hain, toh jo answer aata hai, woh bhi hamesha ek rational number hi hota hai. Ise 'closure property' bhi kehte hain.

Example Problem ko kaise solve karein?

Step 1: Sabse pehle, humne 3/5 waale terms ko ek saath likha (Rearrange kiya). Ise 'Commutative property' kehte hain. Aisa karne se calculation aasan ho jaati hai.

-2/3 × 3/5 - 3/5 × 1/6 + 5/2

Step 2: Ab, dono terms mein -3/5 common hai, toh humne use bahar nikal liya (common le liya). Ise 'Distributive property' kehte hain.

-3/5 × (2/3 + 1/6) + 5/2

Step 3: Fir humne bracket ke andar waale fractions (2/3 + 1/6) ko LCM lekar solve kiya, jiska answer 5/6 aaya.

Step 4: Ab equation ban gayi: -3/5 × 5/6 + 5/2. Pehle multiply solve kiya toh -1/2 aaya.

Step 5: Aakhir mein -1/2 + 5/2 ko solve kiya aur final answer 2 aa gaya.
