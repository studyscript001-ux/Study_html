Exercise 6.1

1. Which of the following numbers are not perfect cubes?

(i) 216: Prime factorization is 2 × 2 × 2 × 3 × 3 × 3 = 2³ × 3³. All factors are in triplets. So, it is a perfect cube.
(ii) 128: Prime factorization is 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 = 2³ × 2³ × 2. One '2' is left over. So, it is not a perfect cube.
(iii) 1000: Prime factorization is 2 × 2 × 2 × 5 × 5 × 5 = 2³ × 5³. All factors are in triplets. So, it is a perfect cube.
(iv) 100: Prime factorization is 2 × 2 × 5 × 5. No factors are in triplets. So, it is not a perfect cube.
(v) 46656: Prime factorization is 2³ × 2³ × 3³ × 3³. All factors are in triplets. So, it is a perfect cube.

Not perfect cubes: 128, 100.

2. Find the smallest number by which each of the following numbers must be multiplied to obtain a perfect cube.

(i) 243: 243 = 3 × 3 × 3 × 3 × 3. We need one more '3' to make a triplet. So, multiply by 3.
(ii) 256: 256 = 2³ × 2³ × 2 × 2. We need one more '2' to make a triplet. So, multiply by 2.
(iii) 72: 72 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 3 × 3. We need one more '3'. So, multiply by 3.
(iv) 675: 675 = 3 × 3 × 3 × 5 × 5. We need one more '5'. So, multiply by 5.
(v) 100: 100 = 2 × 2 × 5 × 5. We need one '2' and one '5'. So, multiply by 2 × 5 = 10.

3. Find the smallest number by which each of the following numbers must be divided to obtain a perfect cube.

(i) 81: 81 = 3 × 3 × 3 × 3. The extra factor is '3'. So, divide by 3.
(ii) 128: 128 = 2³ × 2³ × 2. The extra factor is '2'. So, divide by 2.
(iii) 135: 135 = 3 × 3 × 3 × 5. The extra factor is '5'. So, divide by 5.
(iv) 192: 192 = 2³ × 2³ × 3. The extra factor is '3'. So, divide by 3.
(v) 704: 704 = 2³ × 2³ × 11. The extra factor is '11'. So, divide by 11.

4. Parikshit makes a cuboid of plasticine of sides 5 cm, 2 cm, 5 cm. How many such cuboids will he need to form a cube?

Volume of the cuboid = 5 × 2 × 5.
To make a cube, all factors in the volume's prime factorization must be in triplets.
The factors are 5 × 5 × 2. We are missing one '5' and two '2's to make complete triplets.
So, we need to multiply by 5 × 2 × 2 = 20.

He will need 20 such cuboids.

Exercise 6.2

1. Find the cube root of each of the following numbers by prime factorisation method.

(i) 64: 64 = 2³ × 2³. Cube root = 2 × 2 = 4.
(ii) 512: 512 = 2³ × 2³ × 2³. Cube root = 2 × 2 × 2 = 8.
(iii) 10648: 10648 = 2³ × 11³. Cube root = 2 × 11 = 22.
(iv) 27000: 27000 = 2³ × 3³ × 5³. Cube root = 2 × 3 × 5 = 30.
(v) 15625: 15625 = 5³ × 5³. Cube root = 5 × 5 = 25.

2. State true or false.

(i) Cube of any odd number is even. False (e.g., 3³ = 27, which is odd).
(ii) A perfect cube does not end with two zeros. True (It ends with three, six, nine... zeros).
(iii) If square of a number ends with 5, then its cube ends with 25. False (e.g., 5²=25, 5³=125. 15²=225, 15³=3375).
(iv) There is no perfect cube which ends with 8. False (e.g., 2³ = 8).
(v) The cube of a two digit number may be a three digit number. False (The smallest two-digit number is 10, and 10³ = 1000, which has four digits).
(vi) The cube of a two digit number may have seven or more digits. False (The largest two-digit number is 99, and 99³ = 970299, which has six digits).
(vii) The cube of a single digit number may be a single digit number. True (e.g., 1³ = 1, 2³ = 8).

Exercise 6.1 ka Explanation

Q1: Perfect Cube hai ya nahi?
Perfect cube check karne ke liye, number ke prime factors nikalte hain. Agar sabhi factors 3-3 ke group (triplets) me ban jaate hain, toh woh perfect cube hai. Agar koi factor bach jaata hai, toh nahi hai.

Q2: Kis se Multiply karein?
Pehle prime factors nikalo. Fir dekho ki 3-3 ke group banane ke liye kaunse factor kam pad rahe hain. Jitne factors kam hain, unko multiply karke wahi number answer hoga.

Q3: Kis se Divide karein?
Isme bhi prime factors nikalo. 3-3 ke group banane ke baad jo bhi factor extra bach jaate hain, unhi ko multiply karke jo number aayega, usse divide karna hai.

Q4: Kitne Cuboids chahiye?
Cuboid ka volume nikalo (5 × 2 × 5). Iske factors hain 5, 5, 2. Ek perfect cube banane ke liye sabhi factors 3-3 ke group me hone chahiye. Yahan ek '5' aur do '2' kam hain. Toh hume 5 × 2 × 2 = 20 cuboids chahiye.

Exercise 6.2 ka Explanation

Q1: Cube Root kaise nikalein?
Number ke prime factors nikalo. Fir 3-3 ke same factors ke group (triplets) banao. Har group me se ek factor bahar le lo aur un sabko aapas me multiply kar do. Wahi cube root hai. Jaise 64 = (2×2×2) × (2×2×2). Ek group se ek '2' aur dusre se ek '2' liya. 2 × 2 = 4 answer hai.

Q2: Sahi ya Galat?
Yeh questions cubes ki properties par based hain. Jaise, odd number ka cube hamesha odd hota hai, aur even ka cube even hota hai. Smallest 2-digit number (10) ka cube (1000) 4-digit ka hota hai, isliye 3-digit ka nahi ho sakta.
