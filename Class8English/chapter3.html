The Company's "superior weapons" refers to the advanced military technology that the British East India Company possessed, such as modern firearms, cannons, and warships, which were more advanced than the traditional weapons used by Indian rulers.

An artisan is a skilled craft worker who makes things by hand. The artisans suffered because the British policies favored imported goods from England and imposed heavy taxes on local products, which destroyed Indian handicrafts and industries.

Working with the Text

1. Do you think the Indian princes were short-sighted in their approach to the events of 1757?

Yes, the Indian princes were short-sighted. They were busy fighting among themselves and failed to see the larger threat posed by the British. They often sought British help against their rivals, not realizing that this would eventually lead to their own downfall.

2. How did the East India Company subdue the Indian princes?

The East India Company subdued the Indian princes by taking advantage of their rivalries, using superior weapons, and following the policy of "divide and rule." They supported one prince against another and gradually extended their control over all of India.

3. Quote the words used by Ram Mohan Roy to say that every religion teaches the same principles.

Ram Mohan Roy said: "Cows are of different colours, but the colour of their milk is the same. Different teachers have different opinions but the essence of every religion is the same."

4. In what ways did the British officers exploit Indians?

British officers exploited Indians by:

  • Drawing big salaries while making fortunes in private business
  • Imposing heavy taxes on farmers
  • Passing laws that crippled Indian industries
  • Jailing Indians without trial under Regulation III of 1818
  • Exporting British goods to India without import duty, harming local industries

Story: The Clever Fox and the Foolish Goat

One day, a fox accidentally fell into a deep well. She tried to jump out but couldn't. She was wondering how to get out when a goat happened to pass by.

The goat peeked into the well and asked, "Hello! Is this water sweet?" The clever fox saw an opportunity and replied, "Oh yes! It's too sweet! I've had so much, I might faint."

Hearing this, the thirsty goat immediately jumped into the well. As the goat drank water, the fox quickly climbed onto the goat's back and jumped out of the well.

Before leaving, the fox said, "Thanks for the help. Come out when you can." The goat realized her mistake and remembered her mother's advice: "Be careful how you take the advice of people you don't know."

The moral of the story is: Never trust strangers blindly and think before you act.

3. Paragraph on the new method of teaching history

I think the new method of teaching history through comic books is an excellent idea. History can sometimes seem boring to students when taught through traditional textbooks, but comics make it engaging and fun. When students read comic strips, enact historical characters, and have discussions, they remember the events better. This method encourages participation from all students and helps them understand what historical characters might have been thinking. Using visual aids like comics appeals to different learning styles and makes complex historical events easier to understand. This innovative approach should be adopted in more schools to make learning history enjoyable.
