Chapter 2: The Tsunami

Comprehension Check (Part I)

Say whether the following are true or false.

1. True - Ignesious lost his wife, two children, his father-in-law, and his brother-in-law.

2. False - Sanjeev was swept away while trying to save the cook's wife.

3. False - Meghna was not saved by a relief helicopter; she floated for two days and was brought to shore by a wave.

4. True - Almas's father realized the danger when he saw the sea water recede.

5. True - Her mother and aunts were washed away with the coconut tree they were holding onto.

Comprehension Check (Part II)

Answer the following in a phrase or sentence.

1. Tilly's family came to Thailand to celebrate Christmas.

2. They saw the water swelling and coming in, the beach getting smaller, and the sea foaming, bubbling and forming whirlpools.

3. Yes, Tilly's mother was alarmed when she saw her daughter becoming hysterical.

4. Tilly had seen the sea behaving this way in a geography lesson video about tsunamis.

5. They went to the third floor of the hotel to escape from the tsunami.

6. Her geography teacher would have felt proud and happy that his lesson had saved lives.

Comprehension Check (Part III)

Answer using a phrase or a sentence.

1. Very few animals died in the tsunami.

2. Sixty visitors died in Yala National Park, but no animals died except two water buffaloes.

3. People say that the elephants of Yala National Park ran away from the beach about an hour before the tsunami hit.

4. The dogs in Galle refused to go for their daily run on the beach, which probably saved their owner's life.

Working with the Text

1. When he felt the earthquake, do you think Ignesious immediately worried about a tsunami? Give reasons for your answer.

No, Ignesious did not immediately worry about a tsunami. He was more concerned about his television set and took time to carefully place it on the ground. The sentence that shows they didn't have time to plan is: "Then the family rushed out of the house."

2. Which words in the list below describe Sanjeev?

(i) I don't know if Sanjeev was cheerful, ambitious or humorous.

(ii) I think that he was very brave, heroic and selfless.

(iii) Sanjeev was not heartless, brash or careless.

3. How are Meghna and Almas's stories similar?

Both Meghna and Almas were young girls who were swept away by tsunami waves. Both lost their families and struggled to survive in the water by holding onto floating objects (a wooden door and a log of wood). Both were eventually rescued but were traumatized by their experiences.

4. What are the different ways in which Tilly's parents could have reacted to her behavior?

Tilly's parents could have ignored her warnings or scolded her for being hysterical. Instead, they trusted her and acted quickly. If I were in their place, I would have also listened to my child, especially when she was so upset and was referring to something she had learned in school.

5. If Tilly's award was to be shared, who should she share it with?

Tilly should share the award with her geography teacher who taught her about tsunamis and her parents who trusted and listened to her.

6. What are the two different ideas about why so few animals were killed in the tsunami?

The two ideas are: (1) Animals have a sixth sense that helps them detect approaching disasters. (2) Animals have more acute hearing that helps them hear or feel the earth's vibration. I find the second idea more believable as it is based on animals' heightened senses rather than supernatural abilities.

Speaking and Writing

1. Diary Entry as a Relief Volunteer

31 December, 2004
The killer tsunami struck these islands five days ago. But the victims are being brought in even now. Each one has a story to tell. Today I met a young girl named Meghna who floated in the sea for two days holding onto a wooden door. She lost both her parents but somehow survived. Her courage is incredible.

I also heard about Sanjeev, a policeman who saved his family but died trying to save the cook's wife. His bravery reminds me that even in the darkest times, there are heroes among us.

The people here have lost everything, yet they show remarkable resilience. They are helping each other and working together to rebuild their lives. It's heartbreaking to see their suffering, but inspiring to witness their strength.

2. Classroom Learning Helping Outside

Last summer, my family went on a trek in the mountains. During our hike, my younger sister accidentally touched a poisonous plant and her hand started swelling. I remembered from my science class that applying mud mixed with water could help draw out the poison and reduce swelling. I quickly made a mud paste and applied it to her hand. Within minutes, the swelling started to reduce and the pain lessened. My parents were amazed and grateful that I remembered this lesson. It felt good to use classroom knowledge in a real-life situation to help someone.

Poem: Geography Lesson

When the jet sprang into the sky,
it was clear why the city
had developed the way it had,
seeing it scaled six inches to the mile.
There seemed an inevitability
about what on ground had looked haphazard,
unplanned and without style
When the jet sprang into the sky.

When the jet reached ten thousand feet,
it was clear why the country
had cities where the rivers ran
and why the valleys were populated.
The logic of geography —
that land and water attracted man —
was clearly delineated
When the jet reached ten thousand feet.

When the jet rose six miles high,
it was clear the earth was round
and that it had more sea than land.
But it was difficult to understand
that the men on the earth found
causes to hate each other, to build
walls across cities and to kill.
From that height, it was not clear why.

Working with the Poem

1. Find three or four phrases in stanzas one and two which are likely to occur in a geography lesson.

Phrases likely to occur in a geography lesson: "developed the way it had", "scaled six inches to the mile", "cities where the rivers ran", "valleys were populated", "logic of geography", "land and water attracted man".

2. Seen from the window of an aeroplane, the city appears...

(ii) as neat as a map.

3. Which statements are examples of "the logic of geography"?

(i) There are cities where there are rivers.
(iii) It is easy to understand why valleys are populated.
(v) The earth is round, and it has more sea than land.

4. Mention two things that are clear from the height and two that are not clear.

Clear from the height:
1. Why cities developed in certain patterns
2. Why cities are located near rivers and valleys are populated

Not clear from the height:
1. Why people hate each other
2. Why people build walls and kill each other
