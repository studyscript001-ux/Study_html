Chapter 4: Bepin Choudhury's Lapse of Memory

Comprehension Check (Part I)

1. Why did the man stare at Bepin Babu in disbelief?

The man stared at Bepin Babu in disbelief because Bepin Babu claimed he had never been to Ranchi, while the man (Parimal Ghose) had clear memories of arranging Bepin Babu's trip to Ranchi in 1958 and spending time with him there.

2. Where did Bepin Babu say he went in October '58?

Bepin Babu said he spent the Puja holidays in October '58 with a friend in Kanpur.

3. Mention any three (or more) things that Parimal Ghose knew about Bepin Babu.

Parimal Ghose knew:

  • Bepin Babu had a fall in Hudroo and cut his right knee
  • Bepin Babu didn't have any children
  • Bepin Babu's wife had died ten years ago
  • Bepin Babu's only brother had died insane
  • Bepin Babu always carried a bag with books on sight-seeing trips

Comprehension Check (Part II)

1. Why did Bepin Babu worry about what Parimal Ghose had said?

Bepin Babu worried because Parimal Ghose knew many intimate details about his personal life, which made him doubt his own memory about the Ranchi trip.

2. How did he try to decide who was rightâ€”his memory or Parimal Ghose?

He tried to check his right knee for an old injury mark that Parimal had mentioned. He also considered calling Dinesh Mukerji, who was supposedly in Ranchi at the same time.

3. Why did Bepin Babu hesitate to visit Mr Mukerji? Why did he finally decide to phone him?

Bepin Babu hesitated because he thought it would be ridiculous to ask Dinesh about the Ranchi trip if he had never been there. He finally decided to phone him to settle the question without showing his embarrassment in person.

4. What did Mr Mukerji say? Did it comfort Bepin Babu, or add to his worries?

Mr Mukerji confirmed that he had been to Ranchi in 1958 and that Bepin Babu was there too. This added to Bepin Babu's worries and made him doubt his own memory even more.

Comprehension Check (Part III)

1. Who was Chunilal? What did he want from Bepin Babu?

Chunilal was an old school friend of Bepin Babu who was going through financial difficulties. He wanted Bepin Babu to help him find a job.

2. Why was Dr Chanda puzzled? What was unusual about Bepin Babu's loss of memory?

Dr Chanda was puzzled because Bepin Babu remembered everything except one particular incident (the Ranchi trip). This was unusual as memory loss typically affects multiple memories, not just one specific event.

Comprehension Check (Part IV)

1. Had Bepin Babu really lost his memory and forgotten all about a trip to Ranchi?

No, Bepin Babu had not really lost his memory. He had never been to Ranchi. The entire episode was an elaborate trick played by Chunilal to teach Bepin Babu a lesson for not helping an old friend in need.

2. Why do you think Chunilal did what he did? Chunilal says he has no money; what is it that he does have?

Chunilal did this to teach Bepin Babu a lesson for being unfeeling and not helping an old friend. Though he had no money, he had imagination which he used to create this elaborate trick.

Working with the Text

1. The author describes Bepin Babu as a serious and hardworking man. What evidence can you find in the story to support this?

Evidence includes:

  • He worked for a big firm and had a responsible job
  • He had a reputation for being a tireless, conscientious worker
  • He had never missed work in twenty-five years
  • He spoke for half an hour at an important meeting

2. Why did Bepin Babu change his mind about meeting Chunilal? What was the result of this meeting?

Bepin Babu changed his mind because he thought Chunilal might remember something about the 1958 Ranchi trip. The result was that Chunilal also confirmed that Bepin Babu had gone to Ranchi, further deepening his confusion.

3. Bepin Babu lost consciousness at Hudroo Falls. What do you think was the reason for this?

Bepin Babu lost consciousness due to extreme mental stress and anxiety about his supposed memory loss. The realization that he didn't recognize any places in Ranchi despite everyone claiming he had been there caused him to collapse.

4. How do you think Bepin Babu reacted when he found out that Chunilal had tricked him?

Initially, Bepin Babu might have felt angry and embarrassed. However, he probably realized the lesson Chunilal was trying to teach him about helping friends in need. He might have felt ashamed of his earlier behavior.

Speaking and Writing

1. What do you think happened after Bepin Babu came to know the truth?

After learning the truth, Bepin Babu probably felt a mix of emotions - anger at being tricked, but also shame for not helping his old friend. He might have realized the lesson Chunilal was trying to teach him about friendship and helping others. I think he decided to help Chunilal, understanding that his friend's actions came from a place of desperation.

2. Imagine you are Bepin Choudhury. Write a letter to Chunilal.

Letter to Chunilal:

Dear Chunilal,

I received your letter and must admit I was initially angry about the trick you played on me. But after thinking about it, I realize I deserved it for not helping you when you needed me most.

I'm truly sorry for my behavior. An old friendship like ours is precious, and I should have been more supportive during your difficult time.

Please visit me this weekend so we can discuss how I can help you. I'll see what I can do about finding you a suitable job or helping in other ways.

Your friend,
Bepin Choudhury

Poem: The Last Bargain

"Come and hire me," I cried, while in the morning
I was walking on the stone-paved road.
Sword in hand the King came in his chariot.
He held my hand and said, "I will hire you with my power,"
But his power counted for naught, and he went away in his chariot.

In the heat of the mid-day the houses stood with shut doors.
I wandered along the crooked lane.
An old man came out with his bag of gold.
He pondered and said, "I will hire you with my money."
He weighed his coins one by one, but I turned away.

It was evening. The garden hedge was all afflower.
The fair maid came out and said, "I will hire you with a smile."
Her smile paled and melted into tears, and she went back alone into the dark.

The sun glistened on the sand, and the sea waves broke waywardly.
A child sat playing with shells.
He raised his head and seemed to know me and said, "I hire you with nothing."
From henceforward that bargain struck in child's play made me a free man.

Working with the Poem

1. Who is the speaker in the poem?

The speaker in the poem is a man who is looking for work and wants to be hired.

2. "The king, sword in hand" suggests...

(ii) power

3. The old man offered the speaker a lot of money. Why did he turn down the offer?

He turned down the offer because he realized that money cannot buy happiness or true fulfillment. He was looking for something more meaningful than material wealth.

4. Find in the poem, lines that match the following.

(i) "I will hire you with nothing."

(ii) "Her smile paled and melted into tears"

(iii) "But his power counted for naught"

5. How did the speaker feel after talking to the child on the beach?

After talking to the child, the speaker felt truly free and happy. The bargain made with the child, which required nothing in return, gave him a sense of true freedom and fulfillment that power, money, or false happiness could not provide.
